# Radiant-Level Tips - Coaching Knowledge Base

This document contains the specific techniques, mindset patterns, and micro-skills that separate Radiant players from everyone else. These are not generic tips -- they are the precise habits and knowledge that high-elo players use to win gunfights, rounds, and games consistently.

---

## Radiant-Specific Positioning Secrets

### Off-Angle Theory
- The "default" angle is the angle everyone expects: the common spots where players hold on every map. At Radiant level, default angles get pre-aimed. You die holding them.
- Off-angles are 1-3 meters away from the default. They force the enemy to micro-adjust their crosshair after clearing the default spot. That 0.1-0.2 second adjustment is the difference between dying and getting the kill.
- **Rule**: Hold the off-angle for ONE fight, then reposition. If you get a kill from an off-angle, the enemy team will pre-aim it next round.
- **Examples**: Instead of holding Ascent A Main from the default Tree position, hold from the left side of Dice. Instead of holding B Main from default Logs, hold from the corner near Market entrance.

### Depth Positioning
- Most players think in 2D (left/right). Radiant players think in 3D (left/right/depth).
- **Close depth**: Holding close to the corner means the enemy sees you the instant they peek. You have angle advantage but must react instantly.
- **Far depth**: Holding far from the corner means the enemy's crosshair is on the corner, not on you. You appear in their peripheral vision, forcing an adjustment.
- **When to hold close**: When you're in a 1v1 or holding a tight corridor. Close depth maximizes angle advantage.
- **When to hold far**: When multiple enemies might peek. Far depth gives you time to see and react to multiple targets.

### The "Unpeeked" Position
- After your team clears a site, there are always positions that the clearing team did NOT check. These "unpeeked" positions are goldmines for lurkers and retakers.
- Common unpeeked positions: deep corners behind the spike plant zone, under elevated positions that teammates cleared from above, behind doors that were opened but not checked behind.
- Knowing what your team cleared and what they didn't is a Radiant-level skill that comes from experience and communication.

### Post-Plant Positioning Triangle
- After planting, the team should form a triangle around the spike: one player close watching the spike, one player at medium range covering an approach, one player far watching for rotators.
- The triangle ensures that any retaker must deal with multiple angles simultaneously.
- The close player calls defuse sounds. The medium player takes the first fight. The far player denies rotations.

## Movement Techniques

### Counter-Strafe
- Counter-strafing is pressing the opposite movement key to instantly stop your momentum before firing. Without counter-strafing, your first shot is inaccurate while moving.
- **A-D counter-strafe**: Moving right (D), tap left (A) to stop, fire. Moving left (A), tap right (D) to stop, fire.
- **Timing**: The stop happens approximately 1-2 frames after pressing the opposite key. Your shot must come AFTER the stop, not during the key press.
- **Practice routine**: Go into the Range, strafe left/right, counter-strafe, one-tap the bot. Repeat 500 times. Your muscle memory should make this automatic.
- **Advanced**: Double counter-strafe. A-D-A (strafe right, stop, strafe left) to jiggle an angle and fire during the brief stop in the middle. This is the bread-and-butter movement of Radiant players.

### Jiggle Peek
- A jiggle peek is a rapid A-D movement that briefly exposes you to an angle and then pulls you back behind cover.
- **Purpose**: Gather info (did someone shoot? did I see a player?), bait a shot (force the enemy to fire, revealing their position), trigger utility (set off a tripwire to reveal it).
- **Timing**: The jiggle should expose you for ~50-100ms. Any longer and you're wide enough to get shot. Any shorter and you don't see anything.
- **How to practice**: Stand at a corner in a custom game. A-D rapidly so that only your shoulder and part of your model peeks past the corner. Do this until you can control exactly how much you expose.

### Ferrari Peek
- A ferrari peek is a full-commitment wide swing where you strafe out from cover at maximum speed, pre-aiming the expected enemy position.
- **When to use**: When you KNOW the enemy position (from info utility, sound, or teammate calls) and want to take a favorable aim duel with momentum advantage.
- **Why it works**: The wide swing puts your moving model far from where the enemy is aiming (at the corner edge). By the time they adjust to your actual position, you've counter-strafed and fired.
- **When NOT to use**: Against an Op (you die before counter-strafing), against unknown positions (you might swing into a crossfire), when holding an angle (you're already in position).
- **Pro tip**: The ferrari peek is most effective with a Phantom because the spray is more forgiving if your first shot misses during the counter-strafe.

### Wide Swing
- Similar to ferrari peek but with a broader angle. You swing so wide that you clear the entire angle in one motion.
- **When to use**: When clearing a deep angle where the enemy could be anywhere along a wall or corridor.
- **Pre-aim**: During the wide swing, your crosshair should trace along the wall/corner at head height. As you swing, you're sweeping the entire angle.
- **Counter-strafe timing**: Stop the wide swing when you see the enemy. The counter-strafe must be instant.

### Crouch Timing
- **Never crouch peek.** At Radiant level, crouching into an angle puts your head at body-shot height for an enemy aiming at head level. You die faster.
- **When to crouch**: DURING a spray, not before it. Start standing, fire 3-4 bullets, crouch to pull the spray down. This also drops your hitbox below the enemy's crosshair if they haven't adjusted.
- **Crouch timing in fights**: Crouch after 0.2-0.3 seconds of firing. This makes your hitbox change AFTER the enemy has committed their aim to your standing head position.
- **Never crouch vs. an Op**: A crouching target is slower and easier to hit. Stand and counter-strafe.

## Crosshair Placement Advanced Tips

### The Head Height Line
- Crosshair should always be at standing head height relative to the angle you're watching. This is non-negotiable.
- **Map geometry knowledge**: Every map has consistent head height markers. On Ascent, the top of A Main boxes is head height. On Bind, the middle of the Hookah window frame is head height. Learn these markers for every map.
- **Elevation changes**: When moving up or down stairs/ramps, your crosshair must adjust for the angle. Going up ramps requires slightly lowering crosshair. Going down requires raising it.

### Pre-Aim Points
- Every angle has a "pre-aim point" -- the exact pixel where a head will appear when an enemy peeks. At Radiant level, your crosshair should be on that pixel before the enemy appears.
- **Common pre-aim points**: Corner edges at head height, gap openings, doorway entries, window frames.
- **Practice**: Walk through every map in a custom game, stopping at each angle and placing your crosshair on the pre-aim point. Do this until it's automatic.

### Crosshair Placement During Rotation
- When rotating between sites, your crosshair should sweep through every angle you pass at head height.
- Do NOT look at the ground while running. Do NOT look at the sky. Your crosshair should be at head height even when running through safe areas because the one time you don't, a lurker kills you.

### Multi-Angle Crosshair Placement
- When multiple angles can produce a threat simultaneously, place your crosshair between the two angles, splitting the difference.
- **Example**: On Ascent A site retake, enemies can be in Heaven or Tree. Place your crosshair between the two spots so the adjustment to either is minimal.
- **Priority**: Pre-aim the MORE likely angle based on info, but keep the adjustment distance to the secondary angle under 10cm of mouse movement.

## Sound Cue Reading

### Critical Sound Cues
- **Footsteps**: Running is audible within ~40m. Walking is nearly silent but still audible within ~15m on certain surfaces (metal grates, wooden floors).
- **Ability deployment**: Every ability has a distinct audio signature. Learn them all. Examples:
  - Jett Updraft: whooshing wind sound
  - Omen Shrouded Step: ethereal teleport sound at departure AND arrival
  - Cypher Spycam: distinct electronic placement sound
  - Killjoy Turret: mechanical deployment sound
  - Chamber Rendezvous: teleport anchor placement sound
- **Weapon switching**: Switching weapons makes a sound. Pulling out the spike makes a specific sound. Listen for the spike pullout to know when enemies are planting.
- **Reload**: Reloading is audible. If you hear a reload, you know the enemy's gun is empty for ~2 seconds. Push them.
- **Spike sounds**: The spike beeping increases in frequency as it approaches detonation. At half-defuse, the beep pattern changes. The defuse itself has a distinct hum.

### Sound Priority System
1. **Spike sounds** (planting, defusing, beeping) -- highest priority, game-deciding info.
2. **Footsteps** -- direct enemy position info.
3. **Abilities** -- indirect position info and ability tracking.
4. **Weapon sounds** -- reload, switch, scope.
5. **Environment** -- rope climbing, teleporter, doors.

### Sound Denial
- Walk (shift) when you don't want to give info. This seems obvious but many Radiant players still make noise at critical moments.
- **When to run**: When the enemy already knows your position, when time pressure forces speed, when you're far enough away that footsteps don't reach.
- **When to walk**: When approaching an angle, when lurking, when flanking, when the round timer allows patience.
- **Fake footsteps**: Run toward a position, then immediately shift-walk in a different direction. The enemy hears running toward the first position and pre-aims there.

## Minimap Awareness

### The 2-Second Rule
- Every 2 seconds, glance at the minimap. This should be a subconscious habit.
- The minimap shows: teammate positions, teammate death locations, spike carrier position, enemy pings from utility.
- A 2-second minimap check tells you: which angles are covered, where enemies were last seen, if teammates are alive, and where the spike is.

### Minimap Reads
- **Teammate positions**: If all 4 teammates are on one side of the map, the other side is unprotected. You need to cover it or call for rotation.
- **Death locations**: Where a teammate died tells you where the enemy is. If your teammate died at A Main, enemies are pushing A.
- **Spike position**: If the spike hasn't moved in 20 seconds, the enemy is either defaulting or faking. If the spike moves toward a site, that's the hit.
- **Enemy utility pings**: When Sova dart or Fade haunt reveals enemies, the minimap shows their positions for a brief moment. Note those positions even if you're across the map.

## Round Timing Exploitation

### The Round Clock
- Rounds are 1:40 (100 seconds). Spike plant takes 4 seconds. Spike detonation takes 45 seconds. Full defuse takes 7 seconds. Half defuse takes 3.5 seconds.
- **Attack timing windows**:
  - 1:40-1:20: Early aggression/fast execute window. Catching defenders still setting up.
  - 1:20-0:50: Default/info gathering. Standard execute window.
  - 0:50-0:30: Late execute. Defenders may have already used utility.
  - 0:30-0:00: Desperation push. Only if needed. Time pressure favors defenders.

### Time Pressure Psychology
- **As attacker**: You control the clock. Slow play forces defenders to stay disciplined. Fast play forces snap reactions. Vary your timing to be unpredictable.
- **As defender**: Play patient. Every second the attacker wastes is a second closer to their deadline. Don't peek aggressively unless you have specific info -- make them come to you.
- **Post-plant**: The clock flips. Defenders have ~40 seconds to retake and defuse (7 seconds for full defuse). Every second you delay the retake reduces their options.

## Spawn-Based Strategies

### Spawn RNG
- At the start of each round, players spawn in slightly different positions within the spawn zone. This affects which player reaches a position first.
- **Fast spawns**: Spawning toward the front of the spawn zone gets you to key positions 0.5-1 second faster. This matters for early aggression.
- **Spawn-based role assignment**: In some pro teams, the player with the fastest spawn takes the aggressive peek. If you spawn front, you peek. If you spawn back, you support.

### Early Round Timing Exploits
- **Ascent Mid**: A fast spawn on defense reaches Mid Doors 0.5 seconds before the attacker reaches the peek angle. This timing advantage means the defender gets the first shot.
- **Bind B Short**: A fast attacker spawn reaches the Short corner before the defender reaches the angle. The attacker can take an early fight.
- **Haven C Long**: Timing is approximately equal. The first fight on C Long is a pure coinflip, which is why teams utility it rather than dry peeking.
- Learn the timing matchups for every map. Know which spawn positions give you timing advantage and which don't.

## Info Denial Techniques

### How to Deny Info
- **Counter-drone**: Shoot drones/hawks/Trailblazers immediately. Every second a drone lives is a second of free info for the enemy.
- **Counter-scan**: Shoot Sova darts (1 HP), Fade haunts (1 HP) immediately. Standing out of their line of sight is better but shooting them is the backup plan.
- **Smoke positioning**: Don't stand directly behind a smoke. Stand to the side of it so that if an enemy pushes through, they don't immediately see you.
- **Sound denial**: Walk when you suspect enemy utility is listening (Cypher camera, Killjoy turret cone, Sonic Sensor).
- **Fake info**: Give false info. Run one direction, walk back the other way. Shoot a Sova dart from one position, then immediately rotate to another.

### Information Economy
- Every piece of info the enemy gets makes their decision easier. Every piece of info you deny makes their decision harder.
- The team that gathers more info and denies more info wins more rounds. This is the fundamental axiom of high-level Valorant.

## Bait and Switch Plays

### The Basic Bait and Switch
1. Make noise at position A (run, shoot, use utility).
2. Quietly rotate to position B.
3. When the enemy rotates to counter position A, strike from position B.
- This works because sound info is temporal -- it tells the enemy where you WERE, not where you ARE.

### Advanced Variations
- **Utility bait**: Throw utility at A site (smokes, flashes) as if executing. When defenders rotate to A, fast push B with remaining team.
- **Player bait**: One player makes noise at A while four quietly group at B. The one player's noise simulates a larger presence.
- **Rotate bait**: On defense, loudly rotate from B to A (running footsteps through mid). Actually, one player stays B in ambush. When attackers push "empty" B, the hidden player gets a pick and the rotating team collapses.

### Timing Windows for Bait and Switch
- The bait must be convincing for at least 3-5 seconds. Shorter and the enemy won't commit to the rotation.
- The switch must happen within 10 seconds of the bait. Longer and the enemy has time to re-read the situation.
- After the bait, listen for enemy rotation footsteps to confirm the bait worked before committing the switch.

## 1vX Clutch Methodology

### When to Save vs. When to Clutch

**Save if:**
- You have a rifle/Op worth saving and the round is clearly lost (1v4+ with no spike planted)
- Your team needs your gun for the next round's economy
- You have no utility left and no info on enemy positions
- Time is under 15 seconds with no spike planted and 3+ enemies alive

**Clutch if:**
- Spike is planted and you have post-plant utility
- You have info on enemy positions (from recent kills, utility, sound)
- Your team's economy doesn't need the save (you can buy regardless)
- You have a realistic path to victory (isolated fights, time advantage)

### 1v1 Clutch Protocol
1. **Determine who has time advantage.** If spike is planted, attacker has time. If not, defender has time.
2. **Gather info without revealing yourself.** Listen for sound cues, check minimap for utility pings.
3. **Force the fight on YOUR terms.** Pre-aim the angle, use utility to displace the enemy, or take an off-angle.
4. **Never repeak the same angle twice.** If you missed a shot, reposition. Repeaking gives the enemy a pre-aimed angle.

### 1v2 Clutch Protocol
1. **Isolate.** You cannot fight two people simultaneously. Use positioning and map geometry to ensure you only face one at a time.
2. **Identify the weaker link.** One enemy is likely in a worse position. Target them first.
3. **Use utility to delay the second player.** Smoke, molly, flash -- anything to buy time for the 1v1 with the first player.
4. **After the first kill, immediately reposition.** The second player heard the fight and knows your position. Move before they pre-aim.

### 1v3+ Clutch Protocol
1. **Play for time.** Every second you waste is a second the enemies must coordinate. Uncoordinated pushes are easier to handle than coordinated ones.
2. **Create paranoia.** Make noise from one position, then relocate. Fire a shot and move. The enemies will second-guess every push.
3. **Target isolated players.** If one enemy overextends, punish them. Then reposition for the next.
4. **If the spike is planted, play post-plant.** Deny defuse with utility. Force them to push you.

## Mental Game at High Elo

### Tilt Management
- Tilt is the number one cause of losing streaks. A tilted player makes worse decisions, peeks wider, holds shorter, and communicates less.
- **Prevention**: After a bad round, take 3 deep breaths during the buy phase. Physically reset (stretch hands, adjust posture).
- **Recognition**: If you notice yourself repeating the same failed play, you are tilted. Switch to a simpler approach.
- **Recovery**: Mute distracting teammates. Focus on one fundamental (crosshair placement) rather than trying to outplay opponents.

### Confidence Calibration
- **Overconfidence**: Taking fights you shouldn't, ego peeking after a multi-kill round, ignoring team utility in favor of solo plays. Overconfidence peaks after a big round and leads to feeding.
- **Underconfidence**: Hesitating on peeks, not taking fights you should, playing passive when the situation demands aggression. Underconfidence appears after multiple deaths and leads to ineffective play.
- **Calibration**: Before each round, ask: "What does the team need me to do?" not "What do I feel like doing?" Role-based play removes emotional decision-making.

### The Autopilot Trap
- Autopilot is when you stop making conscious decisions and default to habits. At Radiant, autopilot is death because enemies adapt.
- **Signs of autopilot**: Running the same play every round, not adjusting to enemy patterns, not communicating, defaulting to the same positions.
- **Counter**: Force yourself to make one conscious decision per round that is different from the previous round. Change your position, timing, or approach.

## Warmup Routines

### Pre-Ranked Warmup (20 minutes)
1. **Aim Lab / Kovaak's** (5 minutes): Gridshot or similar for mouse control warmup. Not for improvement -- just for waking up your hand.
2. **Range: Hard Bots** (5 minutes): Vandal one-taps on hard difficulty. Focus on crosshair placement and counter-strafe timing.
3. **Deathmatch** (10 minutes): Play one full deathmatch with Vandal. Focus on crosshair placement, not winning. Do not chase kills. Clear angles methodically, as if it were a real game.

### Extended Warmup (30 minutes, for tournament/important sessions)
1. **Kovaak's tracking + flicking** (10 minutes): Switching between tracking (smooth aim) and flick (snap aim) scenarios.
2. **Range: Eliminate 50** (5 minutes): Speed challenge. Try to beat your personal record.
3. **Deathmatch x2** (10 minutes): First DM with Vandal (one-taps). Second DM with Phantom (spray).
4. **Custom game** (5 minutes): Walk through the map you expect to play, reviewing angles, lineups, and setups.

## VOD Review Methodology

### Self-VOD Review Protocol
1. **Watch the entire map first** without pausing. Note which rounds felt off but don't analyze yet.
2. **Second watch**: Pause after every death. Ask: "Why did I die?" Categorize into:
   - **Positioning**: Was I in the wrong spot?
   - **Timing**: Did I peek at the wrong time?
   - **Aim**: Did I whiff?
   - **Info**: Did I not have enough info before committing?
   - **Communication**: Did my team not comm something?
3. **Third watch**: Focus on rounds you WON. Were you just lucky? Did you do something replicable? Winning due to skill is different from winning due to opponent mistakes.
4. **Pattern recognition**: After reviewing 3-5 maps, identify your top 2-3 recurring mistakes. Focus your next practice session on fixing those specific patterns.

### Pro VOD Review Protocol
1. **Pick a specific player on a specific agent on a specific map.** Don't try to learn everything at once.
2. **Watch their positioning** round by round. Note where they stand, when they rotate, and why.
3. **Watch their utility usage.** When do they use each ability? What info triggers the usage?
4. **Watch their economy decisions.** What do they buy on different rounds?
5. **Apply one thing** from the VOD to your next 5 games. Master it before reviewing more footage.

## Communication Protocols

### Essential Callouts
1. **Contact calls**: "One A Main," "Two pushing B Long," "Spike B site." Immediate, concise, no filler.
2. **Damage calls**: "One lit 80 A Main." Include HP if known, location, and count.
3. **Ability calls**: "Omen smoke fading B Main," "KAY/O suppress incoming," "Viper wall going down."
4. **Death calls**: "I'm dead, one A Main," "Traded, A clear." Even in death, your info is valuable.
5. **Economy calls**: "They're on eco," "Save round," "Full buy."

### Communication Discipline
- **No emotion in comms.** "I whiffed" is useless info. "One lit 40 B Main" is useful info.
- **No backseat gaming.** Telling a clutching teammate what to do adds pressure and noise. Call info only.
- **Call before the fight, not after.** "Flashing A Main in 2" gives teammates time to react. "I flashed A Main" is too late.
- **Use consistent terminology.** The entire team should use the same callout names. Inconsistency causes confusion.

### IGL Communication
- The IGL calls the play ONCE and clearly: "Hit B, smoke main and site, flash in 3, Raze entry."
- Teammates acknowledge with "Copy" or nothing. Do not repeat the call back.
- If the call changes mid-round, the IGL says "ROTATE" or "SWITCH [new site]." One word is enough.

## IGL Decision Trees

### Attack Side Decision Tree
```
Round Start
|-- Fast push? (enemy slow setup, weak site read)
|   |-- Yes -> Execute immediately with full utility
|   |-- No -> Default
|       |-- Gather info (dart, drone, jiggle peek)
|       |-- Info shows...
|           |-- Light defense on A -> Hit A
|           |-- Light defense on B -> Hit B
|           |-- Heavy stack -> Fake and hit opposite
|           |-- No clear read -> Split push (pressure both, commit to weaker)
|-- Time below 0:40?
|   |-- Yes -> Commit NOW. No more defaulting.
|   |-- No -> Continue gathering info
```

### Defense Side Decision Tree
```
Round Start
|-- Any early contact?
|   |-- Yes -> Call rotation/support
|   |-- No -> Hold default positions
|-- 0:30 into round, no contact?
|   |-- Enemy is likely slow defaulting
|   |-- Consider an aggressive peek for info
|   |-- Do NOT overcommit
|-- Enemy executing?
|   |-- Hold site if numbers allow (2+ defenders)
|   |-- If solo, delay and fall back
|   |-- Call for rotation
|-- Post-plant?
|   |-- Coordinate retake utility
|   |-- Push together, never solo retake
|   |-- Use remaining utility on spike
```

### Economy Decision Tree
```
Team credits assessment:
|-- All 5 can full buy? -> Full buy
|-- 3-4 can full buy? -> Team saves to full buy next round
|-- Just lost pistol round?
|   |-- Force buy (Spectre + light armor + partial util)
|   |-- OR full save for Round 3 (team decision)
|-- Won pistol + bonus round?
|   |-- Keep Spectre/Stinger, buy full utility
|   |-- Do NOT buy Phantom/Vandal on bonus round
|-- 2 rounds from loss? -> Consider force buying to avoid elimination
```

### Timeout Decision Tree
```
Should we timeout?
|-- Lost 3+ rounds in a row? -> Yes, reset mentality
|-- About to hit map point (win or lose)? -> Yes, prepare
|-- Enemy changed strategy and we haven't adapted? -> Yes, discuss counter
|-- Teammate is visibly tilted/unfocused? -> Yes, calm and refocus
|-- Economy is confusing (mixed buys)? -> Yes, coordinate
|-- None of the above? -> No, save timeout for later
```

## Summary: The Radiant Mindset

1. **Every round is independent.** Last round's result doesn't affect this round's chances. Reset mentally.
2. **Process over outcome.** Did you make the right decision? That matters more than whether you won the round. Bad decisions that result in kills are still bad decisions.
3. **Information is everything.** The team with more info wins. Gather it, share it, deny it.
4. **Fundamentals beat tricks.** Good crosshair placement, counter-strafing, and positioning beat flashy plays 90% of the time.
5. **Adapt or lose.** If something isn't working, change it. Doing the same thing and expecting different results is the definition of a hardstuck player.
6. **Communicate.** The best Radiant players talk constantly -- not with emotion, but with info. Callouts win rounds.
7. **Review your play.** If you're not watching your VODs, you're not improving. Every player in Radiant got there by identifying and fixing their mistakes.

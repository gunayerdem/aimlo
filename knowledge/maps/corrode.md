# CORRODE — Detayli Strateji ve Analiz

> PATCH-SENSITIVE NOTE: Corrode is a recent map addition. Death zone patterns and meta may evolve.

## 1. Core Principles
- Corrode features tight corridors and an industrial theme with heavy verticality and close-range engagements
- The map rewards disciplined utility usage and coordinated pushes through narrow choke points
- Attack fundamentals: Attackers must rely on smoke coverage and flash entries to break through confined lanes; dry peeking into tight corridors is a death sentence
- Defense fundamentals: Defenders benefit from strong anchor positions in compact sites with limited entry points; crossfires are devastating in narrow spaces
- Tempo principles: The map favors slow, methodical executes on attack — rushing through corridors without clearing angles leads to stacking deaths; defenders should play retake-oriented setups when outnumbered on a site

## 2. Common Death Zones
- **A Main Corridor**: The long, narrow hallway leading to A site funnels attackers into a predictable path. Players die here because they wide-swing without utility, giving defenders easy first bloods. Coaching context: if a player repeatedly dies in A Main, they are not using enough pre-entry utility.
- **B Tunnel Junction**: The intersection where B Tunnel meets the site connector creates a crossfire trap. Deaths here indicate poor clearing discipline — players push through without checking the off-angle cubby.
- **Mid Pipes**: The industrial pipe area in mid is a common Op angle. Players die here by ego-peeking into a known sightline. If this keeps happening, the player is not respecting information and needs to use utility before contesting.
- **A Site Catwalk**: The elevated catwalk on A site gives defenders a strong off-angle. Attackers die here because they forget to clear verticality. Coaching context: vertical awareness is a trainable habit.
- **B Back Alley**: The narrow corridor behind B site is a retake death zone. Defenders rotating through Back Alley get caught by lurkers who exploit the tight space.

## 3. Pattern -> Meaning Mapping
- IF: Player dies in A Main Corridor without using utility -> MEANING: No pre-entry discipline, treating a tight corridor like an open space -> FIX: Practice flash + smoke entries, never dry peek narrow lanes
- IF: Player gets flanked through Mid Pipes repeatedly -> MEANING: No mid control or information denial -> FIX: Assign one player to watch mid or place utility (tripwire, alarm bot) for flank coverage
- IF: Player loses retake duels in B Back Alley -> MEANING: Not using utility to clear before pushing narrow retake paths -> FIX: Coordinate retake with at least one flash or molly before re-entering
- IF: Player dies on A Catwalk as defender -> MEANING: Holding the same angle too many rounds, becoming predictable -> FIX: Rotate between catwalk and site-level positions every 2-3 rounds
- IF: Multiple players die at B Tunnel Junction -> MEANING: Stacking into the choke without spreading entries -> FIX: Stagger entry timing and use a split approach from connector

## 4. Side-Specific Mistakes

### Attack
- Rushing corridors without smoke coverage — Corrode's tight layout punishes uncoordinated aggression harder than any other map
- Ignoring mid control — mid on Corrode provides critical split options; skipping it limits attack to one-dimensional plays
- Over-committing to a site after losing the entry — tight spaces make trades difficult, and feeding into a lost corridor gives defenders free multi-kills
- Not clearing vertical angles on A site — the catwalk and elevated positions catch players who only clear ground level

### Defense
- Playing too aggressively in corridors — getting picks in tight lanes is tempting, but dying early leaves the site defenseless
- Neglecting rotate timing — Corrode's compact layout means rotations are fast, but defenders who rotate too late get caught in transitional positions
- Stacking one site without information — the map's corridors make it easy for attackers to fake and punish over-rotations
- Not using utility to slow pushes through narrow lanes — mollies and slows are extremely powerful in Corrode's tight choke points

## 5. Comp / Map Interaction Notes
- Controllers are essential — smoke coverage in narrow corridors is non-negotiable on Corrode; Viper and Omen excel with their ability to block long corridor sightlines
- Sentinels with area-denial (Killjoy, Cypher) thrive because trip wires and turrets cover the limited entry paths efficiently
- Duelists with flash-based entries (Phoenix, Yoru) are stronger than dash-based duelists because tight spaces limit movement abilities
- Initiators with narrow-space clearing utility (Breach, Fade) outperform wide-area initiators; Breach stuns through walls are devastating in corridors
- Double controller setups are viable and popular on Corrode due to the number of choke points that need smoking simultaneously

## 6. Coaching Lines
- "Corrode is not a map where you can rely on mechanics alone — corridor discipline wins rounds."
- "If you keep dying in the same hallway, you are not the problem — your approach to that hallway is."
- "On Corrode, the team that uses more utility in corridors wins more rounds. It is that simple."
- "Stop wide-swinging in tight spaces. Use your flash, wait for the pop, then swing. Every time."
- "Mid control on Corrode is like mid control on Ascent — whoever owns it controls the tempo of the round."
- "Retaking on Corrode requires patience. Clear every corner in the corridor before you reach the site."

## 7. Rank Modulation
- **Iron-Silver**: Players run through corridors without any utility. Focus coaching on basic corridor discipline: stop, use one ability, then push. Deaths in corridors will drop dramatically with this one habit.
- **Gold-Platinum**: Players use utility but not in coordination. Coach them on timing: the flash should pop as the entry player swings, not five seconds before. Corridor timing is everything on Corrode.
- **Diamond-Ascendant**: Players have corridor fundamentals but get predictable. Coach mid-round adaptations: if the enemy is stacking A corridor, shift mid and split. Map awareness and read-based decisions become the differentiator.
- **Immortal-Radiant**: Focus on anti-strat and round-to-round adaptation. At this level, Corrode becomes a chess match in narrow lanes — who reads the opponent's corridor defaults better wins the half.

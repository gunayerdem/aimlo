# AGENT: Skye

## 1. Role Identity
Skye is the versatile initiator who combines flashes, healing, and scouting into a single adaptive kit. She is the most flexible initiator -- her Guiding Light (bird flash) gives directional info on pop, her Trailblazer (dog) scouts and concusses, her Regrowth heals teammates, and her Seekers track down the nearest enemies. Skye is the initiator who adapts to what the team needs round by round. Need info? Send the dog. Need entry? Pop the flash. Need sustain? Heal the team. This flexibility makes her the ideal co-IGL initiator.

## 2. Core Responsibilities
- Flash for teammates using Guiding Light with precise pop timing to blind enemies without flashing the team
- Scout with Trailblazer before pushes, using the concuss to disable a key defender
- Heal teammates between fights with Regrowth to keep the team's HP economy healthy
- Deploy Seekers to locate enemies on retakes or when info is sparse
- Provide the team with flexible utility that adapts to round needs -- flashes for fast rounds, heals for slow ones
- Read whether a flashed enemy was hit (audio cue on Guiding Light) to give the team instant info

## 3. Common Mistakes
- Popping Guiding Light too early or too late, flashing teammates instead of enemies
- Trailblazer used from unsafe positions, leaving Skye's body vulnerable for 5+ seconds
- Regrowth used on a single teammate with minor damage, wasting the pool for later fights
- Seekers deployed in the open where enemies easily shoot them before they track
- Flashing without communicating to teammates, causing team-blinds that lose rounds
- Over-healing at the expense of flash and scout utility -- Skye is an initiator first, healer second

## 4. Pattern -> Meaning

**IF** teammates consistently report being flashed by Skye's Guiding Light
**MEANING** Flash pop timing or direction is off -- the bird is popping where teammates are looking
**FIX** Pop the bird above or to the side of the entry angle, not directly in the entry path. Call out "flashing" before every bird. Pop timing should match teammate peek timing -- 0.5 seconds before they peek.

**IF** Trailblazer is destroyed before reaching any enemy
**MEANING** The dog is being sent through obvious, pre-aimed paths
**FIX** Send the dog through off-angles, over boxes, or through smokes. Vary the path each round. The dog is most effective when it appears from an unexpected direction.

**IF** Regrowth pool is full at the end of rounds
**MEANING** The player is saving heal instead of using it to maintain team HP
**FIX** Heal between fights, not during them. If 2+ teammates are below 100 HP after a fight, heal before the next engagement. An unused heal pool at round end is wasted value.

**IF** Guiding Light gets the "hit" audio cue but no teammate follows up
**MEANING** Flash timing and team push timing are not synchronized
**FIX** The hit cue means an enemy is blinded. Call "they are flashed" immediately and the team must peek within 1 second. Practice this timing in scrims.

**IF** Seekers are deployed and die immediately
**MEANING** Seekers are launched in positions with too many enemy sightlines
**FIX** Deploy Seekers from behind cover or through smokes. They need a few seconds to find their targets. Use them on retakes when you need to locate scattered enemies.

## 5. Map Interactions
- **Haven**: Three sites benefit from Skye's flexibility -- she can flash any entry, scout any site, and heal between rotations. A long dog scout is a staple.
- **Ascent**: Guiding Light through A main or B main is strong flash entry. Dog through mid for info. Heal after mid fights keeps the team healthy for site takes.
- **Breeze**: Open spaces make the dog vulnerable but flashes cover wide angles. Seekers on retakes are high value on this large map.
- **Bind**: Tight corridors make dog scouts very effective in hookah and showers. Flash entries are strong in confined spaces.
- **Lotus**: Three sites again favor Skye's adaptability. Dog through rotating doors catches defenders off-guard.

## 6. Matchup Notes
- Against Jett: Skye's flash forces Jett to dash or die. If Jett dashes, she has used her key ability without getting a kill. Win-win.
- Against Killjoy: Dog triggers Killjoy utility safely, clearing alarmbot and turret without risking player HP.
- Against Sage: Regrowth competes with Sage's heal. On rounds with both, the team has exceptional sustain.
- Paired with a controller: Skye flashes through controller smokes for devastating blind entries. Flash from inside the smoke is nearly impossible to dodge.

## 7. What To Tell The Player

### When performing well
**Player Action:** You pop Guiding Light 0.5 seconds before your teammate's peek, call the hit audio cue immediately, and Regrowth your team between every fight to maintain a rolling HP advantage.
**Enemy Perception:** Defenders see flashes timed perfectly with peeks and believe the entry player has exceptional coordination. They do not realize the hit cue is giving your team a confirmed-blind signal — they think teammates are guessing when to swing.
**Enemy Reaction:** They are forced to either turn away from every Guiding Light (losing 0.5-1 second of crosshair time on the entry) or eat the flash and fight blind. Turning away repeatedly trains a flinch response that you can exploit by sending the bird without popping it — they turn away from a flash that never pops.
**Exploit Window:** The 0.5-1 second window between the flash pop and your teammate's peek arrival is a guaranteed first-shot window against a blinded defender. Between fights, each Regrowth session adds 20-40 HP across the team — over 3 engagements, that is 60-120 HP of advantage that compounds every duel from a coin flip into a favorable trade.
**Repeat Strategy:** Fight 1: Guiding Light from above, call hit cue, teammate swings. Between fights: Regrowth anyone below 100 HP behind cover. Fight 2: Guiding Light from a side angle (never the same trajectory twice). Fight 3: send Trailblazer from a varied path to scout before the flash. Always listen for the hit cue — no hit means no enemy, redirect.
**Enemy Adaptation:** After 4-5 rounds, defenders will pre-turn on every flash sound (reducing blind duration to 0.3 seconds), hold deeper positions outside Trailblazer pathing, or rush before you can set up the flash-peek timing.
**Counter-Adaptation:** When they pre-turn every flash: send Guiding Light and do NOT pop it — they turn for nothing and your teammate catches them mid-rotation looking the wrong way. When they hold deep: Trailblazer into their position to force them to shoot it (revealing location) or take the concuss. When they rush: Regrowth your team to full HP and hold the angle — rushers against full-HP defenders with a flash in hand lose the trade math.

**When struggling**: "You are team-flashing. Call every bird before popping it and wait until your teammate is ready to peek. The flash exists for them, not for you."

**When being predictable**: "Same dog path every round means enemies pre-aim it. Send it from different positions or through smokes. The concuss is powerful but only if the dog survives to reach them."

## 8. Rank Modulation
- **Low (Iron-Silver)**: Focus on not team-flashing. Call out every bird. Use the dog before pushes. Heal teammates when they are hurt. Keep it simple.
- **Mid (Gold-Platinum)**: Refine flash pop timing to match teammate peeks. Dog from varied angles. Heal between fights, not during. Listen for the flash hit cue.
- **High (Diamond-Ascendant)**: Flash through smokes for blind entries. Dog to trigger enemy utility. Seekers timed for retakes. Heal discipline -- know when to heal vs. save.
- **Elite (Immortal-Radiant)**: Flash pop timing within 0.5 seconds of teammate peek. Dog as bait to draw utility. Adaptive heal management based on team HP economy across the half.

# AGENT: Breach

## 1. Role Identity
Breach is the brute-force initiator whose entire kit fires through walls. Every ability -- Flashpoint, Fault Line, Aftershock, and Rolling Thunder -- penetrates solid surfaces, making Breach the ultimate anti-position agent. He does not need line of sight to any enemy; he needs to know where they are. Breach is the initiator who punishes players for hiding behind cover and rewards teams with strong communication. He is the most team-dependent initiator in the game and the most devastating when the team plays around his utility.

## 2. Core Responsibilities
- Flash through walls for teammates with Flashpoint, using the through-surface mechanic to create unavoidable blinds
- Concuss enemies with Fault Line to stun and slow them before team pushes
- Clear positions with Aftershock, forcing enemies off common holds or dealing lethal damage to stationary targets
- Deploy Rolling Thunder to concuss an entire site, enabling full team executes or retakes
- Coordinate with teammates constantly -- Breach's kit requires the team to follow up within specific timing windows
- Punish defenders who anchor positions behind cover by forcing them to move or die

## 3. Common Mistakes
- Flashpoint timing that blinds teammates more than enemies -- Breach flash goes through walls in both directions
- Aftershock used on positions where enemies can easily sidestep the small damage zone
- Fault Line fired without team follow-up, giving concussed enemies time to recover
- Rolling Thunder deployed without the team ready to push immediately after the concuss
- Playing too far from walls, reducing the through-wall utility that is Breach's entire identity
- No communication before abilities, causing teammates to mis-time their peeks and pushes

## 4. Pattern -> Meaning

**IF** Flashpoint consistently blinds teammates
**MEANING** Flash timing and direction are not coordinated with team positioning
**FIX** Call "flashing through [wall name]" before every Flashpoint. The team must know which side of the wall the flash comes from. Flash pops need to be timed when teammates are looking away from the flash and ready to peek after.

**IF** Aftershock deals zero damage across multiple rounds
**MEANING** Aftershock is placed on positions enemies do not hold or where they can easily escape
**FIX** Aftershock on tight corners, default plant spots, and common camp positions. It works best where enemies have limited escape routes -- boxes, cubby spots, and corner holds. Pair it with Fault Line to slow their escape.

**IF** Fault Line fires but no teammates peek during the stun window
**MEANING** Fault Line timing is not synced with team aggression
**FIX** Call "Fault Line going in 3, 2, 1" and the team peeks on 1. The stun window is short -- if teammates are not ready to peek during the concuss, the utility is wasted.

**IF** Rolling Thunder is used as a desperation play in clutch situations
**MEANING** The ult is being saved too long instead of used for decisive pushes
**FIX** Rolling Thunder is a team execute tool. Use it when 3+ teammates are alive and ready to push together. A 5v5 Rolling Thunder execute is far more valuable than a 1v2 desperation ult.

**IF** the player positions away from walls most rounds
**MEANING** They are not leveraging Breach's through-wall utility
**FIX** Breach should always be near a wall he can flash, concuss, or aftershock through. His positioning should be dictated by nearby surfaces, not standard angle-holding.

## 5. Map Interactions
- **Fracture**: Best Breach map. Every position has walls to flash/concuss through. Aftershock through multiple surfaces. Rolling Thunder across either site is devastating.
- **Split**: Tight corridors and walls everywhere. Flashpoint through A ramp walls, B heaven walls. Fault Line in mid.
- **Ascent**: A main and B main both have walls for through-surface utility. Aftershock on default plant spots.
- **Bind**: Hookah and showers are ideal for through-wall flashes. Aftershock through teleporter walls.
- **Pearl**: B site walls enable cross-site Flashpoint. Fault Line through mid doors controls rotations.

## 6. Matchup Notes
- Against Killjoy: Aftershock destroys Killjoy utility through walls. Lockdown can be cancelled with Rolling Thunder if positioned correctly.
- Against Sage: Aftershock through Sage wall destroys it. Fault Line concusses enemies behind wall.
- Against Omen: Breach can flash and concuss Omen through walls regardless of Omen's teleport position.
- Paired with a duelist: Breach flashes and concusses, duelist enters. This is the strongest initiator-duelist combo in the game when coordinated.

## 7. What To Tell The Player

**When performing well**:
Pattern: You are firing Flashpoint through walls so the flash pops on the defender's side while teammates are positioned to swing the instant it detonates, and calling a "Fault Line in 3, 2, 1" countdown so your team peeks during the concuss window.
Why it works: Through-wall Flashpoint is nearly unavoidable because enemies cannot see the flash approach or turn away in time — the wall blocks their line of sight to the indicator. The countdown for Fault Line synchronizes your team's peek with the 2-second concuss window, meaning they swing against opponents who cannot counterstrafe or adjust aim. Enemies hit by both Flashpoint and Fault Line in sequence face 3+ seconds of compromised fighting ability.
How to repeat: Step 1: Position next to a wall that separates you from the defender's hold angle. Step 2: Fire Flashpoint through the wall and call "flashing" so your teammates begin their swing. Step 3: Follow immediately with Fault Line through the same wall, counting down "3, 2, 1" for the team peek. Use Aftershock on tight corners or default plant spots where enemies have no lateral escape route.

**When struggling**: "Your team is not following up because they do not know when your abilities are coming. Call out everything. Breach without communication is the worst initiator in the game. Breach with communication is one of the best."

**When being predictable**: "Same wall, same flash, every round. Enemies are turning before you even flash. Move to different walls, change the flash angle, or delay the timing. They have your pattern memorized."

## 8. Rank Modulation
- **Low (Iron-Silver)**: Focus on one Flashpoint position per site. Call out before flashing. Use Fault Line when you see enemies. Keep Aftershock for common camp spots.
- **Mid (Gold-Platinum)**: Coordinate flash timing with teammate peeks. Aftershock on default plants. Fault Line before team pushes. Start using Rolling Thunder for executes.
- **High (Diamond-Ascendant)**: Through-wall utility from multiple wall positions. Aftershock-Fault Line combos that trap enemies. Rolling Thunder on coordinated five-person pushes.
- **Elite (Immortal-Radiant)**: Read-based Aftershock on known enemy positions. Flash timing within frames of teammate peeks. Rolling Thunder used proactively to force enemy repositioning before the push even begins.

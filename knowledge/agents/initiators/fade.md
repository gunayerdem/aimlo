# AGENT: Fade

## 1. Role Identity
Fade is the aggressive info-gatherer who chains abilities together to create cascading disadvantages for enemies. Unlike Sova's precision lineup approach, Fade's intel is closer range, faster to deploy, and designed to punish enemies immediately after revealing them. Her Haunt reveals, Prowler chases, Seize traps, and Nightfall terrorizes -- all creating a chain of abilities that strips enemies of their ability to hold positions. Fade rewards players who can sequence utility rapidly and follow up on their own info.

## 2. Core Responsibilities
- Reveal enemy positions with Haunt (eye) placed at angles that maximize scan area while being hard to destroy
- Follow up reveals immediately with Prowler to chase and nearsight tagged enemies
- Use Seize to lock down enemies in place, denying escape and enabling team trades
- Deploy Nightfall through walls to trail, deafen, and decay enemies before a full execute
- Chain abilities in sequence: Haunt reveals, Prowler chases, Seize locks, team pushes
- Provide faster, more aggressive info-gathering than lineup-dependent initiators

## 3. Common Mistakes
- Throwing Haunt into positions where it is instantly destroyed before scanning anyone
- Using Prowler without prior info -- the prowler wanders aimlessly without a Haunt or teammate callout to guide it
- Seize thrown into open space where enemies easily sidestep the tether zone
- Nightfall used defensively to stall rather than offensively to enable a push
- Not chaining abilities together -- using each ability in isolation instead of as a sequence
- Haunt thrown too high, scanning areas above enemies instead of at their level

## 4. Pattern -> Meaning

**IF** Haunt is consistently destroyed before completing its scan
**MEANING** The player is throwing Haunt into obvious, pre-aimed positions
**FIX** Throw Haunt above or behind common positions so enemies must turn away from teammates to destroy it. Use walls and ceilings to hide the eye's approach angle.

**IF** Prowler is sent without any prior reveal or info
**MEANING** Prowler is being wasted as a blind scout rather than a follow-up tool
**FIX** Prowler should chase already-revealed enemies. Use it after Haunt reveals a position, or when a teammate calls an enemy location. The prowler's chase AI is most effective on tagged enemies.

**IF** Seize consistently catches zero enemies
**MEANING** Seize placement is in the wrong spots or mistimed
**FIX** Seize should be placed at known enemy positions (from Haunt info), at choke points during a push, or on spike during post-plant to deny defuse. It is a trap, not a random throw.

**IF** abilities are used one at a time with gaps between each
**MEANING** The player is not chaining -- Fade's power is in the sequence, not individual abilities
**FIX** Practice the chain: Haunt to reveal, Prowler to chase immediately after, Seize to lock if needed, team enters during the chaos. The entire sequence should happen within 3-5 seconds.

**IF** Nightfall is held until clutch situations
**MEANING** The player is saving ult for personal plays instead of team executes
**FIX** Nightfall is an execute enabler. Use it at the start of a coordinated push when the full team can capitalize on the trail, deafen, and decay.

## 5. Map Interactions
- **Lotus**: Rotating doors and tight corridors make Seize extremely effective. Prowler through C main is devastating. Fade thrives here.
- **Pearl**: Haunt over B site walls reveals multiple positions. Seize at B main choke locks defenders in place.
- **Ascent**: Haunt over A main or B main provides reliable site scans. Nightfall through mid controls both flanks.
- **Split**: Tight chokes make Prowler nearly unavoidable. Seize at A ramps or B heaven traps defenders.
- **Bind**: Haunt through hookah scans B site effectively. Prowler through teleporters catches rotators.

## 6. Matchup Notes
- Against Omen: Haunt reveals Omen even after teleport. Prowler can chase Omen through smokes.
- Against Chamber: Seize prevents Chamber from using Rendezvous to escape. Critical for locking down Chamber's aggression.
- Against Killjoy: Prowler triggers Killjoy utility, clearing it before the team enters. Haunt reveals turret and alarm bot positions.
- Paired with a flash agent: Fade reveals, flash agent blinds, team enters. The reveal-into-flash combo is stronger than either alone.

## 7. What To Tell The Player

### When performing well
**Player Action:** You chain Haunt into Prowler into Seize within a 3-5 second window, placing the Haunt eye behind or above defenders so destroying it requires them to turn away from entry.
**Enemy Perception:** Defenders hear the Haunt throw and must immediately decide: shoot the eye (turning away from the entry path) or ignore it and get tagged with a terror trail. They experience Fade's kit as an escalating trap — each ability compounds the previous one's punishment.
**Enemy Reaction:** They are forced into a lose-lose-lose sequence. Shooting Haunt: costs 1 second of crosshair time on entry. Ignoring Haunt: terror trail reveals them AND sends Prowler directly to their position. Running from Prowler: Seize locks their escape route. The full chain strips vision, movement, and aim within 5 seconds with no single correct counterplay.
**Exploit Window:** The 2-3 second Prowler nearsight on a tagged enemy is the kill window — your team swings against a defender who cannot see, is pinned by Seize, and has already spent 1-2 seconds dealing with Haunt. The total debuff chain lasts 5+ seconds, which is long enough for a full site entry.
**Repeat Strategy:** Round 1: Haunt from a wall-bounce angle, Prowler to the tagged position, Seize on escape. Round 2: Haunt from the opposite side of the site to catch defenders who shifted positions. Round 3: skip Haunt and send Prowler raw — defenders who wait for Haunt audio before holding are caught off-guard by the Prowler arriving first.
**Enemy Adaptation:** After 3-5 rounds, defenders will pre-aim Haunt landing spots to destroy it in under 0.5 seconds, play deeper positions outside Seize range, or push before the chain begins.
**Counter-Adaptation:** When they instant-destroy Haunt: use wall-bounce trajectories that hide the eye's approach until the last moment. When they play deep: Seize their deep position first (before Haunt) to lock them in place, then Haunt over the locked enemy. When they push early: Prowler the push to nearsight the aggressors and call your team to collapse on the blinded rushers.

**When struggling**: "Your abilities are disconnected. Stop using them one at a time. Throw Haunt, then immediately send Prowler to the revealed position, then Seize if they try to run. The chain is everything."

**When being predictable**: "Enemies are pre-aiming your Haunt spots and destroying them before the scan. Change your throw angles -- use bounces off walls, throw from different positions, or delay the timing to catch them off-guard."

## 8. Rank Modulation
- **Low (Iron-Silver)**: Focus on Haunt placement that actually scans enemies. Use Prowler as a simple scout. Do not worry about chaining yet -- just get info.
- **Mid (Gold-Platinum)**: Introduce the Haunt-into-Prowler chain. Learn Seize spots at choke points. Start using Nightfall on team executes.
- **High (Diamond-Ascendant)**: Full ability chains within 3-5 seconds. Seize on spike for post-plant. Nightfall timed with full team commitment.
- **Elite (Immortal-Radiant)**: Adaptive Haunt angles based on opponent tendencies. Seize used to deny specific escape routes of known player habits. Nightfall timing varied to catch off-guard.

# Sentinels - Radiant Coaching Knowledge Base

Sentinels are the defensive backbone of any team. They anchor sites, delay pushes, gather passive info, and provide retake value. At Radiant level, a sentinel's setup is often the difference between winning and losing defensive rounds. Great sentinel players think 3 steps ahead -- they anticipate pushes, set traps where enemies WILL be, and have backup plans for every scenario.

---

## Sage

### Abilities Breakdown
- **Barrier Orb (C)** - 400 creds. Deploys a solid ice wall with multiple segments. Each segment has 400 HP when fully fortified (takes 3.3 seconds to reach full strength). Can be rotated before placement. Blocks movement, bullets, and abilities. Can be broken by enemies or Sage herself.
- **Slow Orb (Q)** - 200 creds each, 2 charges. Throwable orb that creates a field of ice on the ground, slowing all players who walk through it. Lasts 7 seconds. Makes a loud crunching sound when enemies walk on it (audio cue). The slow is significant -- roughly 50% movement speed reduction.
- **Healing Orb (E)** - Free, 45-second cooldown. Heals a single ally or Sage herself over 5 seconds. Restores 60 HP. Cannot overheal. Hold the ability and look at an ally to heal them, or press the ability button to self-heal.
- **Resurrection (X)** - 8 ult points. Revives a dead ally with full health after a brief channel. Sage is locked in place during the channel (~2 seconds). The revived ally is briefly vulnerable upon resurrection. The most impactful non-damage ultimate in the game.

### Site Anchoring
- **Wall placement is your identity.** A good Sage wall turns a 50-50 site into a 70-30 defender advantage. Key wall positions per map:
  - **Bind B**: Wall across B Long to force attackers through the chokepoint. Or wall Hookah entrance to eliminate one path entirely.
  - **Split A**: Wall across A Ramps to delay the push and force enemies to break it. Your team hears the break sound and rotates.
  - **Haven C**: Wall across C Long entrance. Forces Garage split or full rotate.
  - **Icebox A**: Wall across A Belt to split the site. Attackers must commit to one side.
  - **Ascent B**: Wall across B Main entrance. Classic stall.

### Setup Strategies
- **Slow + Wall combo**: Place wall across the choke, then Slow Orb in front of the wall. Enemies must walk through slow (loud audio cue) to reach the wall, then break the wall (loud break sound). This buys 10-15 seconds of delay.
- **One-person hold**: Sage can solo hold a site by stacking wall + slow. Teammates can stack the other site until they hear the push.
- **Aggressive wall boost**: Place wall against a box or ledge to boost yourself or a teammate to an off-angle. Examples: Bind A Short boost to look over the wall, Icebox A boost onto the containers, Split B boost onto Heaven ledge.
- **Retake wall**: Save wall for retakes. Place it to split the site, isolating the planter from their team. Push one side.

### When to Pick Sage
- Your team needs a solo site anchor
- The map has choke points where Barrier Orb provides maximum stall (Split, Bind, Icebox)
- Your composition lacks healing and you need sustain
- You want Resurrection for man-advantage in clutch rounds

### Map Strengths
- **Split**: S-tier. Wall across either A Ramps or B Main is the most efficient site stall in the game. Slow Orb on Mid. Self-sustaining anchor.
- **Icebox**: S-tier. Wall across A Belt, wall boost onto containers, Slow Orb B Kitchen. Resurrection behind cover on A.
- **Bind**: A-tier. Wall Hookah or B Long. Slow Orb Short. Wall boost A Short.
- **Haven**: A-tier. Three sites mean more demand for solo anchoring. Sage holds any site alone.
- **Lotus**: A-tier. Wall across rotating doors removes an entry path entirely.
- **Ascent**: B-tier. Playable but the map is open and wall is easier to break at distance.
- **Breeze**: B-tier. Large sites reduce wall impact. Still valuable for healing and ult.
- **Pearl**: B-tier. Long corridors make wall placement predictable.

### Common Mistakes
1. **Walling too early** - If you wall at the start of the round and nobody pushes, you wasted 400 creds. Wait for audio cues or initial contact.
2. **Not rotating wall orientation** - Default wall placement is perpendicular to your view. Rotate it (R key) to fit corridors properly. Many low-rank Sages never rotate.
3. **Healing during fights** - The heal takes 5 seconds. Healing mid-fight means you're not shooting for 5 seconds. Heal between engagements, not during.
4. **Resurrection in the open** - The channel locks you in place. If you rez in the open, both you and the revived ally die. Always rez behind cover.
5. **Wall boost every round** - Predictable boosts get pre-aimed. Use boost spots sparingly and vary which round you boost.
6. **Slow Orb on allies** - It slows your team too. Don't throw it where teammates are pushing.

### Retake Value
- Sage's retake value comes from Resurrection (turning a 4v5 into a 4v4 or 5v4), wall splits (isolating enemies), and Slow Orb (denying plant or delaying defuse peek).
- **Post-plant Slow Orb**: Throw Slow Orb on the spike. Enemies cannot defuse while slowed without being audible (crunch sound) and slow (easy to swing on).
- **Rez on retake**: If your duelist dies on entry, rez them behind cover for a second entry attempt. The man advantage often wins the retake.

### Pro Player Styles
- Sage remains a staple in pro play on specific maps. Her wall defines defensive setups on Split and Icebox, and Resurrection can single-handedly swing a round.

## Cypher

### Abilities Breakdown
- **Trapwire (C)** - 200 creds each, 2 charges. Placeable tripwire that stretches between two walls. Enemies who cross it are tethered, slowed, and revealed after a delay. If not destroyed within 3 seconds, the enemy is dazed. Trips can be recalled and repositioned.
- **Cyber Cage (Q)** - 100 creds each, 2 charges. Throwable cage that creates a cylindrical zone of vision denial when activated. Enemies passing through trigger an audio cue. Lasts 7 seconds. Can be activated remotely.
- **Spycam (E)** - Free, 15-second cooldown on recall or destroy. Deployable camera that Cypher can view remotely and fire a tracking dart. The dart reveals the enemy's position for a few seconds. Camera has 1 HP.
- **Neural Theft (X)** - 6 ult points. Used on a dead enemy body. Reveals the positions of all living enemies for a brief moment (two pings). Extremely powerful info for mid-round rotations and retakes.

### Site Anchoring
- **Cypher is the ultimate information sentinel.** His value is in knowing where enemies are WITHOUT engaging them.
- **Tripwire placement tiers**:
  - **S-tier placements**: Wires at ankle height in doorways (hard to spot while sprinting), wires on stairs where the geometry hides them, wires at the back of corridors so enemies hit them after committing to the push.
  - **A-tier placements**: Standard choke wires at head height (visible but punishing), wires behind boxes that catch flankers.
  - **Avoid**: Wires in the open where attackers can shoot them from distance, wires in obvious spots that get droned/scanned every round.

### Setup Strategies
- **Cage + Wire combo**: Place a Cyber Cage on the ground with a Tripwire slightly behind it. When enemies hit the wire, activate the cage. They are trapped, slowed, and vision-denied simultaneously. Peek through the cage (you know where they are; they don't know where you are).
- **Cross-map info**: Place Spycam watching one site, anchor the other with Tripwires. This gives you info on both sites simultaneously.
- **Flank denial**: One Tripwire watching your team's flank on attack is invaluable. Place it in a corridor behind your team after the initial take.
- **One-way Cyber Cage**: Place a Cyber Cage on an elevated surface (box edge, windowsill). When activated, the cage hangs off the edge, creating a one-way where you see feet but enemies see nothing.
- **Rotating setups**: Change your Tripwire and Spycam positions every 2-3 rounds. At Radiant level, enemies track your setup pattern and avoid traps if they're always in the same spot.

### When to Pick Cypher
- The map has narrow corridors and choke points for Tripwires (Bind, Ascent, Split)
- Your team needs flank denial on attack
- You want an info sentinel who gathers intel passively (no active piloting needed)
- The map rewards aggressive Spycam placement for early info

### Map Strengths
- **Bind**: S-tier. Hookah Tripwires, Short Tripwires, Spycam B Long. Every corridor is a trap opportunity. Neural Theft on the tiny map has maximum impact.
- **Ascent**: S-tier. A main Tripwires, B main Tripwires, Spycam Mid. The map's structure is perfect for Cypher's kit.
- **Split**: A-tier. A Ramps Tripwires, B main Tripwires. Tight corridors for Cage + Wire combos.
- **Haven**: A-tier. Triple sites mean more areas to deny, but Cypher can cover two with trips + cam on the third.
- **Sunset**: A-tier. Mid corridor Tripwires, Spycam B main. Tight geometry.
- **Pearl**: B-tier. Long corridors let enemies shoot trips from distance.
- **Breeze**: C-tier. Too open. Tripwires are visible from far away, Spycam gets sniped.
- **Icebox**: B-tier. A site Tripwires work, B Kitchen Spycam. Vertical play limits trip placement.

### Common Mistakes
1. **Same setup every round** - Enemies drone your trips and know where they are. Rotate between 3-4 different setups per site.
2. **Spycam in obvious spots** - If the camera is destroyed within 5 seconds every round, it's too obvious. Find creative angles.
3. **Not recalling trips** - Tripwires can be recalled and repositioned mid-round. If you rotate, bring your trips with you.
4. **Neural Theft on bodies you can't reach** - The ult requires proximity to the body. Don't die trying to reach a body in enemy territory. Use it on bodies in safe areas.
5. **Panic activating Cyber Cage** - The cage audio cue only triggers on enemy passage. Wait for the Tripwire tether before activating the cage for maximum combo value.
6. **Ignoring Spycam dart** - The tracking dart from Spycam is free info. Use it aggressively when enemies are distracted.

### Retake Value
- **Neural Theft is the best retake info tool in the game.** 6 ult points for full enemy team reveal. Use it at the start of every retake.
- Cyber Cage on spike during retake provides vision denial for the defuser.
- Tripwires during retake can be placed behind the team to catch lurkers.

### Pro Player Styles
- **Chronicle (Fnatic)**: Known for creative Spycam placements that opponents cannot predict. Rotates his setups constantly. His Cage + Wire combos have a high conversion rate because he varies timing and placement.
- Cypher has seen a resurgence in VCT 2024-2025 after multiple buffs. His information-gathering passive style fits structured team play.

## Killjoy

### Abilities Breakdown
- **Nanoswarm (C)** - 200 creds each, 2 charges. Deployable grenade that sits on the ground until activated. When activated, creates a swarm of nanobots dealing 45 DPS for 4 seconds. Invisible to enemies until activated. Total potential: 180 damage per Nanoswarm.
- **Alarmbot (Q)** - 200 creds. Deployable bot that tracks and chases the nearest enemy, exploding on contact to apply Vulnerable (double damage from all sources for 4 seconds). Has a limited activation range around Killjoy. 100 HP.
- **Turret (E)** - Free, 20-second cooldown on destroy. Deployable turret that auto-fires at enemies in its cone of vision. Deals low damage (3 round burst, 8 damage per bullet) but tags enemies with a slow effect. Primarily an info tool, not a damage tool.
- **Lockdown (X)** - 8 ult points. Deploys a device that winds up for 13 seconds, then detains all enemies in its large radius for 8 seconds. Detained enemies cannot move, shoot, or use abilities. The device has 150 HP and can be destroyed during windup.

### Site Anchoring
- **Killjoy is the queen of site lockdown.** Her setup turns a bomb site into a kill zone.
- **Standard site setup pattern**:
  1. Place Turret watching the main approach (for info, not damage).
  2. Place Alarmbot at the entry point (Vulnerable debuff + audio cue).
  3. Place both Nanoswarms on default plant spots or under common push paths.
  4. Hold a safe angle and wait for audio cues.
- **When enemies hit the setup**: Alarmbot chases and applies Vulnerable, you activate Nanoswarm(s) on the Vulnerable enemies (double damage = 90 DPS instead of 45 = 360 damage per Nanoswarm). This combo is lethal.

### Setup Strategies
- **Molly lineups from safety**: Nanoswarms can be placed pre-round in hidden spots on plant positions. When the spike is planted, activate them from safety for post-plant denial. Learn the hidden spots for each map.
- **Aggressive turret placement**: Place turret watching flanks or aggressive angles to get early info on enemy rotations. The turret's slow tag confirms enemy position even if you can't see them.
- **Alarmbot bait**: Place Alarmbot slightly behind a corner. When it chases an enemy, the enemy must decide: shoot the bot (giving you time to peek) or shoot you (getting hit by the bot's Vulnerable).
- **Range awareness**: Killjoy's Alarmbot and Turret deactivate if she is too far away (40m approximately). This means she MUST stay on or near her site. Rotating too far disables her setup.
- **Fake site setup**: Place turret and one Nanoswarm on a site to simulate a full setup, then actually anchor the other site. The turret's presence alone deters pushes.

### When to Pick Killjoy
- The map has enclosed bomb sites where her utility covers all angles (Ascent, Bind, Haven)
- Your team needs a solo site anchor who doesn't require backup
- You want the strongest post-plant denial in the game (double Nanoswarm on spike)
- The enemy runs fast executes that Alarmbot + Nanoswarm can punish

### Map Strengths
- **Ascent**: S-tier. B site Nanoswarm placements under default plant are hidden and lethal. Turret on B Logs, Alarmbot B main entrance. A site also fully coverable.
- **Bind**: S-tier. Hookah Nanoswarm placements, Turret Short, Alarmbot B Long. Post-plant mollies on both sites.
- **Haven**: A-tier. Can setup any site effectively. C site Nanoswarm on default plant, Turret C Long.
- **Sunset**: A-tier. Tight sites for Nanoswarm coverage. Mid turret for info.
- **Split**: A-tier. A/B Heaven turret for info. Nanoswarm on plant spots.
- **Lotus**: B-tier. Sites are more open, reducing Nanoswarm coverage. Turret through doors.
- **Icebox**: B-tier. A site setup works but B site is complex for her kit.
- **Breeze**: C-tier. Large sites and long sightlines make her setup easy to clear from distance.

### Common Mistakes
1. **Same Nanoswarm spots every round** - At Radiant level, enemies track your molly positions and avoid or destroy them. Vary between 4-5 different spots.
2. **Activating Nanoswarm without Alarmbot Vulnerable** - The double damage from Alarmbot Vulnerable is what makes Nanoswarm lethal. Wait for the Alarmbot proc.
3. **Turret facing the wrong direction** - Turret should watch the angle you're NOT watching. If you're watching A Main, turret watches Catwalk.
4. **Rotating too far from setup** - If your Alarmbot and turret deactivate because you rotated too far, you've lost all site utility. Stay within range or recall utility before rotating.
5. **Lockdown without cover** - The 13-second windup is long. Place Lockdown behind walls or in positions enemies can't easily shoot. If they destroy it, you wasted 8 ult points.
6. **Not hiding Nanoswarms** - Visible Nanoswarms get destroyed for free. Place them under boxes, in corners, behind objects.

### Retake Value
- **Lockdown is a retake ult.** Don't use it to hold sites (you have your setup for that). Save Lockdown for retakes -- place it behind cover, let it wind up, and push when enemies are detained for 8 seconds.
- Nanoswarm on spike during retake denies defuse attempts.
- Turret repositioned during retake provides info on enemy positions.

### Pro Player Styles
- Killjoy is one of the most consistent sentinel picks in VCT. Her setup is standardized at the pro level, but the best players (like those on DRX and Fnatic) vary their Nanoswarm positions every round to prevent enemy adaptation.
- **Less (LOUD)** has been known to flex to Killjoy on specific maps, using aggressive turret placements for early info that feeds into LOUD's fast-rotation playstyle.

## Chamber

### Abilities Breakdown
- **Trademark (C)** - 200 creds, 1 charge. Deployable trap that scans for enemies in a cone. When triggered, creates a lingering slow field. Audio cue on trigger. Primarily a flank-watching tool.
- **Headhunter (Q)** - 150 creds per bullet, up to 8 bullets. Heavy pistol with ADS (aim down sights) that one-shot headshots at any range. Fires like a Sheriff on steroids. Extremely powerful on eco rounds.
- **Rendezvous (E)** - Free, 30-second cooldown on use or 45 seconds if the anchor is destroyed. Deploys a teleport anchor. Chamber can teleport to the anchor from within a certain range. The aggressive repositioning tool -- take a fight, TP out if it goes bad.
- **Tour De Force (X)** - 7 ult points. Gives Chamber a powerful custom sniper rifle. Kills create a large slow field around the killed enemy. One-shot kill to the body at any range. 5 bullets. The slow field is the key utility here -- it denies pushes.

### Site Anchoring
- Chamber anchors differently from other sentinels. He doesn't set up utility on site -- he IS the setup. His presence on a long angle with Headhunter or Op + Rendezvous escape is his site hold.
- **Aggressive anchor**: Push into an off-angle, take a fight, TP back to safety. This is Chamber's signature play pattern.
- **Trademark as the setup**: His one trap covers one approach. Place it on the angle you're NOT watching to get an audio cue of enemy approach.
- **Dual-site presence**: Place Rendezvous anchor on one site, push aggressively on another. If things go bad, TP back. This pseudo dual-site presence is unique to Chamber.

### Setup Strategies
- **Op + Rendezvous**: This is THE Chamber play. Buy an Op, hold a long angle, get a pick, TP away. This was the dominant meta strategy in 2022-2023 before multiple nerfs.
- **Headhunter eco rounds**: With 150 per bullet, a full Headhunter (1200 creds) on an eco round is devastating. It one-shot headshots at any range, giving Chamber a "mini-Op" capability.
- **Tour De Force eco**: Chamber's ult on an eco round gives the team a free Op-level weapon. The slow fields also deny pushes.
- **Trademark + angle hold**: Place Trademark to watch your off-angle flank, then push the opposite angle. When the trap triggers, rotate to address the flank.

### When to Pick Chamber
- You are a strong Op player who wants an escape tool (Rendezvous)
- The map has long sightlines for Op/Headhunter play (Breeze, Icebox, Haven)
- Your team needs a sentinel who can also take aggressive duels
- You want eco-round value with Headhunter and Tour De Force

### Map Strengths
- **Breeze**: A-tier. Long sightlines for Op play. Rendezvous escape from Mid or A Hall. Tour De Force slow fields deny wide pushes.
- **Haven**: A-tier. Op from C Long or A Long with TP escape. Trademark on the unattended site.
- **Icebox**: A-tier. Op B Kitchen, TP to safety. Headhunter on eco through B site.
- **Ascent**: A-tier. Op Mid with TP to Cat or A site. Trademark B main.
- **Lotus**: B-tier. Some Op angles but Rendezvous range limits dual-site play.
- **Bind**: B-tier. Shorter sightlines reduce Op value. Headhunter in Hookah still effective.
- **Split**: B-tier. Tight corridors limit Op play. Headhunter works in Mid.
- **Pearl**: A-tier. Long B main for Op. Rendezvous to safety.

### Common Mistakes
1. **Rendezvous anchor in predictable spots** - If enemies know where your TP anchor is, they push it and kill you on arrival. Vary anchor placement.
2. **Op without TP escape plan** - If your TP is on cooldown or destroyed, you are a sitting duck with an Op. Track your cooldowns.
3. **Over-investing in Headhunter on gun rounds** - 1200 creds for 8 Headhunter bullets on a gun round is wasteful. Buy 2-4 bullets as a utility sidearm, not a full 8.
4. **Forgetting Trademark** - Chamber only has one trap. If you forget to place it, you have zero site utility. Place it every round.
5. **Playing Chamber like a sentinel** - Chamber is a sentinel in name only. He plays like a duelist with a safety net. Don't play passive.
6. **Tour De Force body shots without follow-up** - The kill slow field is the value. Don't shoot and hide. Shoot, create the slow field, and push the slowed enemies.

### Retake Value
- Chamber's retake value comes from Tour De Force (slow fields deny post-plant positions) and Headhunter (accurate picks during retake).
- Rendezvous for aggressive peeks during retake -- peek, get info or a kill, TP back.
- Trademark on spike to alert the team when enemies approach for defuse denial.

### Pro Player Styles
- **yay (formerly OpTic/C9)**: The defining Chamber player. Known as "El Diablo" for his Op precision. His Rendezvous usage was the benchmark -- aggressive off-angle Op picks into immediate TP. Retired from pro play but his style defined the Chamber meta.
- After multiple nerfs (Rendezvous range reduction, Headhunter price increase, Tour De Force speed reduction), Chamber has fallen out of the S-tier but remains viable on Op-friendly maps.

## Deadlock

### Abilities Breakdown
- **GravNet (C)** - 200 creds, 1 charge. Throwable disc that deploys a net on impact. Enemies caught in the net are slowed and forced to crouch. They must destroy the net to free themselves (shoot it). Use for area denial and slowing pushes.
- **Sonic Sensor (Q)** - 200 creds each, 2 charges. Deployable sensor that activates when it detects sound (footsteps, gunfire, abilities) in its radius. When triggered, concusses all enemies in its area. Can be destroyed (1 HP).
- **Barrier Mesh (E)** - Free, 40-second cooldown. Deployable mesh barrier that creates multiple orbs forming a wall. Enemies cannot pass through. Each orb has 100 HP. Blocks one path entirely until destroyed. Different from Sage wall in that it's orb-based and lower profile.
- **Annihilation (X)** - 8 ult points. Fires a pulse of nanowire that cocoons an enemy it contacts, pulling them along a nanowire path to a central point. If the enemy reaches the endpoint, they die. Teammates can destroy the cocoon to free the ally.

### Site Anchoring
- Deadlock anchors through area denial and path blocking. Her setup forces enemies to commit utility to break through.
- **Barrier Mesh placement**: Block the primary entry path. Force attackers to either break the mesh (giving audio and time) or take an alternate path (where your team is waiting).
- **Sonic Sensor as audio trap**: Place in corridors where enemies must make noise (running, using abilities). The concuss punishes fast pushes.
- **GravNet for post-plant**: Throw GravNet on spike to slow and crouch-lock the defuser. They are sitting ducks.

### Setup Strategies
- **Barrier Mesh + Sonic Sensor**: Block a path with Barrier Mesh, place Sonic Sensor on the alternate path. Enemies are funneled and punished.
- **GravNet for entry denial**: Throw GravNet into a choke as enemies push. The crouch-lock makes them easy headshot targets.
- **Annihilation for picks**: Target the spike carrier or the star player. If Annihilation connects, the team must waste utility to save them or lose the player.

### When to Pick Deadlock
- The map has narrow chokepoints where Barrier Mesh blocks entire paths
- You want a sentinel who provides area denial rather than info (compared to Cypher)
- The enemy runs fast executes that Sonic Sensor and GravNet can disrupt
- You need post-plant denial with GravNet

### Map Strengths
- **Lotus**: A-tier. Barrier Mesh blocks doors. Sonic Sensor in corridors. GravNet through tight entries.
- **Split**: A-tier. Barrier Mesh A main or B main. Sonic Sensor on Ramps/Mid.
- **Bind**: B-tier. Barrier Mesh Hookah or Short. Sonic Sensor in teleporters.
- **Haven**: B-tier. Can block one entry path per site. Sonic Sensor Garage.
- **Sunset**: B-tier. Tight corridors for Barrier Mesh and Sonic Sensor.

### Common Mistakes
1. **Barrier Mesh in easy-to-break positions** - If enemies can shoot the orbs from distance without committing, the mesh is free. Place it around corners or in positions where enemies must be close.
2. **Sonic Sensor in obvious spots** - The sensor has 1 HP. Place it in hidden positions where enemies trigger it before seeing it.
3. **Annihilation at long range** - The nanowire has travel time. At long range, enemies dodge it easily. Use at medium to close range.
4. **GravNet without follow-up** - The crouch-lock lasts ~5 seconds but enemies can shoot their way out. Peek them while they're stuck.
5. **Not using Barrier Mesh on retakes** - The mesh can block post-plant positions, forcing enemies out of cover.

### Retake Value
- Barrier Mesh during retake blocks escape routes, trapping enemies on site.
- GravNet on spike area during retake locks down the defuse zone.
- Annihilation on a retake picks off one enemy, turning a 4v4 into a 4v3.

## Vyse

### Abilities Breakdown
- **Razorvine (C)** - 200 creds each, 2 charges. Deployable vine wall that damages enemies who pass through it. Creates a zone of metallic vines that act as both a damage barrier and a visual/audio cue. Use for area denial and slowing pushes.
- **Shear (Q)** - 250 creds, 1 charge. Targeted ability that creates a metallic zone. After a brief delay, the zone activates, damaging and revealing enemies inside. Use for corner clearing and info gathering.
- **Arc Rose (E)** - Free, recollectable. Deployable metallic flower that creates a vision-blocking field when enemies approach. Functions as a reactive smoke that activates on enemy proximity. The automatic activation means Vyse doesn't need to manually trigger it.
- **Steel Garden (X)** - 8 ult points. Creates a large field of metallic flora that suppresses enemy abilities and distorts their vision. Enemies inside cannot use abilities and have reduced visibility. The suppression zone covers a large area and lasts several seconds.

### Site Anchoring
- Vyse anchors through reactive utility. Her kit responds to enemy aggression rather than requiring pre-planned activation.
- **Arc Rose as reactive smoke**: Place it in corridors. When enemies approach, it auto-activates, blocking their vision and alerting you.
- **Razorvine for area denial**: Block entry paths with vine walls. Enemies take damage walking through, giving you time and an audio cue.
- **Shear for info**: Use on common positions to reveal enemies and deal chip damage.

### Setup Strategies
- **Arc Rose + Razorvine combo**: Arc Rose provides vision denial when enemies push, Razorvine deals damage as they walk through. The combination slows and punishes pushes.
- **Steel Garden for retake denial**: The suppression field removes enemy ability usage during post-plant, similar to KAY/O's NULL/CMD but as a zone rather than an aura.
- **Aggressive Shear usage**: Use Shear proactively to clear positions before your team pushes on attack.

### When to Pick Vyse
- You want a sentinel with reactive, automatic utility (less micro-management than Killjoy/Cypher)
- The map has tight corridors where Razorvine and Arc Rose maximize coverage
- Your team needs a sentinel with ability suppression (Steel Garden)
- You want a sentinel who provides value on both attack and defense through Shear info

### Map Strengths
- **Ascent**: A-tier. Corridors for Razorvine, Arc Rose on A main or B main. Steel Garden for retakes.
- **Bind**: A-tier. Tight spaces for all abilities. Hookah and Short are ideal for Razorvine.
- **Split**: A-tier. Ramps and corridors suit her reactive kit.
- **Sunset**: A-tier. Mid and site corridors for Razorvine and Arc Rose.
- **Lotus**: B-tier. Door mechanics interact with her kit in interesting ways.

### Common Mistakes
1. **Arc Rose in the open** - The reactive smoke needs to be in a corridor where enemies must push through it. In the open, they walk around it.
2. **Razorvine without line-of-sight advantage** - Place vines where YOU can see enemies walking through from a safe angle.
3. **Steel Garden too late** - The ult needs to be placed before the enemy retake, not during. The setup time makes late usage risky.
4. **Not recollecting Arc Rose** - Like Gekko's utility, recollectable pieces should always be picked up.

### Retake Value
- Steel Garden is the primary retake tool -- suppressing enemy abilities makes retaking significantly easier.
- Shear reveals enemy positions during retake.
- Razorvine blocks escape routes during retake pushes.

## General Sentinel Philosophy at Radiant Level

### The Information Hierarchy
1. **Passive info** (tripwires, turrets, alarms) > **Active info** (droning, darting). Passive info costs no attention. Active info requires focus.
2. Set up passive info at the start of every round. This is non-negotiable for sentinel players.
3. Call all info immediately: "One crossing B Long," "Trip triggered Hookah," "Turret tagged A Main."

### Rotation Protocol
- Sentinels should be the LAST to rotate unless the hit is confirmed. Your setup is your value -- leaving it reduces your impact.
- If you must rotate, recall or destroy your utility and redeploy on the new site.
- Exception: if the enemy is full executing your site and you cannot hold, rotate early and set up a retake.

### Sentinel Economy
- Always buy full utility. A sentinel without setup is worse than any other role without abilities because you provide nothing without your kit.
- On eco rounds, buy utility with Classic. Your Tripwire/Turret/Nanoswarm is more valuable than a Spectre.
- Save Lockdown/Resurrection for gun rounds. Don't waste them on eco saves.

### Setup Variation
- At Radiant level, enemies track your setups. If you place the same traps in the same spots, they destroy them for free every round.
- Rotate between at least 3 different setups per site. Label them mentally: "Setup A" (aggressive), "Setup B" (standard), "Setup C" (retake-oriented).
- Change setups after the enemy successfully clears your traps. They will repeat the clear if it worked.

### Sentinel on Attack
- On attack, sentinels primarily provide flank denial. Place a trip or turret behind your team to prevent flanks.
- Post-plant is where sentinels shine on attack. Nanoswarms, Slow Orbs, Trapwires on spike -- all deny defuse.
- Sage wall for safe plant, Cypher camera for post-plant info, Killjoy Lockdown for retake denial.
- Don't entry as a sentinel. Stay alive for post-plant value. A dead sentinel on attack has minimal utility.

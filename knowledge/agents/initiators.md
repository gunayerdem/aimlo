# Initiators - Radiant Coaching Knowledge Base

Initiators gather information and displace enemies to open up site takes. At Radiant level, initiator players are often the IGL or co-IGL because their utility dictates the flow of every round. A good initiator gets their team at least 2 pieces of info before any execute begins.

---

## Sova

### Abilities Breakdown
- **Owl Drone (C)** - 300 creds. Pilotable drone that tags enemies with a dart, revealing them for 3 pings. The drone has 100 HP and can be shot down. While piloting, Sova is completely vulnerable. Scouting duration is ~10 seconds.
- **Shock Bolt (Q)** - 150 creds each, 2 charges. Electric arrow that detonates on impact, dealing up to 90 damage in its center. Can bounce up to 2 times. Used for post-plant lineups, clearing corners, and chip damage. The bounces allow creative lineups from safe positions.
- **Recon Bolt (E)** - Free, 40-second cooldown. Arrow that sticks to surfaces and pulses sonar, revealing all enemies in line of sight of the bolt for 3 pings. The bolt can be destroyed (1 HP). The most consistent info-gathering ability in the game.
- **Hunter's Fury (X)** - 8 ult points. Three long-range energy blasts that travel through the entire map. Each blast deals 80 damage and briefly reveals hit enemies. Blasts can be fired in sequence with repositioning between them.

### Info Gathering
- **Recon Bolt is your primary info tool.** Learn at least 5 arrow lineups per map -- standard site clears for both attack and defense.
- **Recon placement priority**: Place the dart where it scans the most area without being easily destroyed. High positions (ceilings, boxes, walls above head height) force enemies to look up to shoot it, giving your team a timing advantage.
- **Double info protocol**: Drone first to get initial reads, then Recon Bolt to confirm positions. This two-step info gathering is the Sova standard at pro level.
- **Early round recon**: On defense, fire a Recon Bolt at common attacker positions within the first 10 seconds. This reveals fast pushes and gives your team time to rotate.

### Flash Techniques
- Sova has no flash. His info utility compensates -- a revealed enemy is easier to kill than a flashed one because the info is precise and long-lasting.

### When to Pick Sova
- The map is open with long sightlines where Recon Bolt can scan large areas (Breeze, Ascent, Haven)
- Your team needs reliable, repeatable info gathering
- You want strong post-plant denial with Shock Bolt lineups
- The map has open ceilings for Recon Bolt lineups (Sova struggles on indoor maps)

### Map Strengths
- **Breeze**: S-tier. Open map, every Recon Bolt covers massive area. Shock Bolt lineups for both sites. Hunter's Fury through Mid for cross-map kills.
- **Ascent**: S-tier. Open A site for Recon, B main Owl Drone clears, Shock Bolt lineups for both plants.
- **Haven**: A-tier. Three sites mean more Recon value. Hunter's Fury through Garage. Owl Drone C Long.
- **Icebox**: A-tier. Recon Bolt on A site clears Pipes, Rafters, Belt. B site drone into Kitchen.
- **Bind**: B-tier. Hookah and Short are tight for Recon. Shock Bolt lineups still strong.
- **Lotus**: B-tier. Rotating doors block some Recon angles. Open ceilings on C help.
- **Split**: C-tier. Enclosed map limits Recon Bolt effectiveness. Low ceilings block many lineups.
- **Sunset**: C-tier. Tight corridors and enclosed areas limit Sova's value.

### Common Mistakes
1. **Using the same Recon Bolt lineup every round** - Radiant players destroy the dart instantly if they've seen it before. Rotate between 3-4 lineups per site.
2. **Owl Drone without team cover** - You are defenseless while droning. Always have a teammate watching your body.
3. **Shock Bolt for kills instead of post-plant** - Shock Bolt's primary value is post-plant denial. Save at least one arrow for post-plant.
4. **Hunter's Fury reactively** - The ult is slow and telegraphed. Use it proactively (post-plant denial, early round wall-bang angles) rather than reactively.
5. **Recon Bolt too low** - A dart at ground level is destroyed before the first pulse. Aim for elevated positions.
6. **Not learning lineups** - At Radiant level, lineup knowledge is expected. A Sova without lineups is half an agent.

### Combo Plays
- **Recon + team peek**: Fire Recon, wait for the first pulse, then your duelist peeks the revealed target. The 0.5-second scan gives precise info for the swing.
- **Drone + Recon double scan**: Drone to tag one enemy and make others reposition, then Recon Bolt the area they reposition to. Forces enemies into impossible situations.
- **Shock Bolt + molly stack**: Coordinate your Shock Bolt with a controller's molly (Brimstone Incendiary, Viper Snake Bite) for double post-plant denial. The defuser cannot wait out both.
- **Hunter's Fury + smoke**: Fire Hunter's Fury through a teammate's smoke. The enemy cannot dodge what they cannot see coming.

### Pro Player Styles
- **Chronicle (Fnatic)**: The tactical Sova. Known for creative Recon Bolt placements that are hard to predict. Uses Owl Drone for deep info gathering rather than aggression. His post-plant Shock Bolt lineups are benchmark-level.
- **Less (LOUD)**: Aggressive Sova play. Uses Owl Drone offensively, tagging enemies and immediately pushing behind the drone with teammates. His Hunter's Fury timing on post-plant is among the best in the world.

## Breach

### Abilities Breakdown
- **Aftershock (C)** - 100 creds, 1 charge. Fires a charge through walls that detonates after a delay, dealing 60 damage in three pulses. Use to clear tight corners, cubbies, and common hide spots behind walls.
- **Flashpoint (Q)** - 250 creds each, 2 charges. Flash that fires through walls. Fully blinds enemies facing the point of origin. 2-second blind duration. One of the strongest flashes in the game because it goes through walls with no counterplay except looking away.
- **Fault Line (E)** - Free, 40-second cooldown. Seismic charge that travels forward in a long line, concussing all enemies hit. Can be charged to increase range. Concuss lasts 3.5 seconds. Use for stopping pushes, clearing long corridors, and combo plays.
- **Rolling Thunder (X)** - 8 ult points. Massive seismic wave that travels forward, knocking up and concussing all enemies in a large cone. The knockup is disorienting and prevents shooting for ~1 second. The concuss lasts 6 seconds. One of the best execute ultimates in the game.

### Info Gathering
- Breach does not gather info directly. His value is in displacing enemies from positions. If you Aftershock a corner and the enemy runs out, you got info.
- **Audio cues**: Listen for the audio cue when Flashpoint or Aftershock hit an enemy. The hit sound confirms an enemy is in that position.
- Pair Breach with an info-gathering initiator (Sova, Fade) for optimal value. Breach + Sova is a classic double-initiator combo.

### Flash Techniques
- **Through-wall flashes**: Flashpoint goes through walls. Stand behind a wall, aim at head height on the other side, and flash. Enemies on the other side have zero visual warning -- they only hear the audio cue.
- **Double flash entry**: Fire both Flashpoints in rapid succession. The first forces enemies to turn, the second catches them turning back. Your duelist enters between the two flashes.
- **Popflash timing**: Flashpoint has a brief charge time. Hold the ability until your duelist is ready to swing, then release. The flash should pop as the duelist clears the angle.
- **Defensive flashing**: On defense, use Flashpoint through the wall when you hear enemies pushing. The flash stops the push and gives your team time to reposition.

### When to Pick Breach
- The map has many walls to flash and Aftershock through (Fracture, Split, Bind)
- Your team runs coordinated executes with duelist entry support
- The enemy plays tight positions behind walls where Aftershock has value
- You need an initiator with strong anti-push utility on defense

### Map Strengths
- **Fracture**: S-tier. Walls everywhere for through-wall flashes. Aftershock clears every cubby. Rolling Thunder on either site forces a full retreat.
- **Split**: A-tier. Flashpoint through A Ramps wall, B Heaven wall. Fault Line down Mid.
- **Bind**: A-tier. Flash through Hookah walls, Short walls. Aftershock Hookah cubby.
- **Sunset**: A-tier. Walls throughout Mid and both sites for through-wall utility.
- **Ascent**: B-tier. Some wall flash spots but the map is more open.
- **Haven**: B-tier. Playable on specific sites (C site has great wall flash angles).
- **Breeze**: C-tier. Too open for Breach. Limited wall angles.
- **Icebox**: B-tier. B site walls work for Aftershock and Flash, A site is too open.

### Common Mistakes
1. **Flashing teammates** - Breach's Flashpoint has a wide blind cone. Communicate flash timing clearly: "Flashing in 3... 2... 1..."
2. **Aftershock on positions enemies already left** - Don't Aftershock default positions after the first 30 seconds. By then, enemies have moved.
3. **Fault Line without purpose** - The charge time is noticeable. Don't throw Fault Line randomly. Use it to stop a specific push or clear a specific area.
4. **Rolling Thunder into an empty site** - The ult takes 8 points. Don't waste it unless you know enemies are in the area.
5. **Not playing with walls** - Breach's entire kit works through walls. If you're standing in the open using his abilities, you're playing him wrong.
6. **Solo play** - Breach without team follow-up is useless. His flashes and concusses need teammates to capitalize.

### Combo Plays
- **Double flash + duelist swing**: Flash through wall, duelist swings. Flash again, duelist swings a different angle. Two free peeks.
- **Fault Line + Aftershock**: Fault Line down a corridor to concuss, then Aftershock the corner they retreat to. Enemies have nowhere to go.
- **Rolling Thunder + team execute**: Ult into site, entire team pushes while enemies are knocked up. The 6-second concuss gives your team time to plant and set up post-plant.
- **Breach + Sova**: Sova Recon reveals positions, Breach Aftershocks those exact positions. Guaranteed damage or displacement.
- **Breach + Raze**: Breach flash, Raze satchels in while enemies are blind. One of the most oppressive entry combos.

### Pro Player Styles
- Breach sees consistent pro play on Fracture and Split. Teams use him as the primary initiator when wall utility has maximum value. His flashes are considered the most reliable entry tool in the game when coordinated.

## Skye

### Abilities Breakdown
- **Trailblazer (C)** - 250 creds. Pilotable Tasmanian tiger that scouts ahead and can leap to concuss enemies in a small area. 100 HP. Scouting + concuss in one ability. Duration ~6 seconds.
- **Guiding Light (Q)** - 250 creds each, 2 charges. Controllable hawk flash that can be curved by moving the mouse. Pops on reactivation or after max travel. If the hawk flashes an enemy, Skye gets an audio confirmation ("Blinded!"). The audio confirmation is unique info.
- **Regrowth (E)** - Free, can channel to heal all allies in line of sight. Does NOT heal Skye. Healing pool of 100 HP total. Channel can be broken and resumed. The only AoE heal in the game besides Sage's ult.
- **Seekers (X)** - 7 ult points. Releases 3 seekers that track the 3 nearest enemies. If a seeker reaches its target, the enemy is near-sighted for 3 seconds. Seekers have 150 HP each and can be shot. Use for post-plant info, retake info, or execute scouting.

### Info Gathering
- **Guiding Light flash confirmation**: When the hawk flashes an enemy, Skye hears "Blinded!" This is free info -- you know an enemy is in that direction even without direct vision.
- **Trailblazer scouting**: The tiger provides direct visual scouting. Use it to clear corners and common positions before your team pushes.
- **Seekers as info**: The seekers track enemy positions. Watch which direction they travel to determine enemy locations. Even if enemies shoot them, the direction tells you where they are.
- **Flash-and-push info**: Throw Guiding Light into a site. If you hear "Blinded," your team pushes. If no confirmation, the area may be clear or enemies looked away.

### Flash Techniques
- **Curved flashes**: Guiding Light can be curved around corners by moving the mouse while the hawk flies. This creates popflashes that enemies cannot react to.
- **Short flash**: Release the hawk and immediately reactivate for a close-range flash. Useful for close-quarter peeks.
- **Long flash**: Let the hawk fly further before popping to flash deep positions. Useful for clearing back-site angles.
- **Double flash**: Both Guiding Lights in rapid succession for a double-blind entry. The confirmation from the first flash tells you which angle to swing for the second.
- **Defensive flash**: Fly the hawk backward (toward attackers) and pop. This is counterintuitive but extremely effective for retakes.

### When to Pick Skye
- Your team needs both flashes and healing utility
- The map rewards curved flashes and scouting (most maps work)
- You want an initiator with strong retake potential (Seekers + flashes)
- Your composition lacks a healer but also needs an initiator

### Map Strengths
- **Ascent**: A-tier. Curved flashes through A main, B main. Trailblazer through Mid. Regrowth sustains the team in Mid fights.
- **Haven**: A-tier. Three sites mean more flash and scouting demand. Seekers reveal spread-out enemies.
- **Bind**: A-tier. Curved flashes through Hookah, Short. Trailblazer through teleporters.
- **Split**: A-tier. Tight corridors for curved flashes. Trailblazer through A main/B main.
- **Lotus**: A-tier. Flash through rotating doors. Seekers track enemies across three sites.
- **Sunset**: A-tier. Curved flashes down Mid, both mains. Healing sustains Mid control fights.
- **Breeze**: B-tier. Large open areas reduce curved flash value. Trailblazer gets shot at distance.
- **Icebox**: B-tier. Some flash value but Sova/Fade provide better info on this map.

### Common Mistakes
1. **Not curving flashes** - A straight Guiding Light is dodgeable. Always curve it around corners.
2. **Trailblazer too early** - The tiger is loud and telegraphed. Use it right before an execute, not 30 seconds early.
3. **Healing instead of flashing** - Regrowth heals allies but Skye cannot shoot while channeling. In a fight, flashes are more valuable than healing. Heal between fights, not during.
4. **Ignoring flash confirmation** - The "Blinded!" callout is free info. Call it to your team: "One flashed A Main."
5. **Seekers on eco rounds** - The ult costs 7 points. Don't pop it on rounds you're likely to lose anyway. Save it for gun rounds or critical moments.
6. **Self-healing attempts** - Regrowth does NOT heal Skye. Don't waste channel time expecting a self-heal.

### Combo Plays
- **Skye flash + Jett dash**: Flash into site, Jett dashes in while enemies are blind. The flash confirmation tells Jett exactly where to aim.
- **Trailblazer + team push**: Tiger scouts, concusses an enemy, team swings on the concussed player.
- **Seekers + team execute**: Pop Seekers before the execute. They force enemies to shoot (giving away positions) or get near-sighted (free kills).
- **Skye + Breach**: Double initiator with maximum flash output. Skye curved flash + Breach through-wall flash in rapid succession is almost unplayable for defenders.

### Pro Player Styles
- Skye is one of the most-picked initiators in VCT. Her combination of flashes, scouting, and healing makes her the highest-value initiator on most maps.
- **Boaster (Fnatic)**: IGL who plays Skye for info control. Uses Guiding Light primarily for information (the "Blinded" callout) rather than pure flash. His Trailblazer usage is conservative and calculated.

## KAY/O

### Abilities Breakdown
- **FRAG/MENT (C)** - 200 creds. Throwable explosive that sticks to the ground and pulses, dealing damage in a radius. Each pulse deals increasing damage (25/50/75/100). Enemies in the center take full damage across all 4 pulses (250 total). Use for area denial and clearing corners.
- **FLASH/DRIVE (Q)** - 250 creds each, 2 charges. Standard flash grenade. Right-click throws a short, fast pop flash (1-second cook time). Left-click throws a longer, high-arc flash (1.6-second cook time). The flash pops automatically -- no manual detonation needed.
- **ZERO/POINT (E)** - Free, 40-second cooldown. Throwable suppression blade that sticks to surfaces and suppresses all enemies in a large radius for 8 seconds. Suppressed enemies cannot use abilities. The blade pulses after a delay, and suppressed enemies are revealed on the minimap.
- **NULL/CMD (X)** - 8 ult points. KAY/O overloads, gaining a combat stim and continuously suppressing enemies in a large radius around him. If KAY/O is "killed" during NULL/CMD, he enters a downed state and can be revived by teammates (like Phoenix Run It Back but with team assistance). The suppression aura is the most disruptive ult in the game.

### Info Gathering
- **ZERO/POINT as info**: When the suppression blade suppresses enemies, their count appears on screen ("X ENEMIES SUPPRESSED"). This reveals how many enemies are in the area.
- **Audio cues**: ZERO/POINT emits a distinct sound when it suppresses someone. Listen for the count to determine enemy stack.
- **FRAG/MENT for corner clearing**: If enemies run from FRAG/MENT, their footsteps give away their position.

### Flash Techniques
- **Right-click popflash**: The short throw is KAY/O's bread and butter. It pops in ~1 second with minimal travel distance, making it one of the best popflashes in the game. Master the right-click flash.
- **Left-click long flash**: Throw over walls and buildings for deep flashes. The arc allows flashing over A Main walls on Ascent, B Main walls on Bind, etc.
- **Underhand bounce flash**: Right-click the flash off the ground so it bounces and pops at head height around a corner. This is advanced but extremely effective.
- **Double flash protocol**: Right-click flash for close angle, left-click flash for deep angle. Cover two spots with two flashes in rapid succession.

### When to Pick KAY/O
- The enemy relies heavily on abilities (Viper wall, Killjoy setup, Raze utility, Chamber escape)
- The map has tight chokes where FRAG/MENT and ZERO/POINT cover large areas
- Your team needs an initiator who can also frag (KAY/O plays like a duelist hybrid)
- You want to neutralize the enemy's utility advantage

### Map Strengths
- **Ascent**: S-tier. ZERO/POINT through Mid doors suppresses the entire site. FLASH/DRIVE over A main wall. FRAG/MENT A Dice, B Logs.
- **Split**: A-tier. ZERO/POINT down A Ramps suppresses Heaven. FLASH/DRIVE through B main.
- **Haven**: A-tier. ZERO/POINT from Garage suppresses C site. FRAG/MENT C Long.
- **Bind**: A-tier. ZERO/POINT through B walls. Tight corridors for FRAG/MENT.
- **Sunset**: A-tier. Mid control with ZERO/POINT. FLASH/DRIVE through tight angles.
- **Fracture**: A-tier. ZERO/POINT from B main into site. FRAG/MENT Arcade.
- **Lotus**: B-tier. Open areas reduce ZERO/POINT suppression density.
- **Breeze**: B-tier. Large map but ZERO/POINT on A Hall or B Tunnel has value.

### Common Mistakes
1. **ZERO/POINT at the wrong time** - Throw it BEFORE the execute, not after. The 8-second suppression should cover your team's entry window.
2. **Right-click flash at long range** - Right-click is for close range. It drops fast. Use left-click for distance.
3. **NULL/CMD without team support** - If you get downed, teammates need to be near enough to revive you. Don't ult solo across the map.
4. **FRAG/MENT as a molly** - It's not a molly. It does increasing damage per pulse. Enemies who leave after the first pulse take little damage. Use it on positions where enemies are trapped.
5. **Not counting suppressed enemies** - The "X ENEMIES SUPPRESSED" text is critical info. Call it immediately: "3 suppressed B site."
6. **Flashing teammates** - KAY/O flashes blind everyone. Communicate timing.

### Combo Plays
- **ZERO/POINT + team execute**: Suppress site, removing all defensive utility (Killjoy turret, Cypher traps, Sage wall), then push.
- **NULL/CMD entry**: Ult and run into site. The suppression aura disables all enemy abilities. Your team follows while enemies are utility-less.
- **KAY/O + Raze**: KAY/O flash, Raze satchels in. KAY/O ZERO/POINT removes enemy escape abilities (Jett dash, Chamber TP).
- **FRAG/MENT + Breach Aftershock**: Stack damage on a corner. One hits them from above, the other through the wall.

### Pro Player Styles
- KAY/O has become a staple pick in VCT 2024-2025. His suppression counters utility-heavy metas.
- Teams use NULL/CMD as the primary execute tool on maps like Ascent and Split, pushing behind the suppression aura.

## Fade

### Abilities Breakdown
- **Prowler (C)** - 250 creds each, 2 charges. Deployable creature that travels forward, locking onto enemies or Fade's trail marks. If it reaches an enemy, it near-sights them for 2.75 seconds. Can be steered by looking in the desired direction. Has 100 HP.
- **Seize (Q)** - 200 creds, 1 charge. Throwable orb that tethers and decays enemies caught in its area. Tethered enemies cannot escape the radius without breaking the tether (which takes time). Decay reduces health temporarily.
- **Haunt (E)** - Free, 40-second cooldown. Throwable eye that reveals enemies in line of sight and marks them with a trail. The trail lingers for 12 seconds, allowing Prowlers to track them. Can be destroyed (1 HP). This is Fade's primary info ability.
- **Nightfall (X)** - 7 ult points. Sends a wave of nightmare energy in a cone. Enemies hit are deafened, decayed, and trailed for 12 seconds. The trail allows Prowlers to lock on. The deafen effect removes all audio, making it extremely disorienting.

### Info Gathering
- **Haunt as primary scanner**: Functions like Sova's Recon Bolt but with trail marks. Throw Haunt high to scan a wide area. Enemies are revealed AND trailed, giving persistent tracking.
- **Prowler for direct scouting**: Send Prowlers into areas to check for enemies. They auto-lock onto enemies or trails, confirming positions. Use them like Skye's Trailblazer but with a blind instead of concuss.
- **Trail tracking**: When Haunt or Nightfall marks enemies with trails, Prowlers automatically seek those trails. This creates a chain of info that keeps giving.
- **Seize for position confirmation**: If enemies are Seized, you know their exact location. The tether animation is visible through walls.

### Flash Techniques
- Fade has no traditional flash. Prowler's near-sight is her pseudo-blind. It lasts 2.75 seconds and prevents enemies from seeing beyond close range.

### When to Pick Fade
- The map has corners and positions that Haunt can scan effectively
- Your team needs an initiator with strong retake utility (Nightfall + Prowlers)
- You want to track enemy movements over time (trail marks)
- The enemy plays lurk-heavy or off-angle styles that Prowler tracking counters

### Map Strengths
- **Lotus**: S-tier. Haunt covers wide sites. Prowlers through rotating doors. Seize in tight corridors.
- **Pearl**: A-tier. Haunt scans long corridors. Nightfall through B Long is devastating.
- **Ascent**: A-tier. Haunt over A main or B main. Prowler through Mid.
- **Haven**: A-tier. Haunt covers each site well. Nightfall on C push.
- **Bind**: A-tier. Haunt Hookah/Short. Prowler through teleporter.
- **Sunset**: A-tier. Tight Mid for Seize and Prowler. Haunt scans both sites.
- **Icebox**: B-tier. Haunt on A site works well. B site is tighter for Prowler pathing.
- **Breeze**: B-tier. Open areas make Haunt easy to shoot but scan area is large.

### Common Mistakes
1. **Haunt too low** - Like Sova's dart, a low Haunt is destroyed instantly. Throw it high and let it deploy from an angle enemies can't easily shoot.
2. **Prowler without trail marks** - An unguided Prowler travels in a straight line and is easy to dodge. Use Prowlers AFTER Haunt or Nightfall trails for guided tracking.
3. **Seize in open areas** - The tether radius is small. Use it in tight spaces where enemies cannot easily escape.
4. **Nightfall at long range** - The cone narrows with distance. Use Nightfall at medium range for maximum coverage.
5. **Not following up Prowler blinds** - If Prowler near-sights an enemy, your team has 2.75 seconds of advantage. Push immediately.

### Combo Plays
- **Haunt + Prowler chain**: Haunt marks enemies, Prowlers track the marks. This is Fade's core combo. Always use them in sequence.
- **Nightfall + team execute**: Nightfall deafens, decays, and trails. The deafen effect alone makes retakes nearly impossible for defenders. Follow immediately with a full push.
- **Seize + Raze Paint Shells**: Seize tethers enemies in place, Paint Shells hit the tethered location. Guaranteed damage.
- **Fade + Breach**: Fade Haunt reveals, Breach Aftershock hits the revealed position. If they run, Prowler chases them.

### Pro Player Styles
- Fade has become one of the top-picked initiators in VCT, rivaling Sova on many maps. Her combination of info + debuff utility provides more aggressive value than Sova's pure info kit.

## Gekko

### Abilities Breakdown
- **Mosh Pit (C)** - 150 creds. Throwable creature that explodes after a delay, creating an area of damage. Deals 150 damage over its duration. Use for post-plant and area denial. The creature can be thrown over walls.
- **Wingman (Q)** - 300 creds, recollectable. Sends a creature that flashes enemies in a cone at the first enemy it sees. Can also plant or defuse the spike when targeting the spike location. Wingman has 100 HP. The plant/defuse ability is unique and extremely valuable.
- **Dizzy (E)** - Free, recollectable. Sends a floating creature that blinds enemies it spots. The blind applies to all enemies in Dizzy's line of sight for 1.75 seconds. Dizzy has 100 HP and can be shot. Recollect the globule after use.
- **Thrash (X)** - 7 ult points, recollectable. Pilotable creature that can be steered into enemies. On contact, detains all enemies in a radius for 6 seconds (unable to move or shoot). Recollectable after each use.

### Info Gathering
- **Dizzy as a scanner**: Dizzy flies forward and scans. If enemies are in its LOS, they get blinded AND you know their position. Like Skye's hawk but with a different debuff.
- **Wingman scouting**: Send Wingman into an area. If it flashes, enemies are there. If it doesn't, the area is likely clear.
- **Thrash scouting**: While piloting Thrash, you see through its perspective. Use it for scouting before committing the detain.

### Flash Techniques
- **Dizzy as flash**: Dizzy blinds enemies in its LOS. Throw it over walls or around corners so enemies cannot shoot it before the blind triggers.
- **Wingman flash**: Wingman's cone flash triggers on the first enemy it sees. Send it around corners for a hands-free flash while you hold an angle.

### When to Pick Gekko
- You want an initiator whose utility is recollectable (maximum utility cycles per round)
- The map has post-plant positions where Mosh Pit lineups work (elevated positions for throws)
- You want a Wingman-plant capability (plant spike from safety)
- Your team needs an initiator who can also hold sites with Thrash on defense

### Map Strengths
- **Lotus**: S-tier. Wingman through doors, Mosh Pit lineups, Dizzy over walls. Thrash through corridors.
- **Sunset**: A-tier. Tight mid for Wingman. Mosh Pit post-plant lineups.
- **Bind**: A-tier. Wingman through teleporters. Mosh Pit Hookah/Short.
- **Split**: A-tier. Wingman A/B main. Dizzy over walls.
- **Haven**: B-tier. Three sites mean more recollection opportunities.
- **Pearl**: B-tier. Long corridors for Dizzy and Wingman.

### Common Mistakes
1. **Not recollecting utility** - Gekko's creatures leave behind globules that can be picked up for reuse. ALWAYS recollect. This is free extra utility.
2. **Wingman-plant in dangerous positions** - Wingman can plant the spike, but if you send it to an unsafe position, it gets destroyed before planting.
3. **Mosh Pit without lineup** - Random Mosh Pits are dodgeable. Learn lineups that cover default plant positions.
4. **Thrash from unsafe position** - Like Sova's drone, you're vulnerable while piloting Thrash. Get covered.
5. **Dizzy in the open** - Dizzy has 100 HP and is slow. Don't throw it into open areas where it gets shot instantly.

### Combo Plays
- **Wingman plant + team cover**: Send Wingman to plant while your team holds angles. Safest plant method in the game.
- **Thrash detain + team push**: Thrash detains for 6 seconds. Your team has an eternity to push and plant.
- **Dizzy + duelist entry**: Dizzy blinds, duelist enters. Similar to Skye hawk + entry combo.
- **Mosh Pit + Brimstone Incendiary**: Double post-plant denial. Stack Mosh Pit and molly on spike for maximum denial time.

## Tejo

### Abilities Breakdown
- **Tejo** is an initiator added to the game with a kit centered on targeted demolition and area control.
- His abilities focus on marking and exposing enemy positions through seismic detection.
- His signature ability sends a targeted projectile that reveals and damages enemies in a marked area, combining info gathering with aggression.
- His ultimate creates a massive destabilization zone that forces enemies out of cover and reveals their positions.

### When to Pick Tejo
- Maps with enclosed spaces where his area denial tools have maximum coverage
- When the enemy plays tight stacks that can be disrupted with his AoE reveal
- When your team needs an initiator who combines damage with info

### Map Strengths
- Strong on maps with defined chokepoints and enclosed site areas
- Effective on Bind, Split, and Sunset where tight spaces amplify his AoE

### Common Mistakes
1. **Using reveal abilities without team follow-up** - His reveals are only valuable if the team acts on the info.
2. **Over-aggressive positioning** - Despite his damage potential, he's an initiator, not a duelist.
3. **Wasting ultimate on speculative areas** - Confirm enemy presence before committing his expensive ult.

## Miks

### Abilities Breakdown
- **Miks** is one of the newer initiator additions, bringing a unique support-oriented approach to the role.
- Her kit focuses on creating advantageous conditions for her team through zone control and targeted disruption.
- She features abilities that mark and track enemies who enter designated zones, providing persistent info.
- Her ultimate creates a field that amplifies teammate damage while debuffing enemies, making her ideal for coordinated executes.

### When to Pick Miks
- When your team runs coordinated executes and needs a force multiplier
- Maps where zone control at chokepoints is critical
- When you need an initiator who enhances team damage rather than displacing enemies

### Map Strengths
- Best on maps with well-defined site entries (Ascent, Haven, Pearl)
- Effective where sustained zone control matters more than one-shot info gathering

### Common Mistakes
1. **Playing solo** - Miks amplifies team play. Solo plays waste her kit entirely.
2. **Zone placement without read** - Her zones need to be where enemies ARE, not where they MIGHT be. Get info first.
3. **Ulting defensively** - Her ult is an execute tool. Using it to hold a site wastes its offensive potential.

## General Initiator Philosophy at Radiant Level

### The Info Cycle
1. **Pre-round info**: Use Recon/Haunt/Drone at round start to determine enemy positions.
2. **Mid-round adjustment**: Based on initial info, adjust your approach. If enemies are stacking one site, hit the other.
3. **Execute info**: Right before the execute, scan the site with a second piece of utility. Confirm the number of defenders.
4. **Post-plant info**: Use remaining utility to track rotators and retakers.

### Flash Discipline
- Never flash your own team. At Radiant level, a team flash loses the round. The flashed teammate cannot trade.
- Call every flash: "Flashing A Main in 2 seconds." Give teammates time to look away.
- Vary flash timing. If you always flash at the same point in the execute, enemies learn to look away at that time.
- Pop flashes > full flashes. A flash that pops the instant enemies see it is 10x more valuable than a flash they can dodge.

### Initiator Economy
- Always buy full utility. An initiator without abilities is a worse duelist with no escape.
- On eco rounds, consider buying just abilities with a Classic. Your utility might win the round even without a gun.
- Prioritize recharge abilities (Sova Recon, Fade Haunt, KAY/O ZERO/POINT) as they provide free value over time.

### Double Initiator Meta
- Double initiator (e.g., Sova + Skye, Fade + KAY/O) is common in pro play. The first initiator gathers info, the second provides flashes and disruption for the entry.
- In double initiator comps, define roles: who scans first, who flashes for the duelist, who saves utility for post-plant.
- The info initiator (Sova/Fade) plays slightly behind the flash initiator (Skye/KAY/O/Breach).

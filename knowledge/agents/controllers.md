# Controllers - Radiant Coaching Knowledge Base

Controllers shape the battlefield through vision denial and area control. A good controller doesn't just smoke -- they dictate the pace of the round, enable executes, deny retakes, and control post-plant scenarios. At Radiant level, smoke timing and placement separate good controllers from great ones.

---

## Brimstone

### Abilities Breakdown
- **Stim Beacon (C)** - 100 creds, 2 charges. Deployable field that gives combat stim (increased fire rate, equip speed, reload speed, recoil recovery) to all allies in range. Lasts 12 seconds. Underrated at high elo -- the fire rate boost on Phantom and Spectre is noticeable.
- **Incendiary (Q)** - 250 creds. Grenade launcher molly that creates a fire zone. Deals 60 DPS. Lasts 8 seconds. One of the best post-plant abilities in the game. Can be bounced off walls for lineup executions.
- **Sky Smoke (E)** - Free, 3 charges. Opens a tactical map to place up to 3 smoke orbs anywhere within a large radius. Smokes last 19.25 seconds -- the longest-lasting smokes in the game. Instantaneous deployment. Cannot be placed at extreme distances.
- **Orbital Strike (X)** - 7 ult points. Calls down a massive orbital laser on a targeted area. Deals heavy damage over 4 seconds. Visible on the minimap before impact. Use for post-plant denial, retake denial, or forcing enemies off spike.

### Smoke Lineups Philosophy
- Brimstone smokes are the simplest to place but have the longest duration. This means you get the most "time of denial" per smoke of any controller.
- **Attack**: Place all 3 smokes simultaneously for a fast execute. The instant deployment catches defenders off guard compared to Omen/Astra smokes which have travel time.
- **Defense**: Hold 1-2 smokes for retake. Brimstone is the best retake smoker because he can smoke from safety without line of sight.
- **One-way smokes**: Brimstone can place smokes from the tactical map, making one-ways harder to set up than Omen or Viper. Focus on standard deep smokes rather than one-ways.

### Wall Usage
- Brimstone has no wall ability. His Sky Smokes serve as his vision denial. Compensate by stacking smokes in a line to create a pseudo-wall on wide chokes.

### When to Pick Brimstone
- The map is small enough for his smoke radius to cover all key positions (Bind, Fracture, Split)
- Your team needs reliable post-plant with Incendiary lineups
- You want the fastest execute smokes in the game (instant deployment)
- You are comfortable with lineup-based play

### Map Strengths
- **Bind**: S-tier. Small map, full smoke coverage from anywhere. Incendiary lineups for both sites are easy. Orbital Strike on Hookah or Short is devastating.
- **Fracture**: S-tier. Compact map, all smoke positions reachable. Post-plant lineups on both sites.
- **Split**: A-tier. Can smoke all chokes from safety. Incendiary lineups for Heaven on both sites.
- **Sunset**: A-tier. Mid control smokes, good post-plant lineups.
- **Lotus**: B-tier. Map is large but Brimstone can reach most smoke spots. The rotating doors complicate his lineups.
- **Breeze**: C-tier. Map is too large. Smoke radius doesn't reach B site from A site spawns.
- **Haven**: B-tier. Three sites strain his 3 smokes. Can't smoke all sites effectively.
- **Icebox**: B-tier. Playable but Viper is strictly better here.

### Common Mistakes
1. **Smoking too early** - Brimstone smokes last 19.25 seconds but if you smoke at 1:25 on the round timer, the smoke fades at 1:06 -- giving defenders time to push through before the execute.
2. **Using all 3 smokes on one site** - Save at least 1 smoke for post-plant or mid-round adjustments. Two smokes on site, one in reserve.
3. **Forgetting Incendiary lineups** - Brimstone without lineups is half an agent. Learn at least 3 post-plant lineups per map.
4. **Orbital Strike on empty positions** - The ult is telegraphed. Use it where enemies MUST stand (on spike, in choke points), not where they might be.
5. **Ignoring Stim Beacon** - 100 creds for a fire rate boost is efficient. Place it in common fight areas during executes, not randomly.
6. **Not smoking deep enough** - Shallow smokes let defenders play close and swing through. Smoke deep into the site so defenders are pushed back.

### Post-Plant Value
- Brimstone's Incendiary is a top-3 post-plant ability in the game. With lineups, you can deny defuse from complete safety.
- **Standard lineup protocol**: Plant spike in a default position, retreat to a lineup spot, throw Incendiary when defuse sound is heard. The 8-second duration means the defuser must either tank damage (lethal), stop defuse, or find you.
- Orbital Strike on spike during defuse forces a full reset. 7 ult points is expensive but guarantees the round if timed correctly.

## Viper

### Abilities Breakdown
- **Snake Bite (C)** - 200 creds each, 2 charges. Acid pool that deals 25 DPS and applies Vulnerable (double damage from all sources). Lasts 5.5 seconds. The Vulnerable debuff makes this extremely dangerous in combination with any damage source.
- **Poison Cloud (Q)** - 200 creds. Throwable gas emitter that creates a toxic smoke sphere. Can be toggled on/off. Uses fuel (shared with Toxic Screen). Decays enemy health when inside (up to 50 HP). The reusable smoke is Viper's defining utility.
- **Toxic Screen (E)** - Free. Deployable wall of toxic gas that spans a large distance. Can be toggled on/off. Uses fuel (shared with Poison Cloud). Decays enemy health. Place at round start; cannot be repositioned.
- **Viper's Pit (X)** - 7 ult points. Creates a massive toxic cloud around Viper. Enemies inside have reduced vision and take decay damage. Viper can see highlighted enemies. Lasts indefinitely while Viper remains inside (15-second grace period if she leaves). The strongest site-hold ultimate in the game.

### Smoke Lineups Philosophy
- Viper is unique: her smokes are toggleable and reusable. This means you control WHEN vision is denied, not just WHERE.
- **Fuel management**: Poison Cloud and Toxic Screen share a fuel bar. Running both simultaneously drains fuel in ~15 seconds. Toggle them alternately or in short bursts to conserve fuel.
- **One-way Poison Cloud**: Viper excels at one-way smokes. Place Poison Cloud on elevated surfaces (boxes, ledges, windowsills) so the bottom of the smoke sits above head height. You can see feet while enemies see nothing.
- **Toxic Screen placement**: The wall should cut the map in half, not just block one angle. Think in terms of map control division. A well-placed wall on Icebox B site splits the entire site in two.

### Wall Usage
- **Attack**: Place Toxic Screen to split the site, cutting off defenders' crossfire potential. On Breeze A, the wall can separate Hall from site. On Icebox B, split Kitchen from Yellow.
- **Defense**: Place Toxic Screen to slow rushes. On Bind B, wall across B Long forces attackers to push through it blind. Toggle it on when you hear footsteps.
- **Mid-round**: Toggle the wall off to bait enemies into crossing, then toggle it back on to isolate them.

### When to Pick Viper
- The map has large open areas that need splitting (Breeze, Icebox, Lotus)
- Your team needs post-plant denial (Snake Bite lineups are devastating)
- The map benefits from wall-based site splits
- You want to play a controller who can also anchor a site defensively with Viper's Pit

### Map Strengths
- **Breeze**: S-tier. Viper is mandatory on Breeze. Toxic Screen across A site or B site splits the map perfectly. One-way Poison Cloud on Mid Pillar. Snake Bite lineups on both sites.
- **Icebox**: S-tier. Toxic Screen across B site is the standard. Viper's Pit on A site is nearly unbreakable. One-way smokes on A Pipes.
- **Lotus**: A-tier. Wall across C site or A main. Strong post-plant with Snake Bite.
- **Pearl**: A-tier. Wall across B site or Mid. Viper's Pit on either site is extremely strong.
- **Haven**: A-tier. Wall from C to A cuts the map. Viper's Pit on C site forces full utility commitment to retake.
- **Bind**: B-tier. Playable but the map is small enough for other controllers. Wall across B site from Long.
- **Ascent**: B-tier. Some wall lineups work but Omen/Astra are generally preferred.
- **Split**: C-tier. Map is too narrow for Toxic Screen to provide unique value.

### Common Mistakes
1. **Running both Poison Cloud and Toxic Screen simultaneously for too long** - Your fuel drains in 15 seconds. Toggle strategically.
2. **Placing Toxic Screen after the round starts** - The wall takes time to deploy and is visible. Place it during buy phase at the barrier.
3. **Not learning Snake Bite lineups** - Snake Bite with Vulnerable debuff is lethal in post-plant. Without lineups, Viper loses half her value.
4. **Leaving Viper's Pit too long** - The 15-second grace period seems long but it goes fast. If you leave to rotate, your ult will expire.
5. **One-way Poison Cloud at the wrong height** - Too high and you see nothing. Too low and enemies see you. Practice the exact placement on each map.
6. **Not toggling wall for info** - Toggle Toxic Screen off for 1 second to get a visual read of enemy positions, then toggle it back on.

### Post-Plant Value
- Viper's Snake Bite is arguably the best post-plant ability in the game because of the Vulnerable debuff.
- **Double Snake Bite post-plant**: Plant, lineup one Snake Bite on defuse sound, immediately lineup the second. This covers approximately 11 seconds of defuse denial. The 7-second defuse requires the enemy to either tank lethal damage or wait out both mollies.
- **Viper's Pit post-plant**: Plant inside your Viper's Pit and the enemy must enter the pit to defuse. You have highlight advantage and they take decay damage. This is one of the most oppressive post-plant setups in the game.

## Omen

### Abilities Breakdown
- **Shrouded Step (C)** - 100 creds, 2 charges. Short-range teleport with a brief channeling animation. Audio cue at both departure and arrival points. Use for repositioning to off-angles, reaching elevated positions, or faking rotations.
- **Paranoia (Q)** - 250 creds. Sends a shadow projectile that travels through walls and near-sights enemies hit. Wide hitbox. Lasts 2.5 seconds. One of the strongest flashes for executing onto sites.
- **Dark Cover (E)** - Free, 2 charges, 30-second recharge. Smokable orbs that can be placed anywhere on the map (no range limit). Travel time varies by distance. Smokes are hollow -- you can hide inside them. Last 15 seconds.
- **From the Shadows (X)** - 7 ult points. Global teleport. Omen channels for several seconds and appears as a shadow at the target location. Can be cancelled during channel. Enemies can shoot the shadow to cancel the teleport. Use for flanks, rotations, and aggressive repositions.

### Smoke Lineups Philosophy
- Omen smokes have unlimited range but have travel time. The further the smoke, the longer enemies wait to see it deploy.
- **Elevated smokes**: Omen can place smokes at any height. This enables one-way smokes on any map by placing the smoke slightly above a ledge or surface.
- **Hollow smokes**: Omen's smokes are hollow spheres. Sitting inside them gives you vision of anyone who enters while they cannot see you. This is a high-risk, high-reward play at Radiant level because good players will spray through smokes.
- **Fake smokes**: Send a smoke to a location to imply a rotate, then hit the other site. The travel animation is visible and enemies will call "smoke going B."

### Wall Usage
- Omen has no wall ability. His smokes serve as his vision denial. Use Paranoia as a pseudo-wall during executes -- it near-sights a wide area.

### When to Pick Omen
- The map requires long-range smokes (any map, but especially Ascent, Haven, Lotus)
- You want a controller who can also frag and make plays (Shrouded Step aggression, From the Shadows flanks)
- Your team needs a controller with flash utility (Paranoia)
- You want one-way smoke versatility

### Map Strengths
- **Ascent**: S-tier. Dark Cover on A main, B main from any position. Shrouded Step onto A Heaven, B Garden. Paranoia through Mid.
- **Haven**: A-tier. Three sites mean more smoke demand but Omen's recharge handles it. From the Shadows for cross-map rotations.
- **Lotus**: A-tier. Smokes through doors, Shrouded Step through rotating doors for unexpected angles.
- **Split**: A-tier. One-way smokes on both sites. Shrouded Step onto Heaven. Paranoia through Ramps.
- **Bind**: A-tier. Smoke Hookah and Short simultaneously. Shrouded Step in teleporters for confusion.
- **Sunset**: A-tier. Mid control with smokes and Paranoia. Shrouded Step onto elevated positions.
- **Pearl**: A-tier. Deep smokes B Long, A main. Shrouded Step onto B Hall boxes.
- **Breeze**: B-tier. Playable but Viper provides more value with wall splits. Omen can be paired with Viper.
- **Icebox**: B-tier. Some one-way value but Viper is preferred.

### Common Mistakes
1. **Smoking too early or too late** - Omen smokes have travel time. Account for the ~2 seconds of travel when timing executes.
2. **Predictable Shrouded Step locations** - If you TP to the same spot every round, enemies pre-aim it. Vary your positions.
3. **From the Shadows in obvious locations** - The audio cue is global. Don't ult to a spot where enemies are already watching. Use it for deep flanks or to return to a site your team already cleared.
4. **Not using Paranoia aggressively** - Paranoia goes through walls and near-sights. Use it to initiate fights, not just defensively.
5. **Sitting inside smokes at Radiant level** - Good players spray common smoke positions. Don't do this against players who know the play.
6. **Forgetting to recharge smokes** - Omen's smokes recharge every 30 seconds. If you used both early, check your recharge timer before the next execute.

### Post-Plant Value
- Omen's post-plant value comes from repositioning, not utility denial. Use Shrouded Step to take off-angles that watch the spike from unexpected positions.
- From the Shadows can be used post-plant to teleport behind retaking enemies. Extremely high risk but can win rounds solo.
- Paranoia through walls to blind retaking enemies is reliable and safe.

## Astra

### Abilities Breakdown
- **Gravity Well (C)** - 1 star. Places a star that, when activated, pulls enemies toward its center and applies Vulnerable. 2.75 second delay before activation. Covers a large area.
- **Nova Pulse (Q)** - 1 star. Places a star that, when activated, concusses all enemies in range after a brief delay. Enemies are concussed for 4 seconds. Strong for stopping executes and clearing positions.
- **Nebula (E)** - 1 star, 25-second cooldown after dissipation. Activates a star as a smoke. Lasts 14.25 seconds. Dissipating a star leaves a brief smoke in its place.
- **Cosmic Divide (X)** - 7 ult points. Creates a massive wall that spans the entire map. Blocks bullets and heavily dampens audio. Lasts 21 seconds. The ultimate map control tool.

### Smoke Lineups Philosophy
- Astra places stars during the buy phase or in astral form, then activates them as smokes. This means she pre-plans her smoke positions.
- **Star economy**: Astra has 5 stars total with recall ability. Plan your star placement for the entire round, not just the first 30 seconds.
- **Recall baiting**: Recalling a star creates a brief smoke effect. Use this to bait enemies into thinking you're smoking a site, then activate the real smoke elsewhere.
- **Global coverage**: Like Omen, Astra can smoke anywhere on the map. Unlike Omen, her smokes are pre-placed and instant on activation. This makes her the best controller for reactive play.

### Wall Usage
- **Cosmic Divide** is the strongest wall in the game. It blocks bullets AND audio. Use it to split the map for executes, deny retakes, or isolate bomb sites.
- **Attack**: Place Cosmic Divide to cut off rotations. On Haven, wall between B and C to prevent fast rotations. The wall doesn't block movement, so enemies can walk through but they cannot shoot or hear.
- **Defense**: Use Cosmic Divide to split attackers. If they push A, wall off A from Mid so they cannot support from Mid.

### When to Pick Astra
- Your team runs structured executes with high coordination (pre-planned strats)
- The map benefits from global utility placement (Haven, Lotus, Pearl)
- Your IGL needs a controller who can make reactive mid-round calls
- You face teams with predictable executes that Gravity Well and Nova Pulse can punish

### Map Strengths
- **Haven**: A-tier. Three sites benefit from global star placement. Cosmic Divide between any two sites is game-changing.
- **Pearl**: A-tier. Long corridors for Gravity Well and Nova Pulse. Cosmic Divide splits Mid from sites.
- **Lotus**: A-tier. Stars through rotating doors. Cosmic Divide across any site.
- **Ascent**: A-tier. Mid control with stars. Cosmic Divide A to B.
- **Breeze**: B-tier. Large map but stars cover it. Viper still generally preferred.
- **Split**: B-tier. Map is narrow, reducing Cosmic Divide value. Omen preferred.

### Common Mistakes
1. **Placing all stars pre-round and not adjusting** - Recall stars and reposition them mid-round based on info. Static star placement is wasteful.
2. **Using Gravity Well without follow-up damage** - The pull means nothing if no one shoots the pulled enemies. Coordinate with teammates.
3. **Cosmic Divide too early** - The wall lasts 21 seconds. If you use it at round start, it fades before the execute.
4. **Not using recall smoke** - Recalling a star creates a brief smoke. Use this for free, extra vision denial.
5. **Spending too long in Astral Form** - You are completely vulnerable during Astral Form. Only enter it when safe and for minimal time.
6. **Nova Pulse without timing** - The concuss has a delay. If you activate it when enemies are already through, they dodge it. Predict their timing.

### Post-Plant Value
- Gravity Well on spike during defuse pulls the defuser off the spike. Extremely effective.
- Nova Pulse on spike during defuse concusses the defuser for 4 seconds, enough time for your team to swing.
- Cosmic Divide between spike and the retake path forces enemies to push through without audio cues.

## Harbor

### Abilities Breakdown
- **Cascade (C)** - 150 creds. Moving water wall that travels forward, slowing enemies it passes through. Use for site entries and pushing through chokes.
- **Cove (Q)** - 350 creds. Deployable water shield dome that blocks bullets. Lasts 13 seconds or until destroyed (HP-based). The only smoke in the game that blocks bullets. Use to plant spike safely or hold angles.
- **High Tide (E)** - Free, 40-second cooldown. Long water wall that can be bent by moving the mouse while casting. Slows enemies who pass through. This is Harbor's primary vision denial tool.
- **Reckoning (X)** - 7 ult points. Creates a geyser field in a large area. Geysers successively strike enemies, concussing them. Lasts several seconds. Use for site takes and retake denial.

### Smoke Lineups Philosophy
- Harbor doesn't use traditional smokes. His vision denial comes from water walls (High Tide, Cascade) and Cove.
- **High Tide as a smoke wall**: Think of High Tide like Viper's Toxic Screen but shorter duration and repositionable. Bend it to cover exactly the angles you need.
- **Cove as a plant smoke**: Cove's bullet-blocking dome is uniquely powerful for planting. Place it over the spike plant location for guaranteed safe plants.
- **Cascade for entry**: The moving wall pushes forward, providing rolling cover as you enter a site.

### Wall Usage
- **High Tide (Primary Wall)**: Bend this wall to cover multiple angles simultaneously. On Lotus, curve it from A Main to A Tree to block two angles with one ability.
- **Cascade (Secondary Wall)**: Use as a pushing wall that moves with you. It slows enemies who touch it, giving you a speed advantage.
- **Combining walls**: Use High Tide to block long angles and Cascade to push through close angles. This two-wall approach is unique to Harbor.

### When to Pick Harbor
- Your team runs aggressive, fast executes that need rolling cover
- The map has wide sites where traditional smokes don't cover enough (Lotus, Pearl)
- You need a guaranteed safe plant with Cove
- You want to pair with another controller (Harbor + Viper on Breeze/Icebox)

### Map Strengths
- **Lotus**: A-tier. High Tide bends around site, Cascade through doors, Cove for safe plants.
- **Pearl**: A-tier. High Tide covers wide B site, Cove for safe B plants. Reckoning on Mid.
- **Haven**: B-tier. Can work on any of the three sites with wall bends.
- **Split**: B-tier. Cascade A main, High Tide across Mid.
- **Breeze**: A-tier. Paired with Viper, Harbor covers what Viper's wall doesn't.

### Common Mistakes
1. **Using Cove for vision denial instead of bullet blocking** - Cove costs 350 creds. Use it for plants and critical holds, not as a regular smoke.
2. **High Tide without bending** - A straight wall is predictable and covers one angle. Always bend it to maximize coverage.
3. **Cascade without follow-up** - The moving wall only slows enemies briefly. Push with it or it's wasted utility.
4. **Not pairing with another controller** - Harbor's vision denial is short-duration. On most maps, he needs a co-controller (Viper, Omen) for adequate smoke coverage.
5. **Reckoning in empty areas** - The ult is best used where you know enemies are stacking. Don't waste it on speculative areas.

### Post-Plant Value
- Cove on spike post-plant forces enemies to destroy the dome before defusing -- buying your team ~3 seconds.
- High Tide between spike and retake path provides vision denial.
- Reckoning on spike post-plant concusses anyone trying to defuse.

## Clove

### Abilities Breakdown
- **Pick-Me-Up (C)** - 100 creds. After getting a kill or assist, Clove can activate to gain combat stim and a temporary overshield. Lasts 10 seconds. Encourages aggressive play and is usable after death for a limited time.
- **Meddle (Q)** - 250 creds. Throwable projectile that decays enemies in its area. Reduces maximum health temporarily. Use to weaken enemies before a push or during retakes.
- **Ruse (E)** - Free, 30-second cooldown. Places a smoke from the map view (like Brimstone). Can be used while dead. Lasts 13.5 seconds. This is the defining Clove feature -- smokes from beyond the grave.
- **Not Dead Yet (X)** - 7 ult points. After dying, Clove can self-revive. Must get a kill or damaging assist within 12 seconds or die permanently. Revives with decayed health.

### Smoke Lineups Philosophy
- Clove's Ruse smokes function like Brimstone's Sky Smoke -- placed from a tactical map with instant deployment.
- **Post-death smoking**: Clove is the only controller who can smoke after dying. This means a dead Clove still provides value. Your team should plan around this -- even if Clove dies first, the smokes still come.
- **Aggressive smoking**: Because Clove can smoke while dead, they can afford to play aggressively. Die on entry, then smoke for your team from the grave.
- **Two smokes**: Clove has 2 smoke charges. Use them tactically -- you don't have the 3 smokes of Brimstone.

### Wall Usage
- Clove has no wall. Compensate with Meddle for area denial and Ruse smokes for vision block.

### When to Pick Clove
- Your team needs a controller who can also entry or play aggressively
- You want smoke reliability even if the controller dies early
- The team composition benefits from a hybrid controller-duelist playstyle
- You want post-death utility value

### Map Strengths
- **Sunset**: A-tier. Aggressive mid play with Pick-Me-Up sustain. Post-death smokes for retakes.
- **Ascent**: A-tier. Aggressive B main play. Post-death smokes for team.
- **Bind**: A-tier. Aggressive short/hookah plays. Not Dead Yet for second chances.
- **Split**: B-tier. Tight corridors favor Clove's aggressive style.
- **Haven**: B-tier. Three sites strain 2 smokes but post-death smoking helps.

### Common Mistakes
1. **Playing Clove like a traditional controller** - Clove's kit rewards aggression. Don't sit in the back and smoke.
2. **Not using post-death smokes** - If you die, immediately open the map and place your remaining smokes. This is Clove's unique value.
3. **Not Dead Yet without a plan** - You revive with low health and need a kill in 12 seconds. Know where enemies are before ulting.
4. **Meddle without follow-up** - The decay is strong but useless if no one capitalizes. Meddle before a team push.
5. **Wasting Pick-Me-Up** - The combat stim and overshield require a kill/assist trigger. Don't chase kills just to activate it.

### Post-Plant Value
- Clove's post-plant is strong because even death doesn't stop their smokes. Plant, die on post-plant, smoke from death to deny defuse vision.
- Not Dead Yet post-plant can be game-winning. Die near spike, revive, and contest the defuse.
- Meddle on spike area weakens defusers who must commit to the position.

## General Controller Philosophy at Radiant Level

### Smoke Timing Rules
1. **Smoke on contact, not on setup** - Smoke when your team is ready to push, not 10 seconds before. Early smokes fade before the execute.
2. **Stagger smokes on defense** - Don't smoke everything at once. Smoke one angle, wait for it to fade, smoke another. This extends your denial time.
3. **Smoke refresh** - If your smokes recharge (Omen, Clove), plan your first smoke usage around the recharge timer. Use smoke 1, recharge, use smoke 2, recharge, use smoke 1 again.
4. **One-way timing** - One-way smokes have a window of ~3-4 seconds before enemies adjust. Use that window aggressively, then reposition.

### Controller Economy
- Controllers should always buy their full utility kit. A controller without smokes is a team without a controller.
- On eco rounds, prioritize ability purchases over guns. A Brimstone with smokes and a Classic is more valuable than a Brimstone with a Spectre and no smokes.
- Viper's Poison Cloud (200) and Toxic Screen (free) make her the most eco-friendly controller.

### Double Controller Meta
- Maps like Breeze and Icebox often run double controller (Viper + Omen/Harbor). This provides wall + smoke coverage that single controllers cannot match.
- When running double controller, coordinate who smokes where. Overlapping smokes is wasted utility.
- In double controller comps, one controller plays "anchor" (usually Viper) and the other plays "flex" (Omen/Harbor).

### Controller Communication
- Call "smoking [location]" before placing smokes so teammates know when to push.
- Call "smoke fading [location]" 3 seconds before smoke dissipates so teammates can prepare.
- Call "no smokes" when you are out of charges so the team knows to play safe.
- Call "wall up" and "wall down" for Viper/Harbor toggles.

### Post-Plant Controller Role
- Controllers are the most important agents in post-plant scenarios. Your ability to deny defuse with smokes, mollies, and walls directly wins rounds.
- Always save at least one piece of utility for post-plant. A controller who uses everything pre-plant failed at resource management.
- Learn every lineup on your main. Post-plant lineups are not optional at Radiant level.

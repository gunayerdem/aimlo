# AGENT: Veto

> **PATCH-SENSITIVE NOTE**: Veto is a newer sentinel agent. His ability values, cooldowns, and interactions may change significantly between patches. Always verify current patch notes before applying coaching advice. The strategic concepts below remain valid, but specific numbers and timings should be cross-referenced with the latest game data.

## 1. Role Identity
Veto is the information-denial sentinel. While Cypher gathers intelligence and Killjoy controls space through damage, Veto's entire kit is built around making the enemy team blind and uncertain. He does not just defend sites — he makes enemy information-gathering unreliable. His abilities interfere with enemy recon, mask his team's movements, and create conditions where attackers must push into a site without knowing what awaits them. Veto is the anti-initiator: he directly counters the information-gathering tools that modern compositions rely on.

## 2. Core Responsibilities
- **Information denial on defense**: Veto's primary job is ensuring the enemy team cannot safely gather intel about his site. Their drones, darts, and utility should return unreliable or no information.
- **Recon interference**: His signature ability disrupts enemy reconnaissance tools. When an enemy Sova fires a recon dart or Fade sends a prowler, Veto's interference can render these efforts wasted.
- **Movement masking**: Veto can mask his team's rotation sounds and visual signatures, allowing defensive rotations that would normally be punished by attentive attackers.
- **Defensive deception**: His kit creates ambiguity about how many defenders are on a site and where they are positioned. This forces attackers to over-commit utility to basic information gathering.
- **Anti-default play**: Veto is strongest against teams that play methodical defaults, gathering info before executing. He forces them to execute blind or waste their entire util budget on basic recon.

## 3. Common Mistakes
- Using information denial abilities before the enemy has actually deployed their recon, wasting the effect window.
- Focusing entirely on denial and forgetting that Veto still needs to hold a site with gunplay and positioning.
- Not communicating to teammates which enemy abilities have been disrupted — the team cannot capitalize on denied information if they do not know it happened.
- Using movement masking for rotations that are not actually being contested, wasting cooldowns on unnecessary deception.
- Playing too aggressively because he believes the enemy is blind — denial does not mean safety, it means uncertainty.
- Over-relying on his anti-recon tools and neglecting basic sentinel fundamentals like crossfire positioning.

## 4. Pattern to Meaning

**IF** Veto uses interference abilities but enemy still executes confidently onto site
**MEANING** Either interference timing is wrong (used after recon already completed) or the enemy is executing without relying on recon at all.
**FIX** If timing: teach the player to hold interference until they hear/see the recon ability deploy. If enemy rushes without recon: Veto's denial kit is less valuable and he should focus on gunplay and positioning.

**IF** Veto's team consistently gets caught during rotations despite movement masking
**MEANING** Masking is either not being used during rotations, or enemies are gathering information through means that masking does not cover (e.g., visual contact, process of elimination).
**FIX** Movement masking helps against audio-based reads. If the enemy has visual control of rotation paths, masking alone is insufficient — the rotation path itself needs to change.

**IF** Enemies consistently over-invest utility just to gather basic site information
**MEANING** Veto is working as intended. The enemy is burning 2-3 abilities just to know how many players are on site. This is a net win for the defense.
**FIX** No fix needed. Coach should recognize this as success and communicate it to the player: "Your denial is forcing them to waste their exec util on recon."

**IF** Veto has low impact in rounds where the enemy plays fast and aggressive
**MEANING** His kit counters methodical play, not raw speed. Fast executes bypass his denial window.
**FIX** Against rush-heavy teams, Veto should lean into basic sentinel positioning and gunplay. Save denial for post-plant or retake situations.

**IF** Teammates do not capitalize on denied enemy intel
**MEANING** Communication gap. The team does not know when Veto has disrupted enemy recon.
**FIX** Veto must call out every interference activation: "Their dart is jammed" or "They got no info from that drone." Team awareness transforms denied information into round-winning advantages.

## 5. Map Interactions
- **Ascent**: Strong. Mid control relies heavily on information gathering, and Veto can deny mid reads, forcing enemies to commit without knowing the defensive setup.
- **Haven**: Valuable across three sites where attackers depend on recon to identify which site has fewer defenders.
- **Breeze**: Excellent. Long sightlines make information critical for both sides. Denying recon on A or B forces blind commitments.
- **Split**: Mid is an information battleground. Veto's denial makes mid control a guessing game for attackers.
- **Sunset**: Strong in mid corridor where both teams rely on information to decide their approach.

## 6. Matchup Notes
- **Hard counters Sova**: Sova's entire value is information gathering. Veto directly nullifies recon dart, owl drone, and shock dart lineups that rely on knowing positions.
- **Strong against Fade**: Prowlers and Haunt are high-value recon tools that Veto can disrupt, wasting Fade's core contribution.
- Weaker against **agents who do not rely on information** — Raze does not need to know where you are to throw a grenade at you.
- **KAY/O** suppress can disable Veto's interference, creating a counter-counter dynamic.
- Strong against **default-heavy team compositions** that play for information before committing.

## 7. What To Tell The Player

**Performing well**:
Pattern: You are holding your interference abilities until the enemy deploys their recon tool (Sova dart, Fade haunt, drone), then activating disruption at the moment the scan begins, and calling out every successful denial to your team within 1 second.
Why it works: Timing interference to the moment of enemy recon deployment wastes their cooldown entirely — they have spent an ability and received zero information in return. This forces them to either execute blind or invest a second recon ability, depleting their utility budget before the fight even starts. Your callout ("their dart is jammed") tells teammates the enemy is operating without intel, which means your team can hold aggressive positions that would normally be punished by a successful scan.
How to repeat: Step 1: Listen for the audio cue of enemy recon deployment (dart bounce, drone buzz, haunt throw). Step 2: Activate interference the moment you hear it — not before. Step 3: Immediately call out "no info from that [ability name]" so your team knows the enemy is blind. Against teams that rush without recon, switch to standard sentinel positioning and gunplay — save interference for post-plant or retake situations where the enemy needs info to push.

**Struggling**: "Your interference timing is off — you are either too early or too late. Hold your denial until you see or hear the enemy recon ability activate. If they are rushing without recon, focus on positioning and gunplay instead."

**Predictable**: "The enemy has learned to play around your denial by either rushing before you can activate it or using recon you cannot counter. Vary your timing — sometimes deny early, sometimes hold it. Mix denial with traditional sentinel holds."

## 8. Rank Modulation

**Low (Iron-Silver)**: Players either spam interference abilities at round start or forget to use them. Enemies at this rank rarely use recon effectively anyway, reducing Veto's value. Teach basic sentinel positioning first, denial second.

**Mid (Gold-Platinum)**: Denial timing is functional but inconsistent. Communication about disrupted abilities is rare. Focus on calling out every successful interference and timing abilities to counter specific enemy recon patterns.

**High (Diamond-Ascendant)**: Veto players understand denial timing but may struggle against teams that adapt by abandoning recon-dependent strategies. Focus on recognizing when to switch from denial to traditional sentinel play mid-game.

**Elite (Immortal-Radiant)**: Veto's value is in making entire enemy strategies unreliable. At this level, denial timing must be frame-perfect, and Veto must read the enemy's information economy — when they are relying on recon versus when they are playing off reads and timing.

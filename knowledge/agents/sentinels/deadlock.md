# AGENT: Deadlock

## 1. Role Identity
Deadlock is the sentinel of chokepoint supremacy. While other sentinels spread their utility across a site, Deadlock concentrates her power into making a single pathway absolutely miserable to walk through. Barrier Mesh walls off entries entirely, GravNet grounds and slows anyone caught in it, Sonic Sensor punishes sound-based movement, and Annihilation pulls enemies into a guaranteed death if they cannot break free. She is the anti-rush specialist — teams that rely on speed and momentum to take sites are her prey. Her weakness is adaptability: once her utility is committed, she has little ability to react to shifts.

## 2. Core Responsibilities
- **Chokepoint denial**: Barrier Mesh must block the highest-priority entry path. Unlike a Sage wall, it cannot be broken by simply shooting it — enemies must destroy the orbs, which costs time and attention.
- **GravNet as execute disruptor**: GravNet forces enemies to either crouch through a push (destroying momentum) or destroy the net (revealing position and timing). It should be placed reactively on detected rushes.
- **Sonic Sensor as early warning**: The sensor triggers on sound — footsteps, abilities, gunfire. It provides information and a brief concuss effect. Placement should cover paths where enemies cannot silently approach.
- **Annihilation as round-closer**: Her ultimate pulls a caught enemy through a cocoon along a set path. It is best used on isolated enemies or during post-plant to force a defuser off the spike.
- **Layered denial**: Deadlock's power is in combining utility. GravNet into a chokepoint that has a Sonic Sensor behind it creates a two-layer defense that punishes both slow and fast approaches.

## 3. Common Mistakes
- Placing Barrier Mesh too early, allowing enemies to simply rotate to the other site without wasting anything.
- Using GravNet like a grenade — throwing it into open space instead of placing it on specific paths enemies are forced to walk through.
- Placing Sonic Sensor where ambient noise (teammate utility, gunfire) triggers it constantly, creating noise fatigue.
- Using Annihilation in the open where teammates can easily shoot the cocoon to free the caught player.
- Committing all utility to one chokepoint and having nothing when the team rotates or the enemy takes a different angle.
- Not understanding the Barrier Mesh orb destruction mechanic — enemies who know to shoot the orbs dismantle it quickly.

## 4. Pattern to Meaning

**IF** Barrier Mesh is consistently placed but enemies take the site anyway through a different path
**MEANING** Mesh is blocking a secondary entry while the primary path is undefended. Deadlock is not reading the enemy's preferred attack route.
**FIX** Study which entry the enemy uses most in the first few rounds, then place Mesh there. Adapt mesh placement round-to-round.

**IF** GravNet is thrown but catches zero enemies across multiple rounds
**MEANING** GravNet timing is off — either thrown too early (enemies are not there yet) or aimed at the wrong path.
**FIX** Hold GravNet reactively. Throw it when you hear or see the push beginning, not as a pre-round setup.

**IF** Sonic Sensor triggers but no enemy is actually pushing
**MEANING** Sensor is placed where ambient game sounds or teammate activity triggers it. This creates false alerts.
**FIX** Place sensors on paths where only enemy footsteps would trigger them — typically deep flanks or unoccupied corridors.

**IF** Annihilation is used but the captured enemy is freed by teammates every time
**MEANING** Ultimate is being used in positions where the cocoon path is exposed to multiple enemy angles.
**FIX** Use Annihilation in tight corridors or around corners where the cocoon path is protected from enemy gunfire. Post-plant usage on an isolated defuser is ideal.

**IF** Deadlock dies early and her utility is unused
**MEANING** She is positioning too aggressively for a sentinel. Her kit requires her to be alive to deploy reactively.
**FIX** Play further back. Deadlock's utility is strongest when deployed in response to enemy action, not proactively.

## 5. Map Interactions
- **Lotus**: Strong. The rotating doors and tight corridors are perfect for Barrier Mesh and GravNet layering. Annihilation in B main or A root is devastating.
- **Bind**: Excellent on B site. Hookah is a natural chokepoint for Barrier Mesh, and GravNet at B long fragments any rush.
- **Fracture**: Viable due to the many narrow attack paths. Sonic Sensors on the underground tunnels provide early warning.
- **Haven**: Challenging due to three sites. Best focused on C site where garage is a natural chokepoint.
- **Ascent**: B main is a strong Barrier Mesh position. Sonic Sensor in market provides flank intel.

## 6. Matchup Notes
- Weak against **Raze** whose explosives destroy Barrier Mesh orbs and Sonic Sensors from range.
- Strong against **Neon** and other speed-based agents who rely on rushing through chokepoints — her entire kit is designed to punish this.
- **Sova** can clear Sonic Sensors and scout Barrier Mesh positions from safety.
- Annihilation is hardest to counter when used against **isolated players** — it is weakest against grouped teams who can shoot the cocoon.

## 7. What To Tell The Player

**Performing well**:
Pattern: You are placing Barrier Mesh on the enemy's preferred entry path (identified from the first 2-3 rounds), layering Sonic Sensor behind the mesh to catch anyone who clears it, and holding GravNet reactively for when you hear the push begin.
Why it works: Barrier Mesh on the primary attack path forces enemies to either destroy the orbs (costing 3-5 seconds and revealing position) or reroute to the secondary path — where your team holds crossfires. Sonic Sensor behind the mesh catches anyone who commits through the orb destruction, delivering a concuss right as they enter. Reactive GravNet on the detected push grounds sprinting attackers mid-rush, fragmenting their group into easy individual targets.
How to repeat: Step 1: Watch which entry the enemy favors in rounds 1-3, then place Barrier Mesh on that path from round 4 onward. Step 2: Place Sonic Sensor behind or adjacent to the mesh to catch enemies who push through. Step 3: Hold GravNet in hand and throw it when you hear footsteps committing to the push. Use Annihilation only in tight corridors or post-plant on an isolated defuser where teammates cannot easily shoot the cocoon free.

**Struggling**: "Your Barrier Mesh is not blocking the right entry, or your GravNet timing is off. Watch which path the enemy prefers and place your mesh there. Hold GravNet until you hear the push."

**Predictable**: "The enemy has learned your mesh positions and is routing around them. Vary which chokepoint you lock down each round. Use GravNet to cover the path your mesh does NOT block."

## 8. Rank Modulation

**Low (Iron-Silver)**: Players place Barrier Mesh randomly and forget Sonic Sensors exist. GravNet is thrown like a flashbang. Teach fundamentals: mesh on the main chokepoint, sensor on the flank, GravNet on the rush.

**Mid (Gold-Platinum)**: Utility placement is functional but static. Annihilation is used in open areas where enemies break free. Focus on reactive GravNet timing and protected Annihilation paths.

**High (Diamond-Ascendant)**: Deadlock mains here understand layering but may be too committed to one site. Focus on reading enemy patterns to decide which rounds to mesh which entry.

**Elite (Immortal-Radiant)**: Deadlock's value is in her ability to completely shut down a specific attack strategy for a round. At this level, her mesh and GravNet must adapt every round based on reads. Annihilation should be saved for guaranteed post-plant denial.

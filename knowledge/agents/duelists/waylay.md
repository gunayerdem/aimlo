# AGENT: Waylay

> PATCH-SENSITIVE NOTE: Waylay is a recent addition. Coaching advice may need updates as the meta evolves.

## 1. Role Identity
- Waylay is a hybrid entry-recon duelist who blends aggressive site-taking with information-gathering tools, giving the team both fragging power and intel that traditional duelists lack.
- Pick Waylay when the team composition needs a duelist who can provide some recon value alongside entry pressure. Choose Waylay over pure duelists like Jett or Reyna when the team lacks information-gathering and cannot afford a dedicated initiator slot.
- Waylay's core identity is the intel-aggressive duelist who gathers information as part of the entry process, making every site take smarter without sacrificing the aggression a duelist must bring.

## 2. Core Responsibilities
- **Attack-side duties:** Use recon-oriented abilities to gather pre-entry information, then commit to the entry with aggressive tools. Provide the team with defender positions before the execute begins. Take the opening duel with an informational advantage that other duelists do not have.
- **Defense-side duties:** Use information tools for early detection of pushes. Hold aggressive positions where the recon-entry hybrid kit allows for confident peeks backed by intel. Provide rotation calls based on gathered information before committing to a fight.
- **What the team expects:** The team expects Waylay to deliver both opening kills and pre-fight information. Unlike a pure duelist who enters blind, Waylay should know where at least one defender is before committing. Unlike a pure initiator, Waylay should be the one taking the fight, not just revealing it.

## 3. Common Mistakes
1. **Playing pure duelist and ignoring recon tools** — Waylay players who only use aggressive abilities waste the kit's information-gathering half. Every site take should begin with intel, then transition to aggression.
2. **Playing pure recon and avoiding entry** — The opposite mistake: players who focus on information but never commit to the fight. Waylay is still a duelist. The intel should feed directly into an aggressive play, not be passed to teammates while Waylay hides.
3. **Sequencing abilities incorrectly** — The information ability should come first, followed by the entry tool. Reversing this order means entering blind, which negates Waylay's entire advantage.
4. **Over-relying on recon in close quarters** — At close range, gathering information wastes time that should be spent fighting. Use recon at range, switch to aggression up close.
5. **Not communicating intel to the team** — Waylay's information value is multiplied when shared. Players who gather intel but do not call it out reduce Waylay to a worse version of a standard duelist.
6. **Using the full kit before the execute** — Burning all cooldowns during the setup phase leaves Waylay with no tools during the actual site take. Budget abilities across the round.
7. **Neglecting the duelist fundamentals** — Regardless of the recon hybrid identity, Waylay must still win duels, create space, and take first contact. Information gathering does not replace the core duelist job.

## 4. Pattern -> Meaning
IF: Player gathers information but does not act on it within 5 seconds
MEANING: They are playing too passively, treating Waylay as an initiator rather than a duelist
FIX: Coach the info-to-aggression pipeline. Recon should have a direct follow-up play. Gather intel, then immediately peek the revealed position.

IF: Player entries without using recon abilities first
MEANING: They are defaulting to standard duelist behavior, wasting Waylay's informational advantage
FIX: Drill the recon-first sequence. Before any entry, the information tool should be deployed. Entry without intel is a wasted Waylay round.

IF: Player has low first-kill rate despite getting pre-fight information
MEANING: The intel is not translating into aim advantage, possibly because the player hesitates after getting info
FIX: Reduce the gap between information and aggression. The peek should happen while the enemy is still reacting to the recon tool.

IF: Player dies frequently with unused abilities
MEANING: They are not integrating the full kit into their playstyle, possibly overwhelmed by the hybrid nature
FIX: Simplify the kit usage to two steps: recon first, entry second. Master this sequence before adding complexity.

IF: Team frequently lacks information despite having Waylay
MEANING: The player is not communicating intel or not using recon tools at all
FIX: Emphasize that Waylay's recon is team utility. Every piece of information gathered should be called out, even if Waylay plans to act on it personally.

## 5. Map Interactions
- **Strong maps:** Haven (three sites reward the recon component — Waylay can gather info before committing to which site to execute), Ascent (mid control benefits from intel-backed aggression), Lotus (complex layout with multiple angles rewards pre-fight information gathering).
- **Weak maps:** Breeze (long sightlines may expose recon tools before they deliver value, and the open space reduces the intel advantage), Fracture (simultaneous multi-point entries may not benefit from Waylay's sequential recon-then-entry approach).
- **Position-specific:** Map-specific advice will solidify as competitive meta develops. General principle: Waylay is strongest in areas where one piece of information eliminates a common defender position, enabling a confident entry. Tight chokepoints with predictable hold spots are ideal.

## 6. Matchup Notes
- **Struggles against:** Agents with counter-intel tools like Fade (who can reverse-track Waylay's position), KAY/O (suppression disables the recon-entry combo entirely), and Skye (whose flash and Trailblazer compete in the recon-aggression space).
- **Counters:** Static defenders who rely on surprise. Waylay's pre-fight intel removes the element of surprise from Cypher setups, Killjoy turret placements, and Chamber Trademark positions.
- **Adaptation:** Against counter-intel compositions, delay recon usage to avoid giving away Waylay's position before the execute. Against passive defenses, lean heavily into the recon tools — the information advantage compounds against enemies who do not reposition.

## 7. What To Tell The Player
### When performing well

**Player Action:** You deploy recon before every entry, act on gathered intel within 2-3 seconds, and call defender positions to your team before swinging — combining duelist aggression with initiator information flow.
**Enemy Perception:** Defenders see a duelist who always knows where they are before peeking. They believe your aim is exceptional, but the real advantage is that every fight is pre-aimed because your recon revealed their position 2 seconds ago.
**Enemy Reaction:** They are forced to reposition after every recon scan (costing 2-3 seconds of round time and abandoning prepared positions) or hold and fight against a player who already knows their exact angle.
**Exploit Window:** The 2-3 second window between recon reveal and defender reposition is your kill window. The defender is either still in the scanned position (giving you a pre-aimed duel) or mid-movement to a new position (giving you a shot at a moving target with no crosshair placement).
**Repeat Strategy:** Round 1: recon the primary hold angle, peek within 2 seconds, call the position. Round 2: recon a secondary angle to catch defenders who shifted off the primary. Round 3: skip recon and dry-peek to punish defenders who wait for the scan before committing to their hold. Vary the recon-to-entry timing between instant and delayed.
**Enemy Adaptation:** After 3-5 rounds, defenders will reposition pre-emptively before your recon lands, play positions that are difficult to scan, or push aggressively during your recon deployment to catch you mid-ability.
**Counter-Adaptation:** When they pre-reposition: use recon to confirm the new positions rather than the old ones — the information is still valuable even if they moved. When they play anti-scan positions: clear those spots with duelist aggression and save recon for later in the round. When they push during deployment: hold an off-angle and let them walk into your crosshair while expecting you to be mid-ability.
### When struggling
"Pick one role per round: either lead with recon and commit to the entry, or support with info and let a teammate trade you. Do not do neither."
### When being predictable
"They are reading your recon timing. Delay the information tool by a few seconds or use it from a different angle to avoid them pre-aiming your follow-up peek."

## 8. Rank Modulation
### Low Elo (Iron-Silver)
Focus on the basic two-step: use the information tool first, then fight. Do not worry about optimization — just build the habit of gathering intel before peeking. Call out everything you learn to your team. Play Waylay as a duelist who happens to have a recon tool, not the other way around.
### Mid Elo (Gold-Diamond)
Develop the timing between recon and aggression — the gap should shrink as comfort grows. Start using intel to benefit the whole team, not just personal peeks. Learn which sites on each map give the highest recon value. Begin sequencing abilities across the full round instead of using everything at once.
### High Elo (Ascendant-Immortal)
Master the information-to-action pipeline so it is instantaneous. Use recon adaptively: sometimes for personal entry, sometimes for team coordination. Develop counter-play for enemy teams that try to deny or bait the recon tools. Waylay's pick value at this rank depends on disciplined kit usage.
### Elite (Radiant)
Focus on meta-level reads about when Waylay's hybrid role adds more value than a pure duelist or pure initiator. Optimize ability sequencing per round based on enemy defensive adjustments. The recon timing should vary to prevent pattern reads. At this rank, Waylay's information value may exceed the frag value — ensure both are being delivered.

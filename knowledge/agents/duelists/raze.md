# AGENT: Raze

## 1. Role Identity
- Raze is the explosive area-denial duelist who forces enemies out of positions with damage-based utility, then follows up with aggressive satchel-based entries.
- Pick Raze when the team needs to break through tight choke points, clear corners with utility before peeking, or play on maps with compact sites. Choose Raze over Jett when the map favors ground-level aggression over vertical play, and when utility-based clearing is more valuable than repositioning.
- Raze's core identity is the utility-heavy entry fragger who softens positions with explosives before committing to the fight, combining area denial with high-speed satchel movement.

## 2. Core Responsibilities
- **Attack-side duties:** Use Boombot to gain information on common hold positions before entering. Clear tight angles with Paint Shells (grenades). Satchel into site to create chaos and draw attention. Use Showstopper to break open contested executes.
- **Defense-side duties:** Use Boombot for early information on pushes. Hold tight angles where grenade spam punishes grouped attackers. Use satchels to reposition quickly during retakes or to escape when overwhelmed.
- **What the team expects:** Raze should be clearing space with utility before the team commits. The team expects Boombot info, grenade damage on clustered enemies, and satchel entries that force defenders to react.

## 3. Common Mistakes
1. **Satcheling into site without prior utility** — The double satchel entry is flashy but suicidal if the player has not sent Boombot or grenade first. Satcheling blind means landing in crossfires with no information.
2. **Using grenade as a kill tool instead of a clearing tool** — Paint Shells should force enemies out of positions, not fish for lucky kills. Throwing grenades into open space wastes their area-denial value.
3. **Predictable Boombot pathing** — Sending Boombot down the same lane every round teaches enemies to one-tap it immediately. Vary the timing, angle, or combine it with a team flash.
4. **Panic-firing Showstopper at long range** — The rocket has travel time and a visible trail. Firing it at long range gives enemies time to dodge. Close to mid range is the effective window.
5. **Double satcheling without a plan** — Players chain both satchels for maximum distance but land in positions they did not scout. One satchel for entry, one held for escape or repositioning is often better.
6. **Holding grenades for retake instead of using them on entry** — On attack, Paint Shells are highest value during the execute phase. Saving them for post-plant reduces their clearing utility.
7. **Ignoring Boombot callouts** — Boombot provides information even when it does not chase anyone. If it does not lock on, that position is clear. Players who ignore this waste the intel.

## 4. Pattern -> Meaning
IF: Player dies within 2 seconds of satchel landing on site
MEANING: They are satcheling into uncleared space without using Boombot or grenade first
FIX: Recommend the sequence: Boombot to scout, grenade to clear the Boombot's target area, then satchel entry. Utility first, movement second.

IF: Player has high Showstopper usage but low kill rate with it
MEANING: They are firing the rocket launcher at ranges where enemies can react and dodge
FIX: Coach closer-range Showstopper timing. Use satchel to close distance before firing, or save it for clustered enemies in tight spaces.

IF: Player rarely gets first kills despite playing Raze
MEANING: They are likely playing Raze as a passive damage dealer rather than an entry fragger
FIX: Emphasize that Raze is a duelist, not a sentinel. Utility should lead directly into aggressive peeks and site takes.

IF: Player frequently dies with unused Boombot
MEANING: They are not incorporating Boombot into their pre-fight routine, possibly rushing without information
FIX: Drill the habit of deploying Boombot as the first action in any site take. It costs nothing to send it ahead.

IF: Player gets multi-kills with grenades but team still loses rounds
MEANING: Grenade kills are coming in cleanup rather than during the execute, meaning the entry timing is off
FIX: Shift grenade usage earlier in the round to create space, not later to farm kills.

## 5. Map Interactions
- **Strong maps:** Bind (teleporters amplify Raze's rotation speed, tight sites reward grenades), Split (narrow corridors maximize satchel entries and grenade value), Fracture (multi-entry points let Raze satchel from unexpected angles), Lotus (tight chokepoints at A and C reward explosive clearing).
- **Weak maps:** Breeze (long sightlines reduce grenade and satchel value, Raze cannot compete with Op agents at range), Icebox (vertical fights favor Jett's updraft over Raze's ground-level satchels on A site).
- **Position-specific:** On Bind, Boombot through teleporter provides free information. On Split, A Main grenade spam can single-handedly force defenders off Ramps. On Fracture, satcheling from Arcade to A site catches rotators off guard.

## 6. Matchup Notes
- **Struggles against:** Killjoy (turret reveals satchel trajectory, Lockdown forces Raze off site), Cypher (tripwires catch satchel paths, camera tracks Raze's entry), Chamber (Trademark punishes aggressive satchel flanks).
- **Counters:** Agents who hold tight, static positions. Raze destroys Sage walls, flushes Viper out of Pit setups, and clears Brimstone smoke-camp positions with grenade bounces.
- **Adaptation:** Against sentinel-heavy comps, use Boombot to trigger and waste their utility before the team commits. Against double-duelist comps, coordinate satchel entries with the other duelist's flash for a combined rush.

## 7. What To Tell The Player
### When performing well
**Player Action:** You chain Boombot into Paint Shells into Blast Pack entry, with each ability feeding positional intel to the next within a 4-second sequence.
**Enemy Perception:** Defenders read you as a utility-heavy attacker who always precedes entry with Boombot scout. They expect the bot first, then grenades on their position, then the satchel entry from the same direction.
**Enemy Reaction:** They are forced to shoot the Boombot (revealing their position and taking their crosshair off the entry angle) or reposition away from it (abandoning their prepared hold). Either response displaces them from their chosen crosshair placement.
**Exploit Window:** The 1-2 seconds a defender spends shooting Boombot or dodging Paint Shells leaves their crosshair off the entry angle. Your Blast Pack arrives during this displacement window when they are mid-reposition with no stable aim.
**Repeat Strategy:** Round 1: Boombot to the most common hold angle, grenade the position, satchel in. Round 2: send Boombot to a different angle on the same site. Round 3: skip Boombot entirely and satchel fast to punish defenders who wait for the bot before holding. Vary which ability leads.
**Enemy Adaptation:** After 4-5 rounds, defenders will pre-destroy Boombot from safe angles, play positions outside Paint Shells radius, or push through the utility sequence before you arrive.
**Counter-Adaptation:** When they pre-destroy the bot: use its destruction sound as a free position confirm and grenade that spot immediately. When they dodge grenades: satchel to where they repositioned (the dodge direction is predictable — always away from the grenade). When they push through your sequence: hold an off-angle with a rifle and let them walk into you instead of satcheling.
### When struggling
"Stop satcheling into the unknown. Send the Boombot first, let it do its job, then follow the information it gives you."
### When being predictable
"They are pre-destroying your Boombot. Delay it by three seconds or send it from a different angle."

## 8. Rank Modulation
### Low Elo (Iron-Silver)
Focus on using all utility before dying. Learn basic grenade lineups for common hold spots on two to three maps. Practice single-satchel entries before attempting doubles. Prioritize Boombot usage every round.
### Mid Elo (Gold-Diamond)
Develop the full utility sequence: Boombot, grenade, satchel entry. Learn double-satchel mechanics for faster site takes. Start using Showstopper in combination with satchel closing distance. Understand when to save grenades for post-plant denial.
### High Elo (Ascendant-Immortal)
Master satchel air-strafe for unpredictable landing spots. Develop creative Boombot pathing that avoids instant destruction. Coordinate Showstopper with team executes. Learn grenade lineups that bounce into deep site positions.
### Elite (Radiant)
Focus on reading enemy utility patterns to decide when to fast-satchel versus slow-execute. Optimize Showstopper timing for maximum economic damage (force opponents to save). Boombot timing should vary by round to prevent enemy adaptation.

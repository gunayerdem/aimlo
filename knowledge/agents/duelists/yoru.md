# AGENT: Yoru

## 1. Role Identity
- Yoru is the deception-based duelist who uses teleportation, clones, and dimensional rifting to create confusion, exploit timing gaps, and punish defenders who commit to predictable rotations.
- Pick Yoru when the team wants an agent who can create chaos through misdirection rather than raw firepower. Choose Yoru over other duelists when the enemy team over-rotates on sound cues, or when lurk-based play is part of the team strategy.
- Yoru's core identity is the timing-based trickster who wins rounds through information asymmetry — making enemies think he is somewhere he is not, then punishing their reaction.

## 2. Core Responsibilities
- **Attack-side duties:** Use Gatecrash (TP) to set up lurk-to-site rotations. Deploy Fakeout (clone) to bait utility and gather information. Flash into entries using TP-flash combos. Use Dimensional Drift (ult) to scout sites and create behind-enemy-lines plays.
- **Defense-side duties:** Set up TP behind enemy push paths for aggressive flanks. Use clone to bait out attacker utility in choke points. Use ult to gather information during post-plant or to contest plants safely. Hold off-angles with TP ready for escape.
- **What the team expects:** Yoru should be creating confusion and forcing enemies to check extra angles. The team expects him to generate openings through deception, provide information from deep flanks, and exploit timing windows that no other duelist can reach.

## 3. Common Mistakes
1. **Sending TP into obvious locations** — Gatecrash makes a distinct audio cue. Placing it in common spots (e.g., directly through a chokepoint) alerts enemies to exactly where Yoru will appear. Creative TP placement is Yoru's entire value.
2. **Activating TP without a plan** — Teleporting and then hesitating gets Yoru killed. Every TP activation should have a pre-planned action: flash and swing, ult, or a specific angle to clear.
3. **Using clone without supporting it** — Fakeout is most effective when Yoru is ready to swing behind it. A clone sent alone is quickly identified and ignored. The clone should force a reaction that Yoru immediately exploits.
4. **Ulting for information only** — Dimensional Drift provides excellent scouting, but players who only use it to look around waste its offensive potential. Ult should end with a flash-exit into a kill opportunity.
5. **Playing Yoru as a standard entry duelist** — Yoru does not have the raw entry power of Jett or Raze. Players who try to dry-peek and out-aim opponents without using Yoru's deception tools are playing the wrong agent.
6. **Predictable lurk timing** — Yoru players who lurk every round become predictable. The enemy will hold the flank angle instead of rotating, negating Yoru's timing advantage.
7. **Wasting flash on TP exit** — Yoru's flash should blind enemies at the TP destination. Popping flash too early (before TP arrives) or too late (after enemies react) wastes the combo.

## 4. Pattern -> Meaning
IF: Player dies frequently within 2 seconds of teleporting
MEANING: TP destinations are predictable or the player is not pre-planning post-TP actions
FIX: Coach creative TP placements — behind boxes, into corners that require enemies to turn. Always have a flash ready for TP exit.

IF: Player has high lurk death rate
MEANING: They are lurking at the same timing every round, and enemies are reading the pattern
FIX: Vary lurk timing. Sometimes lurk early, sometimes fake the lurk and play with the team. Unpredictability is Yoru's entire kit.

IF: Player rarely gets kills during or after Dimensional Drift
MEANING: They are using ult defensively or only for scouting, not exploiting the flash-exit combo
FIX: Drill the ult-exit-flash-swing sequence. The ult should end with an aggressive play, not a passive one.

IF: Player sends clone but does not follow up within 3 seconds
MEANING: Clone timing is disconnected from the player's position, making it obvious bait
FIX: Teach the player to position themselves to swing the moment an enemy shoots the clone or reacts to it. Clone and player should arrive at the same time from different angles.

IF: Player's TP is frequently destroyed before activation
MEANING: TP placement is too exposed or enemies have learned the player's TP routes
FIX: Use less obvious TP angles. Place TP behind objects where it is harder to spot. Mix up which rounds use TP and which rounds play without it.

## 5. Map Interactions
- **Strong maps:** Bind (teleporters multiply Yoru's rotation options, TP through teleporter is unique), Split (vertical mid gives strong TP-to-flank routes), Haven (three sites create more timing exploits and rotation tricks), Icebox (B site has strong behind-lines TP spots through tubes).
- **Weak maps:** Breeze (too open for deception-based play, limited cover for TP placement), Lotus (rotating doors reduce TP value since enemies already expect flanks).
- **Position-specific:** On Bind, sending TP through the teleporter while walking the other direction creates genuine confusion about Yoru's position. On Split, TP from A Main to A Heaven during a B fake is a classic Yoru play. On Haven, C Long TP behind Garage creates unpredictable flanks during A executes.

## 6. Matchup Notes
- **Struggles against:** Fade (Haunt reveals Yoru through ult and marks TP locations), Sova (Recon Bolt exposes lurk paths and tracks clone), Killjoy (turret spots clone and TP, Lockdown forces ult usage defensively).
- **Counters:** Teams that over-rotate on sound cues — Yoru's clone footsteps and TP sounds pull enemies out of position. Effective against Cypher (TP bypasses tripwire setups) and against slow-rotating compositions.
- **Adaptation:** Against Fade, time TP activation to avoid Haunt windows. Against Sova, destroy Recon Bolt before committing to lurk. Against info-heavy comps, lean into the clone more — let them "find" Yoru's fake position while he takes the real one.

## 7. What To Tell The Player
### When performing well
Pattern: You are sending Fakeout toward one site while Gatecrash is pre-placed at the opposite site, and your lurk timing varies between early-round and mid-round across different rounds.
Why it works: The clone footsteps pull defenders toward the fake while your TP is already positioned at the real execute site. Varying your lurk timing between rounds 1 and rounds 5+ means defenders cannot establish a pattern for when to expect your flank, so they must hold the angle permanently or risk getting punished.
How to repeat: Step 1: Pre-place Gatecrash toward the actual target site during buy phase. Step 2: Send Fakeout toward the opposite site entrance. Step 3: Wait for enemy rotation audio or minimap movement, then TP and flash on arrival. Alternate between lurking at the 20-second mark and the 45-second mark across rounds.
### When struggling
"You are playing Yoru like Jett. Stop dry-peeking. Every fight should start with a clone, a flash, or a TP — never a raw peek."
### When being predictable
"They have read your lurk timing. Play with the team for two rounds, then lurk again when they stop expecting it."

## 8. Rank Modulation
### Low Elo (Iron-Silver)
Focus on basic TP-flash combos. Learn to send clone before peeking any angle. Do not lurk every round — play with the team at least half the time. Use ult for safe site scouting, not complex plays.
### Mid Elo (Gold-Diamond)
Develop varied lurk timing — sometimes early, sometimes late, sometimes not at all. Practice the ult-flash-exit combo for kills. Learn two to three TP routes per map that avoid common destruction spots. Coordinate clone timing with team executes.
### High Elo (Ascendant-Immortal)
Master the mind-game layer: using TP sound to bait rotations without actually teleporting. Develop round-by-round timing variation that prevents pattern reads. Use clone to force specific utility out of enemies, then exploit the cooldowns.
### Elite (Radiant)
Focus on reading enemy adaptation patterns. If they stopped rotating on clone sounds, use that to your advantage by playing the actual angle the clone ran. Every Yoru decision should be based on what the enemy expects, then doing the opposite. Ult timing should exploit enemy economy and utility cycles.

# AGENT: Neon

## 1. Role Identity
- Neon is the speed-based duelist who overwhelms defenders with fast site executes, using her sprint and slide mechanics to close distances that other agents cannot.
- Pick Neon when the team wants to execute fast-paced site takes that give defenders minimal time to set up crossfires. Choose Neon over Jett when raw ground speed matters more than vertical play, and when the team comp already has an Op player.
- Neon's core identity is the velocity duelist who turns site takes into footraces, forcing defenders to react to speed rather than utility.

## 2. Core Responsibilities
- **Attack-side duties:** Sprint into sites to create immediate chaos. Use Fast Lane (walls) to divide defender crossfires and create safe corridors for the team. Slide-peek into contested positions for aggressive first contact. Use Overdrive to win multi-kill rounds during fast executes.
- **Defense-side duties:** Use sprint for rapid rotations between sites. Hold aggressive forward positions with slide-peek capability. Deploy walls to isolate pushes and funnel attackers into killzones. Use Overdrive for retakes when the site is lost.
- **What the team expects:** Neon should be the first body on site. The team expects her to sprint in, draw attention, and either get the opening kill or create so much chaos that teammates can trade immediately.

## 3. Common Mistakes
1. **Sprinting into site without walls** — Neon's sprint makes noise and draws attention. Without Fast Lane walls to block sightlines, she runs into open crossfires and dies before the slide animation completes.
2. **Using slide at the wrong distance** — Sliding from too far away gives defenders time to track her. Sliding from too close wastes the speed advantage. The ideal slide distance is around 10-15 meters from the target angle.
3. **Deploying walls that block teammates** — Fast Lane walls that cut off teammate sightlines or block follow-up trades are worse than no walls at all. Wall placement must create a corridor for the team, not a barrier.
4. **Activating Overdrive too early** — Popping ult before reaching the fight zone wastes its duration on travel time. Neon should sprint to position, then activate Overdrive when enemies are in range.
5. **Slide-peeking the same angle repeatedly** — The slide is fast but predictable if used on the same corner every round. Enemies will pre-aim slide height after seeing it once.
6. **Ignoring sprint for rotations on defense** — Neon's rotation speed is one of her biggest defensive advantages. Players who walk between sites waste this unique strength.
7. **Using Overdrive as a panic button** — Overdrive should be activated proactively when Neon has positional advantage, not reactively when she is losing a fight. The spin-up time makes reactive use unreliable.

## 4. Pattern -> Meaning
IF: Player dies during sprint before reaching the site
MEANING: They are sprinting through open sightlines without wall cover, or the execute timing is not coordinated with team utility
FIX: Recommend always deploying Fast Lane walls before sprinting. Coordinate sprint timing with a teammate's flash or smoke.

IF: Player gets opening kills but team cannot follow up
MEANING: Wall placement is likely cutting off teammate angles, or the player is sprinting too far ahead of the group
FIX: Review wall angles — they should create corridors the team can push through, not walls the team must go around. Slow sprint start by one second to keep the team in trade range.

IF: Player rarely uses Overdrive or uses it in lost rounds
MEANING: They do not understand Overdrive's role as a round-winning tool, not a desperation measure
FIX: Coach proactive Overdrive usage: pop it when entering a site with two or more enemies alive, not when clutching a 1v3.

IF: Player has high death rate in the first 15 seconds of rounds
MEANING: They are overcommitting to speed without checking for information first
FIX: Teach the difference between fast and reckless. A coordinated fast execute uses team utility to clear one angle before Neon sprints through the remaining space.

IF: Player frequently dies with walls unused
MEANING: They are relying on raw speed instead of Neon's full kit
FIX: Drill the wall-sprint-slide sequence as muscle memory. Walls first, sprint second, slide to arrive.

## 5. Map Interactions
- **Strong maps:** Fracture (multiple entry points reward fast rotation and sprint-based takes), Pearl (long corridors like B Main reward wall-sprint entries), Lotus (A Main and C Mound have strong wall placements for fast takes), Split (A Main sprint-slide is devastating with proper walls).
- **Weak maps:** Breeze (too open for sprint-based play, long sightlines punish Neon before she arrives), Ascent (mid is too exposed for sprinting, sites require more vertical play).
- **Position-specific:** On Pearl, deploying walls down B Main and sprinting creates one of the fastest site takes in the game. On Lotus, C Mound walls can isolate the Waterfall defender and let Neon slide into site unopposed.

## 6. Matchup Notes
- **Struggles against:** Killjoy (turret tracks through sprint, Lockdown forces Neon off her speed advantage), Sage (wall blocks sprint paths, slow orb kills Neon's speed identity), Cypher (tripwires catch sprint lanes).
- **Counters:** Slow-setup controllers like Brimstone and Harbor who need time to deploy utility. Neon arrives before their setups are complete. Also effective against Viper, whose wall takes time to activate.
- **Adaptation:** Against Sage, request a teammate break the wall before the execute. Against Killjoy, vary sprint timing so turret placement becomes a guessing game. Against Chamber, use walls to block Trademark angles.

## 7. What To Tell The Player
### When performing well
Pattern: You are deploying Fast Lane walls before every sprint entry, and your slide-peek distance is landing you at the correct 10-15 meter engagement range.
Why it works: Fast Lane splits the defenders' crossfire into isolated 1v1s, and your sprint-to-slide timing closes the distance before they can adjust. Defenders who are walled off cannot trade their teammate, turning a 1v2 into sequential 1v1s in your favor.
How to repeat: Step 1: Cast Fast Lane to divide the site before sprinting. Step 2: Sprint through the corridor your walls create. Step 3: Begin slide at 10-15 meters from the first expected contact point. After site is taken, save Overdrive for retake scenarios where 2+ enemies are confirmed alive.
### When struggling
"Slow down by one second. Use your walls before you sprint. Speed without setup is just feeding."
### When being predictable
"They are pre-aiming your slide angle. Switch to sprinting past the angle and sliding from the other side."

## 8. Rank Modulation
### Low Elo (Iron-Silver)
Focus on learning wall placement before sprinting. Practice the wall-sprint-slide sequence on every site take. Do not activate Overdrive until you understand when fights will happen. Focus on one fast execute per map.
### Mid Elo (Gold-Diamond)
Develop slide-peek discipline: practice optimal distance and learn to counterstrafe out of slide. Start using sprint for defensive rotations. Coordinate sprint entries with at least one teammate's utility.
### High Elo (Ascendant-Immortal)
Master wall placements that enable team follow-up, not just personal entry. Optimize Overdrive timing for multi-kill potential. Develop sprint fakes — sprinting to make noise without committing, then rotating.
### Elite (Radiant)
Focus on reading defensive setups to decide between fast and delayed executes. Use sprint audio cues as bait for fakes. Overdrive should be timed with team utility for maximum round conversion. Wall angles should adapt round-by-round based on enemy defensive adjustments.

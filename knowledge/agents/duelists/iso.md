# AGENT: Iso

## 1. Role Identity
- Iso is the duel-focused duelist who generates a personal shield through kills and uses his kit to isolate and win individual engagements, making him the strongest 1v1 specialist in the game.
- Pick Iso when the team needs a duelist who can systematically win individual fights through shield generation and wall isolation. Choose Iso over Jett when the team values sustained fight-winning over repositioning, and when the map rewards methodical clearing over speed.
- Iso's core identity is the shield-stacking duelist who converts every kill into durability, forcing enemies to burn extra bullets and utility to bring him down.

## 2. Core Responsibilities
- **Attack-side duties:** Use Double Tap to generate shields before engagements. Deploy the wall to cut sightlines and isolate defenders into 1v1 situations. Entry site by systematically winning isolated duels. Use Kill Contract (ult) to remove a key defender from the round by forcing them into a 1v1 arena.
- **Defense-side duties:** Hold angles where 1v1 engagements are likely. Use the wall to split attacking groups and force them to fight piecemeal. Double Tap shields make Iso harder to trade, so he can hold aggressive forward positions. Use Kill Contract to remove the enemy's best player during retakes.
- **What the team expects:** Iso should be winning duels consistently. The team expects him to stack shields, survive fights other duelists would not, and use his wall to create advantageous 1v1 scenarios for himself and the team.

## 3. Common Mistakes
1. **Not activating Double Tap before fights** — Double Tap requires a kill or orb to grant the shield, but it must be activated before the engagement starts. Players who forget to activate it lose Iso's core defensive advantage.
2. **Using the wall to block sightlines instead of isolating enemies** — Iso's wall is not a Viper wall. Its purpose is to cut enemy groups so Iso can fight one player at a time. Using it as a simple sightline blocker wastes its isolation potential.
3. **Challenging multiple enemies with shield active** — The shield absorbs one instance of damage. Players who swing wide against multiple enemies lose the shield to the first bullet and die to the second player. Shield is for winning 1v1s, not multi-peeks.
4. **Wasting Kill Contract on low-impact targets** — Ulting a player who is already damaged or out of position wastes the ult. Kill Contract should target the enemy's strongest player or an anchoring sentinel whose removal opens the site.
5. **Not shooting the Double Tap orbs** — After a kill, the orb appears briefly. Players who forget to shoot it miss the shield entirely, negating Iso's entire passive economy.
6. **Playing too passively and not forcing duels** — Iso is designed to fight. Playing him as a passive agent means shields never stack and the wall is never used offensively. He must seek engagements.
7. **Poor wall angle on execute** — The wall's angle determines which enemies it isolates. A poorly angled wall splits the wrong players or gives defenders time to reposition. Pre-plan wall angles for each site take.

## 4. Pattern -> Meaning
IF: Player rarely has shield active during fights
MEANING: They are not activating Double Tap before engagements or are missing the orb pickup after kills
FIX: Drill the Double Tap activation as pre-fight routine. Before peeking any angle, Double Tap should be active. After any kill, immediately shoot the orb.

IF: Player dies frequently in 2v1 or 3v1 situations
MEANING: They are not using the wall to isolate enemies, swinging into multiple angles with only a single-hit shield
FIX: Coach wall usage as fight management. Before every engagement against multiple enemies, the wall should split them so Iso only faces one.

IF: Player uses Kill Contract but loses the 1v1 arena fight
MEANING: They may be ulting opponents who are stronger in raw aim duels, or they are not positioning for the arena correctly
FIX: Target selection matters. Ult the sentinel holding site, not the enemy Jett. In the arena, pre-aim head height and use cover boxes.

IF: Player has high first-kill rate but low survival rate after opening picks
MEANING: They are winning the first duel but not collecting the shield orb, entering the second fight without protection
FIX: Emphasize the orb pickup cadence. Kill, shoot orb, reposition, take next fight. The half-second to collect the orb is an investment, not a delay.

IF: Player's wall usage does not result in isolated kills
MEANING: Wall angle is not cutting off the right players, or the wall is deployed too early giving enemies time to reposition
FIX: Review wall angles per site. The wall should be deployed as part of the execute, not pre-emptively. Time it so enemies cannot rotate before the wall activates.

## 5. Map Interactions
- **Strong maps:** Ascent (mid control rewards Iso's 1v1 forcing, wall isolates market from site), Haven (multiple chokepoints let Iso wall-isolate individual defenders), Pearl (long corridors suit wall placement and methodical clearing), Split (tight sites reward shield-stacking through sequential duels).
- **Weak maps:** Breeze (wide open sites make isolation difficult, wall does not cover enough space), Icebox (vertical play reduces wall effectiveness, B site is too open for Iso's ground-level isolation).
- **Position-specific:** On Ascent, walling across A Site to isolate the Heaven player from the generator player creates two winnable 1v1s instead of a 1v2. On Haven, C site wall isolating the Plat defender from the back-site player is one of Iso's strongest executes.

## 6. Matchup Notes
- **Struggles against:** Raze (explosive utility ignores shields and forces Iso out of his preferred 1v1 engagements), Breach (stun and flash disrupt Iso's duel setup, Aftershock forces repositioning), KAY/O (suppression removes Double Tap shield generation).
- **Counters:** Static defenders who hold predictable angles — Iso forces them into 1v1s they cannot avoid. Effective against Killjoy (Kill Contract removes her from turret/alarm setup), Cypher (wall bypasses tripwire placements), and Chamber (forces direct aim duels without Trademark help).
- **Adaptation:** Against Raze, play outside grenade range and force duels at medium distance where shield absorbs a single bullet, not explosive splash. Against KAY/O, time entries after suppression expires. Against Breach, stay out of narrow corridors where his kit is strongest.

## 7. What To Tell The Player
### When performing well
Pattern: You are activating Double Tap before every engagement, shooting the shield orb after each kill, and using your wall to isolate defenders into sequential 1v1 fights.
Why it works: The Double Tap shield absorbs one hit, meaning you survive encounters that would kill any other duelist at equal HP. Collecting the orb after each kill refreshes that shield for the next fight — across a 3-kill site take, that is three extra hits absorbed. Your wall splits 2v1 situations into two 1v1s where your shield gives you the advantage each time.
How to repeat: Step 1: Activate Double Tap during the approach. Step 2: Deploy wall to cut off the second defender before peeking the first. Step 3: Win the duel, immediately shoot the orb, then peek the isolated second player with a fresh shield. Use Kill Contract on the enemy anchor player holding site, not on damaged or low-impact targets.
### When struggling
"You are taking fights without your shield active. Activate Double Tap before every peek. If you missed the orb, slow down and reset."
### When being predictable
"They are avoiding your wall angle and playing around the isolation. Move the wall to a different axis to cut off their escape route."

## 8. Rank Modulation
### Low Elo (Iron-Silver)
Focus on one thing: activate Double Tap before every fight. Learn to shoot the orb after kills. Use the wall as a simple sightline blocker until you understand isolation. Save Kill Contract for situations where one enemy is holding site alone.
### Mid Elo (Gold-Diamond)
Develop wall angles that isolate specific defenders on each site. Practice the orb-pickup cadence so it becomes automatic. Start using Kill Contract to remove high-value targets. Learn to chain duels: kill, orb, shield, next fight.
### High Elo (Ascendant-Immortal)
Master wall timing so enemies cannot reposition before isolation kicks in. Optimize Kill Contract target selection based on enemy economy and role. Use shield stacking to play hyper-aggressively in rounds where aim is on.
### Elite (Radiant)
Focus on reading which enemy is the highest-value Kill Contract target based on their economy, utility, and round impact. Wall angles should adapt round by round as enemies shift positions. Shield management should be second nature — every kill leads to the next shielded fight.

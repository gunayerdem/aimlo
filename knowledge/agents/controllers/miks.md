# AGENT: Miks

> **PATCH-SENSITIVE**: Miks is a newer agent. Ability values, cooldowns, and mechanics may change between patches. Verify current stats before coaching sessions.

## 1. Role Identity
Miks is the newest addition to the controller roster, bringing a distinct approach to vision denial and area control. As a recent release, his meta position and optimal playstyle are still evolving with each patch. Miks offers controller fundamentals -- smokes and area denial -- but with a unique kit identity that distinguishes him from established controllers. Players picking up Miks should understand core controller principles first, then layer his specific mechanics on top.

## 2. Core Responsibilities
- Provide reliable vision denial through his smoke abilities to support team executes and retakes
- Utilize his unique area-control tools to lock down zones and punish enemy pushes
- Maintain controller fundamentals: smoke timing, choke denial, and execute support
- Adapt to evolving meta understanding as the community discovers optimal playstyles
- Communicate utility plans clearly since teammates may be unfamiliar with Miks' specific utility shapes and timings
- Balance learning the agent's nuances with maintaining solid controller fundamentals for the team

## 3. Common Mistakes
- Trying to play Miks like another controller instead of learning his unique kit identity
- Ignoring controller fundamentals (smoke timing, choke denial) while experimenting with flashy mechanics
- Not adapting to patch changes that may alter cooldowns, durations, or ability interactions
- Over-investing in niche plays before mastering the baseline smoke and denial patterns
- Neglecting communication -- teammates may not know what Miks' utility looks like or how to play around it
- Fixating on highlight-reel potential instead of consistent round-by-round value

## 4. Pattern -> Meaning

**IF** the player consistently misses smoke timings during executes
**MEANING** They are focusing on Miks' unique mechanics instead of core controller duties
**FIX** Master the fundamentals first. Smokes for the team take priority over any agent-specific tech. Get the basics down, then add flair.

**IF** teammates frequently walk into or misplay around Miks' utility
**MEANING** Communication gap -- the team does not understand Miks' ability timing and placement
**FIX** Call out every ability before using it. Until teammates are familiar with Miks, over-communicate. "Utility going out in 3 seconds" is better than surprising your own team.

**IF** the player's playstyle changes dramatically between patches
**MEANING** They are chasing meta shifts rather than building consistent habits
**FIX** Build a core playstyle around controller fundamentals that works regardless of patch changes. Adjust details with patches, not the entire approach.

**IF** the player performs well in some rounds but is completely invisible in others
**MEANING** Inconsistency suggests they rely on specific setups rather than adaptable play
**FIX** Develop a default utility plan for every round state -- attacking A, attacking B, defending, retaking. Consistency beats occasional brilliance.

**IF** ability usage mirrors another controller's style (e.g., using utility exactly like Omen or Brimstone would)
**MEANING** The player has not explored Miks' unique kit advantages
**FIX** Spend time in custom games exploring what Miks can do that other controllers cannot. Find 2-3 unique plays per map that only Miks enables.

## 5. Map Interactions
- **General**: Miks' viability varies by map. Evaluate his smoke coverage and area denial range on each map before committing to him in ranked.
- **Open maps (Breeze, Icebox)**: Test whether his kit covers the wider sightlines. If not, a different controller may be more appropriate.
- **Tight maps (Bind, Split)**: Controller fundamentals translate well on smaller maps regardless of specific kit. Miks should perform solidly here.
- **Three-site maps (Haven, Lotus)**: Star/utility management across three sites requires discipline. Evaluate how Miks' ability count supports three-site coverage.
- **Note**: Map-specific meta for Miks will evolve. Revisit this section after each major patch.

## 6. Matchup Notes
- Against established controllers (Omen, Astra, Viper): Miks must find advantages his unique kit provides that these agents do not. If he plays the same game as them, experience favors the opponent.
- Against initiators: Standard controller challenges apply. Miks' smokes need to be timed to deny info utility just like any other controller.
- Paired with a second controller: Miks can fill a supplementary role while learning his kit. Pairing with a proven controller reduces risk in ranked.
- **Caution**: Until community meta stabilizes, avoid hard assumptions about Miks' matchup strengths. Test before committing.

## 7. What To Tell The Player

**When performing well**:
Pattern: You are delivering smokes within 2 seconds of execute calls, using Miks' unique area-control tools to deny at least one position that other controllers cannot reach, and communicating every ability deployment to your team.
Why it works: Fast smoke timing denies the defender reaction window — the 2-second threshold is the maximum before defenders can reposition to off-angles. Miks' unique denial tools cover geometry that standard smokes miss, which means defenders who have memorized how to play around Omen or Brimstone smokes encounter an unfamiliar denial shape they have not practiced against.
How to repeat: Step 1: Pre-select smoke targets during buy phase so execution is instant on the call. Step 2: After smokes land, deploy Miks' unique area-control ability at the position where defenders typically dodge standard smokes. Step 3: Call out every ability placement to teammates so they know which angles are denied. Between rounds, identify one setup per site that only Miks enables and rotate it into your plan.

**When struggling**: "Go back to basics. Forget the fancy Miks-specific tech for now. Smoke the right spots, deny the right chokes, and stay alive. The unique plays come after the fundamentals are solid."

**When being predictable**: "You are using the same utility sequence every round. Miks is new enough that opponents will study your patterns quickly. Mix up your utility order and timing."

## 8. Rank Modulation
- **Low (Iron-Silver)**: Play Miks as a straightforward smoker. Focus on placing smokes at the right time for your team. Ignore advanced kit mechanics until controller fundamentals are comfortable.
- **Mid (Gold-Platinum)**: Begin exploring Miks' unique abilities beyond smokes. Find 1-2 setups per map that leverage his kit. Keep fundamentals as the priority.
- **High (Diamond-Ascendant)**: Develop Miks-specific playbook with unique utility combos. Stay current with patch notes. Adapt playstyle to emerging community meta.
- **Elite (Immortal-Radiant)**: Pioneer new Miks strategies. At this level, being early to discover optimal plays on a new agent is an advantage. Balance innovation with consistency.

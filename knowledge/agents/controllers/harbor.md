# AGENT: Harbor

## 1. Role Identity
Harbor is the fluid controller who uses water-based barriers to create dynamic, moving cover for his team. Unlike static smokers, Harbor's utility flows across the battlefield -- his Cascade shield moves forward, his High Tide wall bends around corners, and his Cove provides a destructible bubble shield. Harbor excels at aggressive, push-oriented play and rewards teams that move together through his water utility. He is the controller for teams that want to take space aggressively rather than passively deny vision.

## 2. Core Responsibilities
- Lead aggressive site takes by casting Cascade forward as a moving shield for the team
- Deploy High Tide as a bending wall to cut off multiple angles simultaneously during executes
- Place Cove on spike for protected plants or to deny specific angles temporarily
- Use Reckoning to clear large areas on site takes or retakes, concussing enemies caught in the zone
- Create moving cover that the team pushes behind, unlike static smokes that the team pushes through
- Coordinate with the team on push timing -- Harbor's utility is useless if the team does not move with it

## 3. Common Mistakes
- Casting Cascade without the team ready to follow -- it moves forward regardless
- High Tide wall angles that bend into teammate faces instead of away from enemy positions
- Cove placed in the open where it gets destroyed instantly from multiple angles
- Reckoning used in tight spaces where enemies easily dodge the geyser pools
- Playing Harbor passively on defense like a traditional smoker -- his defensive kit is weaker than other controllers
- Not bending High Tide around corners, using it as a straight line when curves are the entire point

## 4. Pattern -> Meaning

**IF** Cascade is cast but teammates do not push with it
**MEANING** Either the player is not communicating the push or the team is not used to Harbor's moving-cover playstyle
**FIX** Call out "pushing with Cascade" before casting. Time it when the team is already committed to entering, not as a hopeful invitation.

**IF** High Tide is consistently placed as a straight wall
**MEANING** The player is not using the bending mechanic, losing Harbor's primary advantage over other walls
**FIX** Practice bending High Tide around corners and through doorways. The wall should curve to deny multiple angles that a straight line cannot cover.

**IF** Cove is destroyed within 1-2 seconds of placement every round
**MEANING** Cove is placed in crossfire positions where multiple enemies can shoot it simultaneously
**FIX** Place Cove where only 1-2 angles can shoot it, or use it when enemies are blinded/smoked. Behind cover or in corners extends its lifespan.

**IF** Reckoning gets zero concusses consistently
**MEANING** The area is too small or enemies have easy escape routes
**FIX** Reckoning on open sites and wide areas. Use it to flush enemies from hiding spots, not to damage them in the open.

**IF** the player consistently bottom-frags on defense rounds
**MEANING** Harbor's defensive toolkit is being used passively, which does not suit his kit
**FIX** On defense, Harbor should play aggressive off-angles and use Cascade to fall back. He is not a passive site anchor -- he needs to make plays.

## 5. Map Interactions
- **Pearl**: High Tide bending through B site halls is strong. Cascade on A main provides push cover. One of Harbor's better maps.
- **Lotus**: Three sites and rotating doors benefit from flowing water walls. Cascade through C main is effective.
- **Haven**: High Tide bending from A long through A site denies multiple angles. Reckoning on C site is strong due to wide area.
- **Split**: Cascade up mid or A main creates push opportunities. Tight corridors limit High Tide bending value.
- **Breeze**: Open sites make Reckoning effective. High Tide across A site or B halls covers wide spaces.

## 6. Matchup Notes
- Against Viper: Harbor's moving utility challenges Viper's static setup. Push through Viper walls with Cascade as cover.
- Against KAY/O: Suppress disables Harbor's utility mid-cast. Play outside suppress radius when possible.
- Paired with a duelist entry: Cascade in front of the duelist creates a moving shield for their entry. High synergy with Jett and Raze.
- Against Sova: Recon Bolt scans through Cascade. Harbor does not deny info as well as smoke controllers.

## 7. What To Tell The Player

**When performing well**:
Pattern: You are bending High Tide around corners to deny 3+ defender angles simultaneously, casting Cascade only when your team is within trade distance, and placing Cove on spike for protected plants.
Why it works: A curved High Tide covers angles that a straight wall would miss — the bend denies both the close hold and the deep-site angle in a single cast, forcing defenders to reposition or fight blind. Cascade timed with team proximity means they push through the moving shield together, arriving on site as a group rather than staggered. Cove on spike gives the planter a destructible bubble that buys 2-3 seconds of immunity from crossfire.
How to repeat: Step 1: Communicate "pushing with Cascade" and confirm teammates are within 5 meters before casting. Step 2: Deploy High Tide with a deliberate bend through the site's corner to cut off both the close and deep hold positions. Step 3: Once on site, drop Cove on the plant spot before the spike goes down. Use Reckoning on wide-area sites where enemies have multiple hiding spots to flush them into the open.

**When struggling**: "Your Cascade is going out without the team behind it. Communicate the push. If the team is not following, hold Cascade until they are ready to commit."

**When being predictable**: "Same High Tide angle every round means defenders are playing behind it instead of being denied by it. Vary the wall bend or change the site you wall off."

## 8. Rank Modulation
- **Low (Iron-Silver)**: Use Cascade as simple forward cover. Place High Tide straight across choke points. Focus on staying with the team.
- **Mid (Gold-Platinum)**: Introduce High Tide bending around corners. Time Cascade with team pushes. Use Cove for safe spike plants.
- **High (Diamond-Ascendant)**: Advanced wall bends that deny 3+ angles. Cove used to block specific sightlines, not just spike plant. Reckoning for site takes.
- **Elite (Immortal-Radiant)**: Dynamic High Tide adjustments based on enemy positions. Cascade used to bait utility. Reckoning timing synchronized with team execute.

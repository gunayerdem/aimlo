# MATCHUP: Tejo vs Default Defense

> **PATCH-SENSITIVE NOTE**: Tejo is a newer agent. Ability values, cooldowns, and interactions may change significantly between patches. Always verify current patch notes before applying coaching advice.

## Interaction Identity
Tejo is an initiator built to crack structured defenses. His kit provides tools to break open default defensive setups — the standard positions, crossfires, and utility placements that defenders rely on every round. Default defense means predictable positioning: one player A, one player B, one mid, two flex. Tejo's abilities are designed to punish this predictability by forcing defenders out of their standard spots before the execute begins. This matchup asks: can Tejo disrupt enough of the default to create an exploitable gap, or can the defense adapt their positioning to survive his disruption?

## Common Punish Patterns
**Tejo punishing default defense**: Long-range disruption abilities that reach standard defensive positions from safety. Area denial that forces defenders off their angles before the push even starts. Information-gathering tools that confirm whether the default defense is holding standard positions. Sequenced disruption — first forcing one defender out, then another, creating a cascade that breaks the entire structure.

**Default defense punishing Tejo**: Off-default positioning that Tejo's abilities do not reach. Early aggression into Tejo during his ability deployment animation. Adapting positioning round-to-round so Tejo's pre-planned disruption hits empty spots. Using the time Tejo spends deploying abilities to rotate or set up crossfires in unexpected positions.

## What Repeated Failure Means
If **default defense** keeps getting disrupted by Tejo, defenders are holding the exact positions his abilities target. They are playing textbook defense against an agent designed to punish the textbook. They need to vary their positions round-to-round.

If **Tejo** keeps failing to disrupt, the defense has moved off-default and his abilities are landing on empty positions. He needs better pre-round reads or real-time information to target where defenders actually are.

## What AIMLO Should Say
### When player is on the punished side
**Default defense disrupted by Tejo**: "Your standard positions are exactly where his abilities land. Mix up your defense — hold off-angles, rotate your stack, play unconventional spots. If your default is the same every round, Tejo will break it every round."

**Tejo failing to disrupt**: "Your abilities are hitting empty spots because the defense moved off-default. Use your information tools first to confirm positions, then disrupt. Do not waste disruption on spots you hope are occupied."

### When player is on the punishing side
**Tejo cracking the defense**: "Your disruption is forcing them off their angles before the push. Keep sequencing your abilities to break one position at a time and coordinate with your team to push the gap."

**Default defense adapting**: "You are reading his disruption pattern and positioning around it. Keep varying your spots and he will waste his entire kit on empty angles."

## Rank Modulation
**LOW**: Defenses are random, not default. Tejo has no structured targets. Coach Tejo to recognize standard defensive positions and target them.

**MID**: Defaults are consistent and Tejo's disruption is effective but uncoordinated with the team push. Coach team timing — disrupt, then push immediately while defenders are displaced.

**HIGH**: Defenses start varying off-default against Tejo. Coach information-first disruption: scan, confirm, then displace.

**ELITE**: The matchup is a round-to-round adaptation game. Tejo reads the defense's adaptation pattern and pre-disrupts their new positions. The defense reads Tejo's disruption pattern and positions outside it. Coach meta-level reads.

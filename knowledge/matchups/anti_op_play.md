# MATCHUP: Anti-Operator Play

## Interaction Identity
The Operator (OP) is the most polarizing weapon in Valorant — it kills in one shot at any range, but costs 4700 credits and is weak at close range. Anti-OP play is not a single matchup but a team-wide discipline. It asks: can your team systematically deny long sightlines, force close engagements, and punish the OP player's positioning? Teams that cannot answer the OP lose rounds where the opponent invests in one. Teams that can answer it turn the OP into a 4700-credit liability.

## Common Punish Patterns
**OP player punishing the team**: Holds long angles where duelists must wide-peek to engage. Repositions after each kill using teleport (Chamber) or dash (Jett). Plays off teammate utility that blocks counter-aggression. Forces the enemy team to spend 2-3 abilities just to take one fight. On defense, the OP controls an entire lane alone, freeing teammates to stack elsewhere.

**Team punishing OP player**: Coordinated double-peeks force the OP to choose a target, guaranteeing a trade. Smoke the OP sightline and push a different angle. Flashes force the OP player to look away, creating a timing window. Utility that forces repositioning (Raze grenade, Breach fault line) denies the static positioning the OP requires. Fast site executes overwhelm the OP player before they can set up.

## What Repeated Failure Means
If a team keeps dying to the same OP player round after round, they are peeking long sightlines individually, not using smokes to deny the angle, and not coordinating trades. This is a teamplay and discipline failure, not an aim gap.

If the OP player keeps dying despite good positioning, the enemy team is executing strong anti-OP protocols — coordinated utility, double-peeks, and fast timing. The OP player is holding angles too long instead of repositioning.

## What AIMLO Should Say
### When player is on the punished side
"You are feeding the OP by taking isolated duels at range. Smoke the angle, flash the position, or double-peek so your teammate trades. Stop giving them free one-for-nothing kills. Every time you wide-peek an OP alone, you are choosing to lose that fight."

### When player is on the punishing side
"Your OP is controlling the round. Keep varying your positioning so they cannot pre-flash you. The moment they start coordinating utility against your angle, you need to reposition before they execute — do not hold the same spot after they have smoked it twice."

## Rank Modulation
**LOW**: Players do not buy Operators often, and when they do, they miss or get overwhelmed. Anti-OP play is irrelevant at this level.

**MID**: OP players start getting multi-kills but are beatable with basic smokes. Teach teams to always smoke the OP angle and never dry-peek it alone.

**HIGH**: The OP is a structured part of the team's economy and strategy. Anti-OP requires coordinated utility chains — smoke plus flash plus double-peek. Coach the full protocol.

**ELITE**: OP duels are about timing, positioning, and counter-utility at the highest precision. The anti-OP team must be unpredictable in their approach — same smoke-flash combo every round becomes readable.

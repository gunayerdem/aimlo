# MATCHUP: Veto vs Entry Paths

> **PATCH-SENSITIVE NOTE**: Veto is a newer sentinel agent. Ability values, cooldowns, and interactions may change significantly between patches. Always verify current patch notes before applying coaching advice.

## Interaction Identity
Veto's information-denial kit creates a unique defensive dynamic on entry paths. Standard entry play relies on gathering information (droning, scanning, flashing) before committing. Veto disrupts this sequence by jamming the recon phase, forcing entry fraggers to push blind or waste time re-gathering intel. This matchup is not about Veto killing the entry — it is about making the entry's preparation unreliable so that when they commit, they are working with bad or no information.

## Common Punish Patterns
**Veto punishing entry paths**: Interference activated when the entry team drones or scans, returning false or no information. Movement masking that hides how many defenders are on site — the entry does not know if they face one or three. Recon disruption timed right before the execute, so the entry team's last piece of information is unreliable. Forcing the entry to push with their most recent scan data being from 30+ seconds ago.

**Entry paths punishing Veto**: Pushing without relying on recon at all — flashes and raw aggression bypass Veto's denial because they were never going to drone anyway. Multiple simultaneous info sources that Veto cannot all jam. Fast entries that commit before Veto can activate denial. Using the denied information as information itself — "if Veto jammed our drone, he is on this site."

## What Repeated Failure Means
If **entries** keep failing against Veto, they are too dependent on pre-push information. They drone, Veto jams it, they push anyway with bad intel, and they die to defenders they did not expect. They need to either push without relying on the jammed intel or recognize the jam and reset.

If **Veto** keeps failing to deny entries, the enemy team is not using recon pre-push or is pushing fast enough that denial does not matter. Veto's value drops sharply against raw aggression that never intended to gather info.

## What AIMLO Should Say
### When player is on the punished side
**Entry failing vs Veto**: "Your drone got jammed and you pushed anyway with bad info. When your recon is disrupted, that IS the information — Veto is on that site. Either reset to the other site or push with full flash support instead of relying on the scan."

**Veto failing to deny entry**: "They are not droning before they push, so your denial has nothing to jam. Against pure aggression, play as a standard sentinel — position for the gunfight, use movement masking to hide your team's numbers, and play off teammate utility."

### When player is on the punishing side
**Veto denying entries**: "They are pushing blind because your interference wrecked their recon. Keep timing your denial for the moment they scan and they will never have reliable info on your site."

**Entry beating Veto**: "You are bypassing his denial by pushing with aggression instead of information. Keep this up — Veto cannot jam a flash-and-peek, only a drone."

## Rank Modulation
**LOW**: Entries do not drone before pushing, so Veto's denial is pointless. Coach entries to use recon first (which creates the dynamic for Veto to deny).

**MID**: Entries drone but push regardless of jammed info. Veto's denial works but the entry team does not recognize it. Coach entries to recognize denial and adapt their approach.

**HIGH**: Both sides understand the dynamic. Coach the timing game — when Veto activates denial, when the entry adjusts their info-gathering method, and when to push with flashes instead of drones.

**ELITE**: The matchup becomes a meta-game where the entry team varies between recon-dependent and recon-independent pushes. Veto must read which approach is coming. Coach reads and adaptive denial.

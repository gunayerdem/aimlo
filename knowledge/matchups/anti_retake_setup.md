# MATCHUP: Anti-Retake Setup

## Interaction Identity
Some defensive compositions do not try to stop the initial site take — they concede it and retake with coordinated utility. Anti-retake play means the attacking team recognizes this strategy and builds their post-plant to punish the retake attempt. This matchup is about whether the attackers can make the bomb site unretakable or whether the defenders' coordinated retake utility overwhelms the post-plant hold. It rewards patience and positioning over raw mechanical skill.

## Common Punish Patterns
**Retake comp punishing post-plant**: Coordinated flashes and smokes isolate post-plant holders. Multiple initiator scans reveal every attacker's position simultaneously. Mollies and area-denial flush attackers off advantageous post-plant angles. Breach stun through walls clears entrenched positions. The retake team pushes as a coordinated unit, trading kills efficiently.

**Anti-retake setup punishing defenders**: Killjoy nanoswarms on defuse spot deny the defuse for 4+ seconds each. Crossfire positioning where post-plant holders watch each other's angles. Viper pit or controller smoke on the spike forces retakers to fight inside a disadvantaged zone. Sentinel traps placed during post-plant catch retaking defenders from behind. Off-site lurkers threaten the retake rotation path.

## What Repeated Failure Means
If the **retake** keeps failing, the team is retaking without sufficient utility or retaking one-by-one instead of as a unit. Retake requires everyone to push simultaneously with utility — not a staggered trickle.

If the **post-plant** keeps falling apart, attackers are playing in predictable positions that retake utility easily clears. They are not spreading across multiple angles or are not using denial utility on the spike itself.

## What AIMLO Should Say
### When player is on the punished side
**Retake team failing**: "You are retaking without a plan. Every retake needs smokes, flashes, and scans deployed together. Push together — one-by-one retakes against set up crossfires are suicidal."

**Post-plant team getting retaken**: "You are too predictable after plant. Spread out, put denial utility on the spike, and hold crossfires. If all three post-plant players are in the same area, one flash clears all of you."

### When player is on the punishing side
**Post-plant holding strong**: "Your post-plant setup is winning rounds on its own. Keep placing denial on the spike and spreading your angles so retake utility cannot clear all of you at once."

**Retake comp succeeding**: "Your coordinated retakes are working because you are pushing together with utility. Keep scanning before you commit and trading any teammate who goes down."

## Rank Modulation
**LOW**: Retakes are uncoordinated — players push individually. Post-plant players sit on the spike. Neither side understands the dynamic. Coach basic post-plant spreading and group retake timing.

**MID**: Retakes start using some utility but lack coordination. Post-plant is better but denial utility is inconsistent. Coach synchronized retakes and consistent denial placement.

**HIGH**: Both sides understand the structure. The matchup is about utility economy — who has more tools for the post-plant phase. Coach resource management through the round.

**ELITE**: Post-plant and retake are both optimized. The edge goes to superior reads on enemy positioning and ability timing. Coach micro-timing and fake defuse discipline.
